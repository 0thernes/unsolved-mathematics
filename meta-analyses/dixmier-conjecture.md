# Meta-Analysis: The Dixmier Conjecture

> **AI-generated meta-analysis — not peer-reviewed.** Curated and accredited by **Alexander Donahue** (human-in-the-loop). A survey/assessment of an open problem; it makes no claim of a new result. Citations carry verification flags and require human source-checking.

## Abstract

The Dixmier conjecture asserts that every $k$-algebra endomorphism of the first Weyl algebra $A_1 = k\langle p,q\rangle/([p,q]-1)$, over a field $k$ of characteristic zero, is an automorphism. Posed by Jacques Dixmier in 1968 as "Problème 1" of his structural study of Weyl algebras, it survives as one of the cleaner-sounding open questions in noncommutative algebra. Because $A_1$ is a simple Noetherian domain, every nonzero endomorphism is injective; the conjecture is entirely about surjectivity. The decisive modern development is the 2005–2007 theorem of Tsuchimoto and, independently, Belov-Kanel–Kontsevich that the stable Dixmier conjecture is equivalent to the stable Jacobian conjecture, with $\mathrm{DC}_n$ corresponding to $\mathrm{JC}_{2n}$. This located Dixmier's question inside a famously stubborn problem in affine algebraic geometry, unresolved even in two variables. This meta-analysis surveys the statement, the state of the art, the principal approaches and their barriers, and what a resolution would require. It is an honest assessment of an open problem and claims no new result. The status remains **open** in every dimension, with strong special-case evidence and no counterexample.

## 1. Statement and significance

Let $A_1 = A_1(k)$ be the first Weyl algebra over a field $k$ of characteristic zero: the associative algebra generated by $p,q$ subject to $[p,q]=pq-qp=1$. Over $k=\mathbb{C}$ this is the algebra of polynomial differential operators on the line, with $q=x$ and $p=\partial=d/dx$. Dixmier's 1968 paper *Sur les algèbres de Weyl* proved $A_1$ to be a simple Noetherian domain with trivial center, then closed with six problems; the first asks whether every algebra endomorphism of $A_1$ is an automorphism.

The significance is twofold. Internally, the conjecture is a statement of maximal rigidity: $A_1$ should be co-Hopfian, admitting no proper self-embedding preserving the commutation relation. Equivalently, any $P,Q \in A_1$ with $[P,Q]=1$ must generate all of $A_1$. Externally — and this is what elevated the problem — the 2005–2007 equivalence with the Jacobian conjecture binds noncommutative rigidity to the invertibility of polynomial maps with constant nonzero Jacobian, a question tracing to Keller (1939).

## 2. State of the art

The status is **open** in every dimension $n \ge 1$, including the original $A_1$, with neither an accepted proof nor a counterexample. Several facts are unconditional. Simplicity (Dixmier, 1968) reduces the problem to surjectivity. The conjecture holds for endomorphisms compatible with the Bernstein filtration and for those with bounded-degree images — the "tame" regime. The automorphism group $\mathrm{Aut}(A_1)$ is fully understood, an analogue of the Jung–van der Kulk theorem for $\mathrm{Aut}(\mathbb{A}^2)$.

The central conditional result is the **stable equivalence**: $\mathrm{DC}_n$ is equivalent to $\mathrm{JC}_{2n}$ in a precise stable sense, proved by Tsuchimoto (announced 2005; full version in *Osaka J. Math.*, 2008) and independently by Belov-Kanel and Kontsevich (*Moscow Mathematical Journal*, 2007). Consequently a proof of the Jacobian conjecture in dimension $2n$ would prove $\mathrm{DC}_n$, and conversely. The Dixmier conjecture is therefore now regarded as exactly as hard as the Jacobian conjecture, which is itself open even for $\mathbb{A}^2$.

## 3. Principal approaches and barriers

**Reduction modulo $p$ and the Jacobian equivalence.** Over characteristic $p$, the elements $p^p, q^p$ become central, so $A_1(\mathbb{F}_p)$ is an Azumaya algebra over its center and an endomorphism induces a polynomial map of $\mathbb{A}^2$. Tracking this across all primes converts a hypothetical non-surjective Dixmier endomorphism into a non-invertible polynomial self-map with invertible Jacobian. This is the dominant strategy and the source of the equivalence theorem. Its barrier is intrinsic: it relocates the difficulty rather than removing it, and every approach now inherits the obstructions to the Jacobian conjecture.

**Symplectic and automorphism-group methods.** Since $\mathrm{Aut}(A_1)$ is tame and understood, one tries to leverage it. Belov-Kanel and Kontsevich conjectured a canonical isomorphism $\mathrm{Aut}(A_n)\cong\mathrm{Aut}(P_n)$ with the polynomial symplectomorphism group, via deformation quantization and a tropical $\hbar\to 0$ limit. The barrier: an isomorphism of *automorphism* groups does not control *non-surjective* endomorphisms, which are precisely the objects to be excluded.

**Filtration and degree bounds.** Using the Bernstein filtration and holonomicity (Bernstein inequality), one confirms the conjecture for filtered or small-degree endomorphisms. The barrier is the absence of any a-priori degree bound on a hypothetical counterexample — mirroring exactly the missing degree control in the Jacobian conjecture.

**Holonomic / D-module and tropical-limit programs.** A self-embedding pulls back holonomic modules to holonomic modules; analyzing this functor underlies Tsuchimoto's machinery, but controlling all holonomic modules of the subalgebra $\varphi(A_1)$ is as hard as the original problem. The tropical / deformation-quantization degeneration of the Belov-Kanel–Kontsevich–Elishev–Yu school remains a program rather than a theorem. Every serious route funnels into the same hard core.

## 4. Critical assessment

The dossier's central claim — that the Dixmier conjecture is, up to the stable equivalence, the Jacobian conjecture in noncommutative disguise — is the correct and load-bearing framing, and it is well supported by the literature it cites. The positive evidence is genuine but should be read with care: it is entirely *special-case* evidence (filtered, graded, bounded-degree, structure-preserving endomorphisms) plus the absence of any counterexample. None of this touches the non-tame, unbounded-degree regime where the actual difficulty lives, and the equivalence makes precise why: that regime encodes the Jacobian conjecture's missing degree bounds.

A point of honest caution concerns the equivalence itself. The "stable" qualifier matters. The clean slogan $\mathrm{DC}_n \Leftrightarrow \mathrm{JC}_{2n}$ holds in a stable sense across the families of conjectures; the precise statement for a single fixed $n$ (and the exact direction and uniformity of implications) is more delicate than a one-line equivalence suggests, and a human reader should consult the primary papers rather than rely on the slogan. The dossier is appropriately neutral on claimed direct proofs of $\mathrm{DC}_1$ and on claimed proofs of the two-dimensional Jacobian conjecture, treating all as not accepted; this is the right posture given the long record of erroneous announcements in this area.

## 5. What a resolution would require / open directions

A complete proof must control non-filtered, unbounded-degree endomorphisms with no a-priori degree hypothesis — equivalently, show that any $P,Q$ with $[P,Q]=1$ generate $A_1$. A counterexample would, by the equivalence, yield a counterexample to the Jacobian conjecture in some dimension, widely believed not to exist. Plausible routes: (1) prove the Jacobian conjecture in some dimension $2n$ by commutative degree-reduction methods, whence $\mathrm{DC}_n$ follows; (2) complete the symplectomorphism / tropical-limit program ($\mathrm{Aut}(A_n)\cong\mathrm{Aut}(P_n)$) and extend control from automorphisms to endomorphisms via augmentation topology and lifting; (3) resolve the Mathieu–Zhao subspace / image-conjecture reformulation of invertibility; (4) extract an effective degree bound directly on the Weyl-algebra side via $p$-curvature refinements. None currently yields an unconditional proof.
