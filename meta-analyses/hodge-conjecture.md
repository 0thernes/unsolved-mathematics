# Meta-Analysis: The Hodge Conjecture

> **AI-generated meta-analysis — not peer-reviewed.** Curated and accredited by **Alexander Donahue** (human-in-the-loop). A survey/assessment of an open problem; it makes no claim of a new result. Citations carry verification flags and require human source-checking.

## Abstract

The Hodge conjecture asserts that on a non-singular complex projective variety $X$, every rational Hodge class — an element of $H^{2p}(X,\mathbb{Q})\cap H^{p,p}(X)$ — is a $\mathbb{Q}$-linear combination of cohomology classes of algebraic cycles. Posed by W. V. D. Hodge in his 1950 ICM address and named a Clay Millennium Prize Problem in 2000, it remains **open** in every codimension $p\ge 2$. This meta-analysis synthesizes the problem's dossier: the single unconditional case (the Lefschetz $(1,1)$-theorem, $p=1$); the negative results that fixed the modern statement (the failure of the integral version, Atiyah–Hirzebruch 1962, and of the Kähler extension, Voisin 2002); and the principal conditional programs — absolute Hodge cycles on abelian varieties, Mumford–Tate / motivated-cycle machinery, normal-function singularities, and the algebraicity of Hodge loci (Cattani–Deligne–Kaplan 1995; Bakker–Klingler–Tsimerman 2020). The recurring barrier is structural: cohomology supplies no general engine for constructing high-codimension subvarieties. We assess what is solid versus speculative, and what a resolution would demand. The survey makes no claim of progress beyond the published literature.

## 1. Statement and significance

On a compact Kähler manifold the Hodge decomposition splits complex cohomology as $H^{n}(X,\mathbb{C})=\bigoplus_{p+q=n}H^{p,q}(X)$ with $\overline{H^{p,q}}=H^{q,p}$. An algebraic subvariety of codimension $p$ defines a fundamental class in $H^{2p}(X,\mathbb{Q})$ landing in $H^{p,p}$. The **Hodge conjecture** is the converse: on a smooth projective $X$, every rational class in $H^{p,p}$ comes from algebraic cycles. It is one of the seven Millennium Prize Problems, with a difficulty rating of 97 and centrality 90 in this Atlas — a problem whose resolution would tie the transcendental (harmonic-analytic) and algebraic descriptions of a variety, and would clarify the still-conjectural theory of motives and the relationship of Chow groups to cohomology. Its codimension-1 case, the Lefschetz $(1,1)$-theorem, is the template: it identifies $H^{1,1}\cap H^2(X,\mathbb{Z})$ with divisor classes via the exponential sequence and the Picard group.

## 2. State of the art

**Unconditional results.** Only codimension 1 is fully proved (Lefschetz, 1924). Beyond it, the strongest unconditional evidence is the **algebraicity of Hodge loci**: Cattani–Deligne–Kaplan (1995) showed the locus on which a given Hodge class remains Hodge is a countable union of algebraic subvarieties — precisely what the conjecture predicts — reproved and extended via o-minimality / tame geometry by Bakker–Klingler–Tsimerman (2020). For **abelian varieties**, Deligne (1982) proved every Hodge class is *absolutely Hodge*; the conjecture itself is known for many abelian varieties (those whose Hodge ring is generated by divisors, and numerous Mumford–Tate configurations) but **not all** — Weil-type classes remain open. Various **special families** are settled by exhibiting cycles directly: $K3$ and abelian surfaces, low-degree hypersurfaces and complete intersections, certain Fermat varieties, and explicit Calabi–Yau threefolds (work of Shioda, Schoen, and others).

**Hard ceilings.** Two negative results pin down the live statement. The **integral** Hodge conjecture is false (Atiyah–Hirzebruch 1962, via topological $K$-theory and Steenrod operations; refined by Kollár and Totaro), forcing rational coefficients. The **Kähler** (non-projective) extension is false (Voisin 2002), tying the conjecture to projectivity. The general conjecture about lower filtration levels, false "for trivial reasons" (Grothendieck 1969), survives only in its amended coniveau form.

**Conditional results.** The Lefschetz **standard conjecture** would deliver algebraicity of the Künneth and Lefschetz operators; André's **motivated cycles** realize many Hodge classes with cycle-like properties; the **normal-function** program reduces the conjecture to producing singularities of admissible normal functions. None closes the gap to an actual cycle.

## 3. Principal approaches and barriers

- **Induction by codimension / standard conjectures.** Hard Lefschetz is a theorem, but its algebraic inverse (the Lefschetz standard conjecture) is open; products and intersections of divisors generate only a small subring in high codimension. *Barrier:* no general mechanism produces codimension-$\ge 2$ cycles.
- **Absolute Hodge cycles (abelian varieties).** Deligne's theorem is structurally decisive but does not output a cycle. *Barrier:* Weil and Mumford–Tate "exotic" classes not built from divisors.
- **Mumford–Tate groups / motivated cycles (André).** Recasts algebraicity as a question about invariants. *Barrier:* "motivated" is strictly weaker than "algebraic."
- **Normal functions and Griffiths' infinitesimal method.** The conjecture is *equivalent* to a singularity statement for admissible normal functions after spreading out; the supporting algebraicity (zero loci, via Brosnan–Pearlstein on Cattani–Deligne–Kaplan) is proved. *Barrier:* the singularities — i.e., the cycles — have not been produced in general.
- **Algebraicity of Hodge loci.** Confirmed unconditionally. *Barrier:* an algebraic *locus* is not an algebraic *cycle*.
- **Characteristic $p$ / Tate conjecture.** The arithmetic analogue; progress (Faltings, Tate, Zarhin, $K3$ cases) informs but does not imply Hodge. *Barrier:* the two are independent in general.

The unifying obstruction is not logical (there is no analogue of the parity barrier or relativization) but the **structural scarcity of cycle constructions**.

## 4. Critical assessment

What is **solid**: the dossier's central claim — that exactly one codimension is proved, and that the rest is a landscape of conditional reductions and confirmed predictions — is accurate and well-supported by canonical literature. The negative results (Atiyah–Hirzebruch, Voisin, Grothendieck) are correctly characterized as having *fixed the statement* rather than threatening the open case. The framing of Cattani–Deligne–Kaplan and its o-minimal reproof as "strong evidence that produces no cycle" is exactly the right epistemic register.

What is **speculative or at risk of overstatement**: the phrase that the Hodge conjecture is "equivalent" to a normal-function singularity statement should be read carefully — such equivalences hold in specific formulations (Green–Griffiths; de Cataldo–Migliorini variants) and the dossier itself notes the cycles "have not been produced." A casual reader could mistake the chain of conditional reductions for a near-complete proof; the dossier is, to its credit, explicit that no community-credited near-miss exists. The "many but not all abelian varieties" claim is correct but compresses a technical boundary (Hodge-ring-generated-by-divisors vs. Weil classes) that a specialist should state precisely.

How far is the frontier, honestly: very far. Unlike problems with a single identified obstacle, here the difficulty is the *absence* of a construction engine. The field's own strategy is largely indirect — accumulating structural facts the conjecture predicts. That is real progress on the surrounding theory, not measurable progress toward the cycle-existence statement itself.

## 5. What a resolution would require / open directions

A proof must supply, for an arbitrary Hodge class on an arbitrary smooth projective $X$, an actual $\mathbb{Q}$-combination of subvarieties with that class. A disproof must exhibit a *rational* Hodge class on a *projective* variety provably non-algebraic — and every known counterexample escapes via non-projectivity or integrality. Plausible routes flagged in the dossier: (1) tame geometry / o-minimality pushed from algebraicity of Hodge loci toward effective cycle construction; (2) settling the standard conjectures or the Tate conjecture in enough generality to transfer; (3) proving the predicted normal-function singularities exist; (4) new motivic or derived-category constructions yielding high-codimension cycles. None is presently close.
