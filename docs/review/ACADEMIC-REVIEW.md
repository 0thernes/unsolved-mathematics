# 🎓 Request Academic Review

The Unsolved Mathematics Atlas actively **welcomes review and verification by
academic mathematicians**. AI review is only the first gate; human expertise is
what gives this work scholarly standing. This page explains how to review the
atlas, and how the author requests review responsibly.

## For academics: how to review or verify

You are invited to:

1. **Verify citations.** Pick a problem's `papers.md`; confirm that flagged
   references exist and say what the dossier claims; open a PR or
   [verification issue](../../.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/verification.yml) promoting
   flags to `verified` (or correcting them). See
   [SOURCING.md](../methodology/SOURCING.md).
2. **Referee a meta-analysis.** Read a `meta-analyses/<slug>.md` write-up and file
   an [academic review request/response](../../.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/academic-review-request.yml)
   with your assessment — accept, revise, or reject, with reasons.
3. **Correct the mathematics.** Flag any overstatement, error, or missing context.
   The integrity policy treats a fabricated or overstated claim as the one
   unforgivable defect; your corrections are the highest-value contribution.

Reviewers who consent are credited in [AUTHORS.md](../../AUTHORS.md).

## What we are (and are not) asking

- ✅ We ask experts to **check, correct, and referee** AI-assisted surveys.
- ❌ We do **not** ask anyone to endorse unverified claims, and we do **not**
  present this work as already peer-reviewed.

## Responsible outreach (the author's protocol)

Mathematics has strong norms against spam and against pressuring busy researchers.
The author follows this protocol — and contributors are asked to as well:

- **Never mass-email professors** unsolicited. Approach individuals only with a
  specific, relevant, concise request, and accept silence as an answer.
- **Prefer open venues** where reviewing AI-assisted math work is on-topic
  (below) over cold-emailing.
- **Be upfront** that the material is AI-generated and not peer-reviewed, every time.
- **Respect endorsement rules** (e.g. arXiv requires a genuine endorser; do not
  seek endorsements under false pretenses).

## Venues & channels for human review

Curated, legitimate places to seek review of AI-assisted mathematics surveys.
This is a routing list; appropriateness varies by problem and by community norms.

### Open scholarly communities
- **MathOverflow** — research-level questions; good for targeted "is this
  characterization of the state of the art correct?" questions (not for posting
  whole AI papers).
- **arXiv** (math.* / math.HO) — preprints; requires an endorser and adherence to
  moderation policy. Clearly mark AI assistance.
- **The n-Category Café**, **Terence Tao's blog comments**, topic-specific
  research blogs — for narrow expert feedback where on-topic.
- **Zulip / Lean community, mathlib** — for anything amenable to formalization;
  a formal proof check is the gold standard of verification.

### Institutions & programs (for formal collaboration, by invitation/relationship)
- University mathematics departments' open seminars and "open problems" groups.
- Problem-focused institutes: **Clay Mathematics Institute** (Millennium
  Problems), **AIM (American Institute of Mathematics)**, **Banff (BIRS)**,
  **Oberwolfach (MFO)**, **Simons / Flatiron**, **IHÉS**, **Newton Institute**.
- AI-for-mathematics efforts: **DeepMind / AlphaProof-style groups**, **Lean
  FRO**, **formal-methods labs** — natural homes for AI-assisted math work.

### Journals (only after human verification)
- Survey/exposition venues such as the **Bulletin of the AMS**, **Notices of the
  AMS**, **Expositiones Mathematicae**, or **L'Enseignement Mathématique** are the
  appropriate targets **for a human-verified survey** — not for raw AI output.

> Track every outreach attempt and its outcome in [SUBMISSIONS.md](../../SUBMISSIONS.md).

## A note on credit and integrity

The goal is legitimate accreditation through real review — not the appearance of
it. Credit earned by passing genuine human scrutiny is worth something; credit
claimed by dressing up unverified AI text is worth nothing and damages trust. This
atlas is built to earn the former.
