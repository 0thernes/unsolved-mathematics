# Top Mathematicians — The Continuum Hypothesis

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Georg Cantor | 1845–1918 | originator | Founded set theory; stated CH (1878); proved it for closed sets (Cantor–Bendixson). |
| 2 | David Hilbert | 1862–1943 | expositor | Made CH Problem 1 of his 1900 list; attempted a (flawed) proof in 1926, shaping the agenda. |
| 3 | Kurt Gödel | 1906–1978 | breakthrough | Built $L$; proved Con(ZFC)$\Rightarrow$Con(ZFC+GCH) (1938–40); argued for new axioms (1947). |
| 4 | Paul J. Cohen | 1934–2007 | breakthrough | Invented forcing; proved Con(ZFC)$\Rightarrow$Con(ZFC+$\neg$CH) (1963). Fields Medal 1966. |
| 5 | Robert M. Solovay | b. 1938 | breakthrough | Iterated forcing, random reals, Solovay model; Martin–Solovay axiom; foundations of modern technique. |
| 6 | Saharon Shelah | b. 1945 | frontier | Proper forcing; PCF theory bounding cardinal exponentiation; vast structural results on the continuum. |
| 7 | Donald A. Martin | b. 1940 | breakthrough | Martin's Axiom; determinacy results (Borel determinacy; Martin–Steel projective determinacy). |
| 8 | Matthew Foreman | b. 1957 | breakthrough | Co-author of Martin's Maximum (with Magidor and Shelah), the strongest forcing axiom forcing $\mathfrak{c}=\aleph_2$. |
| 9 | W. Hugh Woodin | b. 1955 | frontier | $\Omega$-logic and the $\neg$CH case; later the Ultimate-$L$ program toward CH; central living figure. |
| 10 | David Asperó & Ralf Schindler | contemporary | frontier | Proved $\mathrm{MM}^{++}\Rightarrow(*)$ (2021), unifying the principal $\neg$CH axioms. |

## Notes

The active community sits within set theory and mathematical logic, concentrated in forcing, large cardinals, inner-model theory, and descriptive set theory. The intellectual center of gravity is the rivalry between two programs reaching opposite verdicts on CH: the forcing-axiom / $(*)$ line (Foreman, Magidor, Shelah, Asperó, Schindler, Viale, and others) pointing to $\mathfrak{c}=\aleph_2$, and Woodin's Ultimate-$L$ / inner-model line pointing to CH. Menachem Magidor, Ralf Schindler, Joel David Hamkins (multiverse view), Peter Koellner (philosophical synthesis), Justin Moore, Ilijas Farah, Sy-David Friedman, and Stevo Todorcevic are prominent contemporary contributors. Malliaris and Shelah's 2012 resolution of $\mathfrak{p}=\mathfrak{t}$ (a half-century-old cardinal-characteristics problem) illustrates the field's continuing vitality on the combinatorics surrounding the continuum. Activity centers on Jerusalem, Berkeley/Harvard, Vienna (Kurt Gödel Research Center), Münster, and Toronto, with the European Set Theory Society and regular Oberwolfach and BIRS workshops as gathering points.
