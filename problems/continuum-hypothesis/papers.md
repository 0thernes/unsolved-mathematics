Notes on identifiers and verification: rows 1–13 are canonical founding and breakthrough works whose existence and authorship are certain; DOIs are given only for the PNAS notes (8, 11, 12) where I am confident of the exact identifier. Several monographs (9, 13, 17, 20, 21) are famous books without a single canonical DOI, so the identifier is left as "—" rather than risk an incorrect number. The DOIs marked "needs-verification" (14, 16, 18, 25) are plausible but should be confirmed against the publisher record before citation; the Foreman–Magidor–Shelah Martin's Maximum work (18) appeared as a two-part paper in the Annals of Mathematics in 1988, and the title/year there should be checked. Hamkins's "The set-theoretic multiverse" (2012, *Review of Symbolic Logic*) and Malliaris–Shelah's $\mathfrak{p}=\mathfrak{t}$ work (2016, *Journal of the AMS*) are also central to the modern frontier but were omitted from the 25 to keep the founding-to-frontier balance.
