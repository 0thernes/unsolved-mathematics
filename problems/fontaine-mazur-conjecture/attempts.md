# Attempts — The Fontaine–Mazur Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

The Fontaine–Mazur conjecture has not attracted spurious "elementary" proof claims in the way that famous Diophantine problems do — its technical prerequisites ($p$-adic Hodge theory, deformation theory, automorphic forms) screen out amateur attempts. Its history is instead one of genuine, incremental, peer-reviewed progress, with the principal "drama" being which hypotheses each breakthrough could remove.

## The Wiles / Taylor–Wiles foundation (1995)

Wiles' first announced proof of the semistable modularity theorem (1993) famously contained a gap in the Euler-system bound on a Selmer group. The repair, the **Taylor–Wiles patching argument** (1995), replaced the Euler system with a system of auxiliary primes. This is not a disputed claim but the canonical example of a near-miss made good; the patching method it introduced is the backbone of every subsequent attack on Fontaine–Mazur.

## Kisin's $\mathrm{GL}_2/\mathbb{Q}$ theorem (2007–2009)

Kisin's proof of most of the two-dimensional odd conjecture is regarded as correct and is the high-water mark for the conjecture over $\mathbb{Q}$. The honest caveat, stated by Kisin himself, is that the theorem carries hypotheses: it requires $\bar\rho|_{G_{\mathbb{Q}_p}}$ to avoid certain degenerate local shapes, the representation to be Hodge–Tate regular, and excludes some small-image and weight-one residual situations. These residual cases — not errors, but genuine gaps — remain the subject of ongoing work and mean the $\mathrm{GL}_2/\mathbb{Q}$ conjecture is "essentially" but not "completely" resolved.

## The Calegari–Geraghty obstruction (2012–2018)

A widely held expectation in the 2000s was that patching would extend smoothly to imaginary quadratic fields and higher rank. Calegari and Geraghty made precise **why this fails**: when the relevant cohomology spreads across multiple degrees (defect $\ell_0>0$, as for $\mathrm{GL}_2$ over imaginary quadratic fields or $\mathrm{GL}_n$, $n\ge 3$), the naive numerical criterion collapses. Their derived reformulation salvages the method but only **conditionally**, contingent on unproven local–global compatibility and properties of Galois representations attached to torsion. This is the central honest limitation of the modern program: many higher-rank results are theorems-modulo-conjectures rather than unconditional.

## Scholze's torsion Galois representations (2013–2015)

Scholze's construction of Galois representations attached to torsion classes in the cohomology of locally symmetric spaces (via perfectoid Shimura varieties) was hailed as opening the **non-self-dual** door. The caveat repeatedly emphasized in the literature is that the local properties needed to feed these representations back into a Fontaine–Mazur-style argument — full local–global compatibility at $p$ and at ramified primes — were not established in full generality, leaving the resulting modularity statements conditional.

## Status of claimed general resolutions

There is **no** announced or disputed proof of the conjecture in full generality, in any dimension beyond two, or over a general number field. Nor is there a credible claimed counterexample. Every advertised result in the literature is honestly hedged with its operating hypotheses (regularity, self-duality, residual modularity, oddness, or conditionality on Galois-representation conjectures). The community consensus is that the conjecture is true and deep, that the $\mathrm{GL}_2/\mathbb{Q}$ case is morally settled with edge cases, and that the general case awaits a $p$-adic local Langlands correspondence and an unconditional theory of Galois representations in torsion cohomology — neither of which yet exists.
