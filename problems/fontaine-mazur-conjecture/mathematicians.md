# Top Mathematicians — The Fontaine–Mazur Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Jean-Marc Fontaine | 1980s–2019 | originator | Created $p$-adic Hodge theory (period rings, de Rham/crystalline/semistable conditions); co-stated the conjecture. |
| 2 | Barry Mazur | 1980s–present | originator | Founded Galois deformation theory; co-stated the conjecture and supplied its global deformation-ring framework. |
| 3 | Andrew Wiles | 1990s | breakthrough | Modularity of semistable elliptic curves; the residual-to-$p$-adic deformation method underlying all progress. |
| 4 | Richard Taylor | 1990s–present | breakthrough | Taylor–Wiles patching; potential-automorphy program extending modularity to higher dimension and CM fields. |
| 5 | Mark Kisin | 2000s–present | breakthrough | Proved the Fontaine–Mazur conjecture for $\mathrm{GL}_2/\mathbb{Q}$ in most cases via local deformation rings. |
| 6 | Matthew Emerton | 2000s–present | breakthrough | Completed cohomology and local–global compatibility; independent proof of much of the $\mathrm{GL}_2/\mathbb{Q}$ case. |
| 7 | Pierre Colmez | 2000s–present | breakthrough | $p$-adic local Langlands for $\mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{Q}_p)$ via $(\varphi,\Gamma)$-modules, the local input Kisin/Emerton use. |
| 8 | Christophe Breuil | 2000s–present | breakthrough | Initiated $p$-adic and mod-$p$ local Langlands for $\mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{Q}_p)$; integral $p$-adic Hodge theory. |
| 9 | Peter Scholze | 2010s–present | frontier | Perfectoid methods; Galois representations attached to torsion in cohomology, opening non-self-dual cases. |
| 10 | Frank Calegari | 2010s–present | frontier | With Geraghty, derived modularity-lifting "beyond Taylor–Wiles," targeting the defect-$\ell_0>0$ obstruction. |

## Notes

The active community sits at the confluence of the Langlands program, $p$-adic Hodge theory, and arithmetic geometry. Beyond the figures above, the frontier is shaped by David Geraghty (derived deformation rings), Toby Gee and James Newton (Serre weights, automorphy lifting, and recent work on symmetric-power functoriality), Ana Caraiani and the "ten-author" potential-automorphy-over-CM-fields collaboration, Vytautas Paškūnas (Banach-space representations and the Breuil–Mézard conjecture), Laurent Fargues and Scholze (geometrization of local Langlands), and Chandrashekhar Khare and Jean-Pierre Wintenberger (Serre's conjecture, which furnishes the residual modularity input over $\mathbb{Q}$). Centers of activity include Paris (Orsay, IHÉS, Jussieu), Bonn (Max Planck Institute, Hausdorff Center), Chicago, Stanford, Harvard, Imperial College London, and Cambridge. Work proceeds along two coupled fronts: building a $p$-adic local Langlands correspondence beyond $\mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{Q}_p)$, and establishing unconditional local–global compatibility for Galois representations in the torsion cohomology of locally symmetric spaces — the two missing pillars for any general resolution.
