# History — Sarnak's Möbius Disjointness Conjecture

_Origin, formulation, and timeline._

## Origin and motivation

The conjecture crystallizes a heuristic that analytic number theorists had long entertained: that the Möbius function $\mu(n)$ behaves "randomly" — like a sequence of independent signs — and therefore should not correlate with any sequence generated by a low-complexity deterministic rule. The slogan, attributed to Sarnak and echoing Iwaniec, is that **"the Möbius function does not correlate with any low-complexity sequence."** Peter Sarnak made this precise in 2010 by importing the dynamical notion of *zero topological entropy* as the formal meaning of "low complexity."

**Precise formulation.** Let $(X,T)$ be a topological dynamical system, i.e. $X$ a compact metric space and $T:X\to X$ a homeomorphism. A sequence $\xi(n)=f(T^n x)$ for $f\in C(X)$ and $x\in X$ is called *deterministic* (or an *observable of $(X,T)$*). Sarnak conjectures: if $(X,T)$ has **zero topological entropy** $h_{\mathrm{top}}(T)=0$, then
$$
\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n\le N} \mu(n)\, f(T^n x) \longrightarrow 0 \qquad (N\to\infty)
$$
for every $f\in C(X)$ and every $x\in X$. One says $\mu$ is **disjoint** (asymptotically orthogonal) from every zero-entropy system.

**Reformulations and relatives.** The conjecture is intimately tied to the much older **Chowla conjecture** (1965) on the vanishing of autocorrelations of $\mu$ (and of the Liouville function $\lambda$). Sarnak observed that *Chowla implies Sarnak*; the converse is false in general, but Tao (2017) showed the logarithmically-averaged Chowla and Sarnak conjectures are *equivalent*. The Prime Number Theorem is exactly the disjointness statement for the trivial one-point system; disjointness from the rotation $n\mapsto n+1$ on a circle is essentially the PNT in arithmetic progressions / $\sum \mu(n)e(n\alpha)=o(N)$ (Davenport, 1937).

## Timeline

- **1937** — Davenport proves $\sup_\alpha \big|\sum_{n\le N}\mu(n)e(n\alpha)\big| \ll_A N(\log N)^{-A}$, the disjointness from circle rotations (Kronecker systems), a prototype.
- **1965** — Chowla states his conjecture on the vanishing of higher correlations of $\mu$ and $\lambda$, the probabilistic backbone of the later picture.
- **2010** — Sarnak formulates the Möbius disjointness conjecture in lectures and the influential notes "Three lectures on the Möbius function, randomness and dynamics," framing zero entropy as low complexity.
- **2012** — Bourgain, Sarnak, and Ziegler prove a general orthogonality criterion ("the BSZ criterion") and verify disjointness for the horocycle flow and nilsystems; Green–Tao's nilsequence estimates cover all nilsystems.
- **2013** — Liu–Sarnak, and Bourgain, establish disjointness for various specific zero-entropy families (e.g. certain skew products, the Thue–Morse and Rudin–Shapiro sequences via Mauduit–Rivat).
- **2015** — El Abdalaoui, Lemańczyk, de la Rue and others develop the "Möbius/Chowla and ergodic theory" program; results for systems with discrete/quasi-discrete spectrum.
- **2017** — Tao proves the **logarithmically-averaged binary Chowla conjecture**, and (with Teräväinen and others) shows the log-averaged Sarnak and Chowla conjectures are equivalent; a major structural breakthrough.
- **2018** — Frantzikinakis–Host prove Sarnak's conjecture (in the logarithmic-average form) for **all systems with countably many ergodic measures**, the strongest general unconditional result.
- **2018–2019** — Disjointness established for many concrete zero-entropy classes: rank-one systems, some interval-exchange transformations, analytic Anzai skew products, systems of low complexity (Huang–Wang–Ye and others).
- **2019** — Tao–Teräväinen prove higher-order log-averaged Chowla for odd orders; partial results toward the full Chowla conjecture.
- **2020s** — Continued progress on positive-entropy obstructions, the "Veech/Möbius" framework, and quantitative disjointness for specific homogeneous and substitution systems; the general conjecture for *arbitrary* zero-entropy systems (with Cesàro, not just logarithmic, averages) remains **open**.

The frontier today is the gap between the logarithmically-averaged results (where deep progress exists) and the original Cesàro-average statement, and between special classes of zero-entropy systems and the universal claim.
