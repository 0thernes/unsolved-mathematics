# Top Mathematicians — Sarnak's Möbius Disjointness Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Peter Sarnak | 2010– | originator | Formulated the disjointness conjecture using zero topological entropy as "low complexity"; co-proved nilsystem and horocycle cases. |
| 2 | Sarvadaman Chowla | 1965 | originator | Stated the correlation conjecture for $\lambda$/$\mu$ that is the probabilistic root and self-correlation case of Sarnak's. |
| 3 | Harold Davenport | 1937 | breakthrough | Proved uniform cancellation in $\sum\mu(n)e(n\alpha)$, the prototype disjointness (circle rotations). |
| 4 | Hillel Furstenberg | 1967 | originator | Introduced the joinings/disjointness framework in ergodic theory that gives the conjecture its precise meaning. |
| 5 | Terence Tao | 2015– | breakthrough | Entropy-decrement proof of logarithmic two-point Chowla; established log-Chowla $\iff$ log-Sarnak. |
| 6 | Ben Green | 2008–2012 | breakthrough | With Tao, proved Möbius orthogonality to nilsequences, supplying the key algebraic class. |
| 7 | Jean Bourgain | 2011–2013 | breakthrough | Co-developed the BSZ criterion; proved disjointness for horocycle flows, some rank-one and IET systems. |
| 8 | Kaisa Matomäki | 2016– | breakthrough | With Radziwiłł, the short-intervals theorem for multiplicative functions powering modern progress. |
| 9 | Maksym Radziwiłł | 2016– | breakthrough | Co-author of the short-interval and averaged-Chowla results; advances on smooth/skew-product disjointness. |
| 10 | Nikos Frantzikinakis | 2017– | frontier | With Host, proved log-Sarnak for systems with countably many ergodic measures; influential surveys. |

## Notes

The active community sits squarely at the interface of **analytic number theory** and **ergodic theory**, and is unusually collaborative across that boundary. Beyond the ten above, central contributors include **Mariusz Lemańczyk**, **Thierry de la Rue**, and **Joanna Kułaga-Przymus** (the Toruń/Rouen ergodic-theory school, who systematized the Chowla–Sarnak dictionary), **El Houcein El Abdalaoui**, **Bernard Host**, **Joni Teräväinen** (Tao's collaborator on higher-order log-Chowla), **Christian Mauduit** and **Joël Rivat** (digits-of-primes and automatic sequences), **Clemens Müllner** (all automatic sequences), **Adam Kanigowski**, and the dynamics group of **Wen Huang, Zhiren Wang, and Xiangdong Ye** in China. Activity is sustained through regular workshops (e.g. at the IAS, CIRM, the Banff and Oberwolfach institutes, and the Polish ergodic-theory programs), and the field's progress is tightly coupled to advances on the Chowla conjecture and on multiplicative functions in short intervals.
