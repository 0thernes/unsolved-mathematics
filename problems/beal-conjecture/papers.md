**Notes on reliability.** Rows 1–5, 7, 17, and 20 are canonical and certainly real; identifiers given there are high-confidence. For the modular-method and generalized-Fermat case studies (rows 6, 8–16, 18, 21–24) the *works and authors are real research in this area*, but exact titles, years, and especially DOIs vary across versions (journal vs. arXiv) and are marked "needs-verification"; identifiers are withheld ("—") rather than guessed. Row 19 (Norvig) is a well-known online computational essay on Beal counterexample search; its publication metadata is informal, hence no DOI. Row 25 is Mochizuki's published IUT paper underlying the *disputed* $abc$ claim relevant to Beal's conditional resolution; the Scholze–Stix objection (2018, unpublished note) is discussed in `attempts.md` and is not listed as a separate row because it has no formal identifier. Rows 16–17 (Tijdeman, Mihăilescu) concern Catalan's conjecture — included because Catalan ($A^x - B^y = 1$) is the companion exponential Diophantine problem and Mihăilescu's 2004 proof is a landmark of the surrounding theory. Human source-checking is required before any "needs-verification" or "ai-suggested" row is treated as established.
