# Attempts — The Beal Conjecture

## Genuine partial results (near-misses by signature)

The substantive progress on Beal consists of complete resolutions of individual exponent signatures $(p,q,r)$ of the generalized Fermat equation $A^p+B^q=C^r$, each a near-miss in that it disposes of one slice of the infinite problem.

- **Fermat's Last Theorem (Wiles, with Taylor, 1995).** Settles the equal-exponent case $A^n+B^n=C^n$, $n\ge 3$ — the diagonal of the Beal range. This is the deepest established sub-result and the template for all that follow.
- **Darmon–Merel (1997).** Resolved $x^n+y^n=2z^n$ and the signatures $(n,n,2)$ and $(n,n,3)$ for $n\ge$ small bounds, extending the modular method to non-diagonal exponents.
- **Bennett, Skinner, Kraus, and collaborators (2000s).** A long sequence of papers eliminating mixed signatures via Frey curves and level-lowering, e.g. families $(2,4,n)$, $(2,n,4)$, and assorted $(3,3,n)$-type cases.
- **Poonen–Schaefer–Stoll (2007), Bruin, Siksek, Chen, Dahmen (2000s–2010s).** Resolved hard individual signatures including $(2,3,7)$, $(2,3,8)$, $(2,3,9)$, $(2,4,5)$, $(3,4,5)$, and others, often by elliptic-curve Chabauty or descent where the modular method left residual newforms. Note that signatures involving an exponent equal to 2 lie just *outside* the strict Beal hypothesis $x,y,z>2$ but bound the surrounding Fermat–Catalan landscape.

These collectively confirm Beal on an expanding but still finite catalogue of signatures. No method among them generalizes to all triples.

## The $abc$ route and the Mochizuki dispute

Because $abc$ implies Beal up to finitely many exceptions, any accepted proof of the $abc$ conjecture would be a decisive near-resolution. **Shinichi Mochizuki** announced a proof of $abc$ via inter-universal Teichmüller theory in 2012, published in *Publications of RIMS* in 2021. The claim is **disputed**: in 2018 **Peter Scholze and Jakob Stix** circulated a detailed objection ("Why $abc$ is still a conjecture"), arguing that a specific identification in the proof of a key inequality (around "Corollary 3.12") collapses a quantity that must be tracked, invalidating the argument. Mochizuki rejected the objection; the broader number-theory community has not accepted the proof. Stated neutrally: $abc$ remains, by mainstream consensus, open, and hence the $abc$-conditional path to Beal is not currently available.

## Claimed direct proofs (retracted, withdrawn, or unrefereed)

The Beal Prize (now \$1{,}000{,}000, administered by the AMS) has attracted a steady stream of purported elementary proofs, almost all from outside the research community. None has passed peer review. Common failure modes, documented by referees and in expository commentary, include:

- **Illegitimate modular-arithmetic reductions** that implicitly assume coprimality conclusions, or that handle only finitely many residues.
- **Errors in infinite descent** that silently require an exponent equal to 2 (where descent genuinely works) and break for $x,y,z>2$.
- **Confusing necessary with sufficient conditions**, e.g. deriving a common-factor relation that holds for *known* solutions and asserting it forbids coprime ones.

In keeping with house policy, no specific amateur preprint is endorsed or refuted here beyond noting that, as of the present, the AMS has not awarded the prize and no claimed elementary proof is accepted. Notably, an elementary proof would be surprising precisely because the nearest established result (FLT) required modularity; a short proof would imply an unexpectedly elementary route to FLT as a corollary, which the community regards as strong prior evidence against any brief argument.

## Status of the "near-misses" taken together

The honest summary: Beal is **confirmed on infinitely many but not all** specific configurations (every fully resolved signature, plus the diagonal via FLT), is **conditionally near-settled** under $abc$, and has **no accepted general proof or counterexample**. The gap between "every fixed exponent triple" and "all exponent triples uniformly" is exactly where every attempt has stalled.
