# Top Mathematicians — The Beal Conjecture

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Andrew Beal | Contemporary (1952–) | originator | Formulated the conjecture (c. 1993) from computational evidence; endowed the \$1M Beal Prize. |
| 2 | R. Daniel Mauldin | Contemporary | expositor | Published the conjecture and prize in the *Notices of the AMS* (1997), fixing its name and framing. |
| 3 | Andrew Wiles | Contemporary (1953–) | breakthrough | Proved Fermat's Last Theorem (1995), settling the equal-exponent coprime case and supplying the modular template. |
| 4 | Richard Taylor | Contemporary (1962–) | breakthrough | Co-authored the Hecke-algebra paper completing Wiles's proof; central to the modularity machinery used throughout. |
| 5 | Henri Darmon | Contemporary (1965–) | frontier | With Granville, proved finiteness per signature (1995); with Merel resolved key ternary cases; architect of "Darmon's program." |
| 6 | Andrew Granville | Contemporary (1962–) | frontier | Co-proved the Darmon–Granville finiteness theorem; foundational analytic and Diophantine input to the Fermat–Catalan framework. |
| 7 | Loïc Merel | Contemporary (1965–) | breakthrough | With Darmon, resolved $x^n+y^n=2z^n$ and related signatures via winding quotients (1997). |
| 8 | Michael A. Bennett | Contemporary | frontier | Prolific resolver of generalized-Fermat signatures via the modular method; co-author of major surveys of the equation. |
| 9 | Samir Siksek | Contemporary | frontier | Modular method over number fields, asymptotic Fermat, and explicit Chabauty; key to extending coverage of exponent triples. |
| 10 | Bjorn Poonen | Contemporary (1968–) | frontier | With Schaefer and Stoll, resolved hard signatures (e.g. $x^2+y^3=z^7$) by descent and Chabauty methods. |

## Notes

The active community on Beal is essentially the **generalized Fermat / Fermat–Catalan** community, since Beal is the coprime $x,y,z>2$ slice of that problem. The center of gravity is the modular-method school — Darmon's program and its descendants — pursued by groups around Michael Bennett (UBC), Samir Siksek and Nuno Freitas (Warwick / and collaborators), Imin Chen, Sander Dahmen, Bartosz Naskręcki, Nicolas Billerey, Luis Dieulefait, and Michael Stoll, among others. Their work proceeds signature by signature, combining Frey curves, modularity over real and imaginary quadratic fields, level-lowering, and explicit (elliptic-curve) Chabauty. A second, looser community connects Beal to the $abc$ conjecture (Masser, Oesterlé; and the disputed Mochizuki program), since an effective $abc$ would essentially settle Beal. The conjecture also draws sustained, mostly fruitless, attention from amateurs because of the seven-figure prize; the refereeing burden falls on the AMS. No single individual is presently "the" Beal expert — progress is distributed across the modular-method network, and a uniform resolution is widely expected to require an idea beyond the current toolkit.
