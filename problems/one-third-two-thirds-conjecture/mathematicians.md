# Top Mathematicians — The 1/3–2/3 Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Sergei S. Kislitsyn | 1968 | originator | Earliest known statement (1968, in Russian) of the balanced-pair question for finite posets; the historical root of the conjecture. |
| 2 | Michael L. Fredman | 1970s | originator | Independently raised the question via sorting/merging lower bounds (1976), supplying the information-theoretic motivation. |
| 3 | Nathan (Nati) Linial | 1980s | originator | Gave the modern canonical formulation (1984) and proved the conjecture for width-2 posets with the sharp constant $\tfrac12-\tfrac{\sqrt5}{10}$. |
| 4 | Jeff Kahn | 1990s | breakthrough | Co-author of the Kahn–Saks $[3/11,8/11]$ theorem and the Kahn–Kim entropy method; central to nearly all general progress. |
| 5 | Michael Saks | 1990s | breakthrough | Co-author of the Brunn–Minkowski / log-concavity proof of the first universal balance constant (1991). |
| 6 | Jeong Han Kim | 1990s | breakthrough | Co-developed the entropy-and-sorting approach (Kahn–Kim), giving a polynomial-time constant-factor comparison. |
| 7 | Graham R. Brightwell | 1990s–2000s | frontier | Verified the conjecture for semiorders, height-2 and other classes; sharpened bounds; author of the standard survey. |
| 8 | Stefan Felsner | 1990s–present | frontier | Co-author of the $\approx 0.276$ record bound and structural results for $N$-free/series–parallel and related classes. |
| 9 | William T. Trotter | 1980s–present | expositor | Leading order-theorist; co-author of the record bound and authority on dimension theory framing the problem's context. |
| 10 | Lawrence A. Shepp | 1980s | breakthrough | Proved the XYZ inequality, the key correlation inequality for linear extensions used as a tool in balanced-pair arguments. |

## Notes

The active community is small and centered in order theory and probabilistic / extremal combinatorics. Its modern intellectual lineage runs through **Jeff Kahn** and **Michael Saks** (the convex-geometry breakthrough) and the order-theory school of **Brightwell**, **Felsner**, and **Trotter**, who supplied the class-by-class verifications and the still-standing $\tfrac12-\tfrac{\sqrt5}{10}\approx 0.276$ record. A second, overlapping community works on the algorithmic descendant — *sorting under partial information* — where **Cardinal, Fiorini, Joret, Jungers, and Munro** showed the information-theoretic bound is achievable up to constants, effectively settling the conjecture's computational payoff even though the sharp $1/3$ remains open. Forests and trees remain a focused sub-front (Zaguia and collaborators). Progress has been steady but incremental: no one has beaten the early-1990s constant for general posets, and the field broadly regards a full proof as requiring a genuinely new inequality or structural idea rather than a refinement of existing methods.
