**Note on completeness and verification.** This conjecture's core literature is small and concentrated: the certainly-real anchors are Fredman (1976, *Theoretical Computer Science*), Linial (1984, *SIAM J. Computing*, width-2 case), Kahn–Saks (1991, *Order*, the $[3/11,8/11]$ bound), Kahn–Kim (entropy/sorting), Brightwell–Felsner–Trotter (1995, the $\approx 0.276$ record), and Brightwell's survey *Balanced pairs in partial orders* (1999, *Discrete Mathematics*). For these the authors, years, venues, and results are well-documented; several DOIs above are given as plausible but flagged **needs-verification** because I am not certain of the exact registered DOI string, and I have deliberately not invented precise numbers where unsure (rows shown with "—"). Rows 11–25 thin out into ambient theory (Trotter's dimension-theory book), the sorting-under-partial-information line (Cardinal–Fiorini–Joret–Jungers–Munro, *Combinatorica*), and class-specific/computational follow-ups whose exact titles and identifiers I could not confirm; these are marked **needs-verification** or **ai-suggested** rather than presented as established. The honest count of canonical, certainly-real papers central to the conjecture is roughly **6–8** (rows 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 10, 13, plus 11 as ambient); the remaining rows are real research directions and likely-real works listed with low confidence, and should be checked against MathSciNet/zbMATH before citation. No identifier here should be treated as confirmed unless marked high-confidence.
