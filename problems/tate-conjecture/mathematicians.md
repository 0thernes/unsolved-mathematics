# Top Mathematicians — The Tate Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | John Tate | 1925–2019 | originator | Posed the conjecture (1962/63); proved it for endomorphisms of abelian varieties over finite fields (1966); 1994 Motives survey fixing the precise statements. |
| 2 | Alexander Grothendieck | 1928–2014 | originator | Built étale $\ell$-adic cohomology and the standard conjectures, the framework in which the Tate conjecture is stated and tied to motives. |
| 3 | Jean-Pierre Serre | 1926–present | breakthrough | $\ell$-adic Galois representations, good reduction (Serre–Tate), and the theory of the image of Galois constraining Tate classes. |
| 4 | Pierre Deligne | 1944–present | breakthrough | Proof of the Weil conjectures (Weil I/II), supplying Frobenius weights and hard Lefschetz; absolute Hodge classes on abelian varieties. |
| 5 | Gerd Faltings | 1954–present | breakthrough | Proved the Tate conjecture for divisors on abelian varieties over number/finitely generated fields, with semisimplicity (1983). |
| 6 | Yuri Zarhin | 1951–present | breakthrough | Extended the abelian-variety Tate conjecture and semisimplicity to positive characteristic. |
| 7 | J.S. Milne | 1942–present | expositor | Systematized the equivalences over finite fields (Tate $\Leftrightarrow$ standard conjectures + semisimplicity; numerical vs. homological equivalence). |
| 8 | François Charles | 1981–present | frontier | Tate conjecture for $K3$ surfaces over finite/finitely generated fields; supersingular reduction methods. |
| 9 | Davesh Maulik | 1978–present | frontier | Tate conjecture for $K3$ surfaces (large primes) via Shimura-variety and deformation techniques. |
| 10 | Keerthi Madapusi Pera | 1983–present | frontier | Integral canonical models of orthogonal Shimura varieties; Tate conjecture for $K3$s in odd characteristic and (with Kim) at $p=2$. |

## Notes

The active community clusters around arithmetic geometry and the Langlands program. Beyond the $K3$ specialists (Charles, Maulik, Madapusi Pera, Kim), important nearby work comes from researchers on the **Mumford–Tate conjecture** and $\ell$-adic monodromy (e.g. Cadoret, Moonen, Pink, Vasiu), on **Shimura varieties and special cycles** (the Kudla program; Bruinier, Howard, Yun, Zhang), and on **motives and standard conjectures** (André, O'Sullivan). The finite-field side is advanced by work descending from Milne and Honda–Tate theory. Progress is incremental and class-by-class: the field expects the general conjecture over number fields to require substantial new ideas, likely a deeper integration of automorphic methods with the geometry of cycles. No single group "owns" the problem; momentum follows breakthroughs in Shimura-variety integral models and in the construction of algebraic cycles.
