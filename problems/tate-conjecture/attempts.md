# Attempts — The Tate Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

The Tate conjecture has a comparatively clean record: unlike some celebrated problems, it has not been plagued by a string of high-profile retracted "proofs" of the general case. Progress has come instead through a sequence of genuine, peer-reviewed theorems that resolve ever-larger classes of varieties, while the full statement over number fields remains open. The notable items below are landmark partial results, a few near-misses, and the standard caveats.

## Tate's own first case (1966)

Tate proved the conjecture for **endomorphisms of abelian varieties over finite fields**, deducing it from a finiteness result on $p$-divisible groups (later cleanly available via Faltings's finiteness theorems). This is the foundational success and the template for all that followed.

## Faltings (1983) — the number-field abelian case

In proving the Mordell conjecture, **Gerd Faltings** also established the Tate conjecture for divisors on abelian varieties over finitely generated fields, together with **semisimplicity** of the associated Galois representations. Widely regarded as the deepest result attached to the conjecture; it earned Faltings the 1986 Fields Medal.

## Zarhin and positive characteristic

**Yuri Zarhin** extended the abelian-variety Tate conjecture and semisimplicity to fields of finite characteristic, first under restrictions ($p>2$) and subsequently more generally, closing gaps left by characteristic-2 subtleties.

## The $K3$ resolution (2012–2016) — a multi-author near-simultaneous push

The Tate conjecture for $K3$ surfaces over finitely generated fields was settled in characteristic $\neq 2$ by a cluster of papers from **Davesh Maulik**, **François Charles**, and **Keerthi Madapusi Pera** (with later work by **Wansu Kim and Madapusi Pera** on $p=2$). These rely on the Kuga–Satake construction and integral models of Shimura varieties. The near-simultaneity and partial overlap of these papers is itself notable; the community regards the $K3$ case as fully and correctly resolved.

## Cautions and disputed/unverified claims

- **General-case "solutions."** The general Tate conjecture over number fields is occasionally the subject of preprints (often on arXiv or vixra) claiming a full proof. None has gained acceptance, and these should be treated as unverified. The reference literature records *no* widely endorsed proof of the general case; any such claim should be checked against the expert consensus and the absence of publication in a leading venue. We state this neutrally: the burden of a complete, refereed argument has not been met.
- **The semisimplicity assumption.** Several otherwise-correct results are *conditional* on the semisimplicity of Frobenius / Galois action; readers must check whether a cited theorem is unconditional. Conflating the conditional and unconditional forms is a common error rather than a dispute.
- **Mumford–Tate transfer.** Attempts to deduce the Tate conjecture from the Hodge conjecture (or vice versa) via the Mumford–Tate conjecture are incomplete: the Mumford–Tate conjecture is itself open in general, so this remains a near-miss bridge rather than a proof.

## Conditional and equivalence results (near-misses by reduction)

Work of **J.S. Milne** and others shows that over finite fields the Tate conjecture is equivalent to the equality of numerical and $\ell$-adic homological equivalence plus the order-of-pole statement, and that it implies Grothendieck's Lefschetz standard conjecture. These do not resolve the conjecture but show exactly what additional input would.
