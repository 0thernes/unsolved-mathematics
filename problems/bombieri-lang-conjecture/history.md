# History — The Bombieri–Lang Conjecture

_Origin, formulation, and timeline._

## Origin

The Bombieri–Lang conjecture is the central higher-dimensional generalization of the Mordell conjecture, asking how the geometry of an algebraic variety controls the distribution of its rational points. Mordell's 1922 conjecture predicted that a smooth projective curve of genus $g \ge 2$ over a number field $K$ has only finitely many $K$-rational points — and such a curve is "of general type" precisely when $g \ge 2$. The natural question is what replaces *finiteness* in higher dimension, where finiteness manifestly fails: a general-type surface can contain rational or elliptic curves carrying infinitely many points, so $X(K)$ need not be finite.

The resolution, due independently to **Enrico Bombieri** (in a 1980 University of Chicago seminar, never formally published by him) and **Serge Lang** (in print across the late 1970s and 1980s), is to weaken "finite" to "**not Zariski dense**." For a smooth projective variety $X$ of general type (with big canonical bundle $K_X$) over a number field $K$, the conjecture asserts that $X(K)$ lies in a proper Zariski-closed subset $Z \subsetneq X$. Lang's stronger geometric form predicts a closed proper **exceptional (special) locus** absorbing all but finitely many points outside it, conjecturally equal to the union of all positive-dimensional images of non-constant maps from abelian varieties.

## Formulations and reformulations

Several intertwined statements travel under the name:

- **Weak (number-field) form:** $X$ of general type $\Rightarrow X(K)$ not Zariski dense.
- **Lang's geometric conjecture:** the special set $\mathrm{Sp}(X)$ is a proper closed subset, and outside it points are sparse; conjecturally $X \setminus \mathrm{Sp}(X)$ is Mordellic and Kobayashi-hyperbolic.
- **Lang–Vojta (integral-point) version** for quasi-projective varieties of log general type, plus function-field and positive-characteristic analogues.

These now sit beneath **Vojta's conjecture** (1987), which would imply Bombieri–Lang via a single height/discriminant inequality.

## Timeline

**1922** — Mordell conjectures finiteness of rational points on curves of genus $\ge 2$.

**1974** — Lang articulates a programmatic vision linking hyperbolicity, integral points, and Diophantine finiteness ("Higher dimensional Diophantine problems," _Bull. AMS_).

**1980** — Bombieri poses the Zariski-density formulation for surfaces of general type in a Chicago seminar.

**1983** — Faltings proves the Mordell conjecture (with the Tate and Shafarevich conjectures), settling the curve case and energizing the higher-dimensional program.

**1986** — Lang's survey "Hyperbolic and Diophantine analysis" (_Bull. AMS_) consolidates the conjectural web and the special-set picture.

**1987** — Vojta proposes his conjectures, drawing the analogy with Nevanlinna theory and giving a unifying inequality implying Bombieri–Lang.

**1991** — Faltings proves the Mordell–Lang conjecture for subvarieties of abelian varieties via a Vojta-type method; extended in **1994** ("The general case of S. Lang's conjecture") to semiabelian varieties.

**1991–1996** — Vojta and McQuillan give further proofs/extensions of Mordell–Lang; McQuillan's value-distribution methods enter the program.

**1997** — Caporaso, Harris, and Mazur show that **uniform** Bombieri–Lang would imply a bound on the number of rational points on curves depending only on genus — a striking, counterintuitive corollary.

**2000s** — Conditional results proliferate (Abramovich, Hassett, Pacelli) for moduli of curves and subvarieties of abelian varieties.

**2018–2021** — Lawrence–Venkatesh give a new period-map proof of the Mordell conjecture and partial higher-dimensional non-density statements, reviving optimism about unconditional progress.

**2021–2024** — Activity around Lang–Vojta for surfaces (Corvaja–Zannier, Levin, Autissier, and others) yields integral-point finiteness in special cases.

**Present** — The conjecture remains unproven in every dimension $\ge 2$; no general-type surface is known unconditionally to have non-dense rational points purely on account of being of general type.
