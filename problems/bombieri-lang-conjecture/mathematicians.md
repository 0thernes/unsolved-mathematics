# Top Mathematicians — The Bombieri–Lang Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Serge Lang | 1970s–2000s | originator | Built the general-type / Mordellic / hyperbolic conjectural web and the special-set formalism; published the conjecture and made it canonical. |
| 2 | Enrico Bombieri | 1980s–present | originator | Gave the sharp Zariski-density formulation for surfaces of general type (1980 Chicago seminar); elementary reworking of Vojta's Mordell proof (1990). |
| 3 | Louis Mordell | 1920s | originator | Conjectured finiteness of rational points on curves of genus $\ge 2$ — the dimension-1 root of the whole program. |
| 4 | Gerd Faltings | 1980s–1990s | breakthrough | Proved Mordell (1983) and the Mordell–Lang conjecture for abelian (1991) and semiabelian (1994) varieties — the deepest confirmed cases. |
| 5 | Paul Vojta | 1980s–present | breakthrough | Formulated the Vojta conjectures implying Bombieri–Lang; gave Diophantine-approximation proofs of Mordell–Lang. |
| 6 | Michael McQuillan | 1990s–present | breakthrough | Foliation/value-distribution proof of semiabelian Mordell–Lang and of Green–Griffiths–Lang for surfaces of general type. |
| 7 | Joe Harris | 1990s | frontier | Co-author of the uniformity theorem showing uniform Bombieri–Lang bounds points on curves by genus alone. |
| 8 | Barry Mazur | 1990s | frontier | Co-author of Caporaso–Harris–Mazur; clarified the structural and philosophical stakes of the conjecture. |
| 9 | Frédéric Campana | 2000s–present | frontier | Orbifold / "special variety" theory refining the general-type-vs-special dichotomy underlying the conjecture. |
| 10 | Brian Lawrence & Akshay Venkatesh | 2018–present | frontier | $p$-adic period-map proof of Mordell and new higher-dimensional non-density results (with Sawin). |

## Notes

The active community sits at the intersection of arithmetic geometry, complex hyperbolicity, and Diophantine approximation. Around the **Vojta-conjecture** core work continues from Vojta himself, with related height-inequality and integral-point research by **Pietro Corvaja**, **Umberto Zannier**, **Aaron Levin**, and **Pascal Autissier** (Lang–Vojta for surfaces). On the **hyperbolicity** side, **Jean-Pierre Demailly**, **Damian Brotbek**, **Erwan Rousseau**, **Simone Diverio**, and **Junjiro Noguchi** drive the complex-analytic analogue, while **Frédéric Campana**'s orbifold program reframes the geometric dichotomy. The **period-map** lineage (Lawrence, Venkatesh, Sawin, and collaborators such as **Daniel Litt** and **Will Sawin**) is the newest and most dynamic unconditional front. Conditional structural consequences continue to be explored by **Dan Abramovich**, **Brendan Hassett**, and others. No single group "owns" the problem; progress is incremental and spread across these overlapping schools, with Faltings's theorems and Vojta's conjecture as the shared reference points.
