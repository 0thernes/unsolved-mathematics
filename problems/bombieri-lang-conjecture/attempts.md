# Attempts — The Bombieri–Lang Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

The Bombieri–Lang conjecture has not attracted the cottage industry of claimed elementary proofs that surrounds problems like Goldbach or Fermat — its technical entry barrier is high, and the experts who work on it are cautious. Progress has come instead through a sequence of landmark *partial* theorems and a few corrections to the literature.

## Faltings's theorems — the decisive partial results

Faltings's 1983 proof of the **Mordell conjecture** settled the curve case (dimension 1) completely and remains the foundational evidence. His 1991 paper, *Diophantine approximation on abelian varieties*, proved the **Mordell–Lang conjecture** for subvarieties of abelian varieties: if $X \subseteq A$ is a subvariety not containing a translate of a positive-dimensional abelian subvariety, then $X(K)$ is finite. The 1994 sequel, *The general case of S. Lang's conjecture*, extended this to semiabelian varieties. These are unconditional Bombieri–Lang in the special situation where the variety sits inside a group — the single largest body of confirmed cases. The proofs rely on Vojta's product-theorem method and were a genuine surprise in their power.

## Vojta and McQuillan — independent confirmations and refinements

Vojta (1991, 1996) gave alternative and strengthened proofs of the Mordell–Lang statements, embedding them in his height-inequality framework. McQuillan (1995) reproved the semiabelian case via a different, more dynamical argument. None of these extend to general $X$, but together they show the abelian-variety case is now extremely robust — a near-miss only in the sense that the techniques stop precisely at the boundary of the group structure.

## Lawrence–Venkatesh — a new method, new cases

The 2020 Lawrence–Venkatesh period-map proof of Mordell, and its higher-dimensional offshoots (including Lawrence–Sawin on the Shafarevich conjecture for abelian-variety hypersurfaces, 2020), are the most recent genuine advances. They establish non-density in higher-dimensional families with large monodromy — true new instances of Bombieri–Lang-type behaviour — but do not reach a general-type variety lacking a suitable period map.

## Conditional and structural near-misses

Caporaso, Harris, and Mazur (1997, *Uniformity of rational points*) is famous not as a proof but as a conditional bombshell: assuming a uniform version of Bombieri–Lang, the number of rational points on a curve of genus $g$ over a fixed number field is bounded by a constant depending only on $g$ (and the field). The result was so counterintuitive that it prompted intense scrutiny of whether the conjecture *should* be believed; no error was found, and the implication stands. Later work by Pacelli, Abramovich, and others tightened these conditional consequences. This episode is the closest the subject has to a "dispute": the worry was about the *plausibility* of the hypothesis, not about a flawed proof.

## On claimed proofs

There is **no widely circulated claimed proof** of the full conjecture that has been retracted or is under active dispute, in contrast to neighbouring areas (e.g. the long controversy over Mochizuki's claimed proof of the closely related **abc conjecture**, which — if correct — would yield deep Diophantine consequences but is not generally accepted by the community). The honest state of affairs is that Bombieri–Lang remains open in every dimension $\ge 2$, with the abelian-variety and large-monodromy families as the only unconditionally settled regimes, and with Vojta's conjecture as the principal route by which a full proof is expected to arrive, if at all.
