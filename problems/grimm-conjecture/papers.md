**Note on shortfall and honesty.** Grimm's Conjecture is a comparatively narrow problem with a small genuine literature. Rows 1, 2, 5, 9, 13, 15, and 19 correspond to canonical, certainly-real works: Grimm's 1969 *Monthly* note; the Erdős–Selfridge analyses; the 1976 Ramachandra–Shorey–Tijdeman paper in *J. Reine Angew. Math.* (vol. 273); the successive editions of Guy's *Unsolved Problems in Number Theory* (problem B32); and the Shorey–Tijdeman monograph *Exponential Diophantine Equations* (Cambridge, 1986/1990). The remaining rows are plausible leads drawn from the surrounding Erdős–Selfridge / Shorey–Tijdeman program on prime factors of blocks of consecutive integers; their exact titles, authorship, and years should be confirmed before citation, hence the "needs-verification" and "ai-suggested" flags and the deliberate absence of DOIs/arXiv ids. No identifiers have been invented. I have not padded with fabricated DOIs; where I am not certain a specific paper exists with the stated metadata, the flag says so.
