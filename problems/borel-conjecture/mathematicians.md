# Top Mathematicians — The Borel Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Armand Borel | 1923–2003 | originator | Posed the topological rigidity question (c. 1953) inspired by rigidity of locally symmetric spaces; foundational work on Lie/arithmetic groups and classifying spaces. |
| 2 | G. D. (George) Mostow | 1923–2017 | breakthrough | Strong (geometric) rigidity of locally symmetric spaces, dim $\geq 3$ — the archetypal positive instance motivating the conjecture. |
| 3 | C. T. C. Wall | 1936– | breakthrough | Surgery theory for compact manifolds; recast Borel rigidity as triviality of the surgery structure set via the surgery exact sequence. |
| 4 | William Browder | 1934– | originator | Co-architect (with Novikov, Sullivan, Wall) of surgery theory, the framework in which the conjecture is precisely stated. |
| 5 | F. Thomas Farrell | 1941– | breakthrough | With Hsiang and Jones, proved rigidity for flat, infranil, and nonpositively curved manifolds; co-formulated the Farrell–Jones Conjecture. |
| 6 | Lowell E. Jones | 1945– | breakthrough | With Farrell, developed the geodesic-flow / controlled-topology method and the Farrell–Jones isomorphism conjecture implying Borel. |
| 7 | Frank Quinn | 1946– | breakthrough | Controlled topology, "Ends of Maps", and the homology theory of $\mathbf L$-spectra; 4-manifold surgery with Freedman. |
| 8 | Michael H. Freedman | 1951– | breakthrough | Topological surgery in dimension 4 (good groups), enabling Borel-type rigidity for aspherical 4-manifolds with amenable $\pi_1$. |
| 9 | Wolfgang Lück | 1957– | frontier | With Bartels (and Reich), proved Farrell–Jones / Borel for hyperbolic and CAT(0) groups; leading expositor of assembly maps and classifying spaces. |
| 10 | Arthur Bartels | 1973– | frontier | Co-author of the controlled-topology proofs of FJC for hyperbolic, CAT(0), $\mathrm{GL}_n(\mathbb Z)$ and lattice groups — the current frontier. |

## Notes

The active community clusters around the **Farrell–Jones Conjecture** as the master mechanism. The German school led by **Wolfgang Lück** (Bonn) and **Arthur Bartels** (Münster), with **Holger Reich** (Berlin), **Henrik Rüping**, **Daniel Kasprowski**, **Mark Ullmann**, **Christoph Winges**, **Holger Kammeyer**, and **Wolfgang Steimle**, has driven inheritance theorems and verifications for ever-larger group classes (lattices in Lie groups, $\mathrm{GL}_n(\mathbb Z)$, mapping class groups in progress). **Shmuel Weinberger** (Chicago) connects the topic to geometric group theory and large-scale geometry; **Tom Farrell** (now at Tsinghua/YMSC) and collaborators continue work on smooth rigidity and exotic structures. Foundational input continues from surgery theory (legacy of **Wall**, **Ranicki**) and controlled topology (**Quinn**, **Ferry**, **Pedersen**). The problem sits at the crossroads of $L$-theory, algebraic $K$-theory, geometric group theory, and 4-manifold topology, and remains a primary organizing target of geometric topology.
