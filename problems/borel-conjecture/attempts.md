# Attempts — The Borel Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

**Mostow rigidity as proof of concept (1968).** Mostow's theorem — that closed hyperbolic $n$-manifolds ($n\geq 3$) with isomorphic fundamental groups are isometric — is the geometric archetype. It is *stronger* than Borel (isometry, not just homeomorphism) but *narrower* (requires a hyperbolic metric). It convinced the community the topological statement was plausible and set the agenda.

**Waldhausen's 3-manifold rigidity (1968).** Waldhausen proved homotopy equivalences of Haken (sufficiently large, aspherical) 3-manifolds are homotopic to homeomorphisms — a complete Borel-type result in dimension 3 for a large class, decades before geometrization closed the remaining cases.

**Farrell–Hsiang on tori and flat manifolds (1977–1983).** Using the surgery exact sequence and a clever use of expanding endomorphisms ("Frobenius" / transfer arguments), Farrell and Hsiang proved topological rigidity of the torus $T^n$ and of flat and infranil manifolds. This was the first systematic confirmation beyond the geometric cases and established the structure-set-vanishing strategy.

**Farrell–Jones nonpositive curvature (1989–1993).** The landmark partial result: rigidity for closed nonpositively curved manifolds in dimensions $\neq 3,4$. Their geodesic-flow / controlled-topology method became the template for all subsequent progress. Crucially, they also showed the **smooth Borel statement is false**, finding negatively curved manifolds that are homeomorphic but carry exotic (non-diffeomorphic) smooth structures — a permanent caution that the conjecture lives in $\mathrm{TOP}$, not $\mathrm{DIFF}$ or $\mathrm{PL}$.

**Bartels–Lück–Reich, hyperbolic and CAT(0) groups (2008–2012).** The current state of the art: a proof of the Farrell–Jones Conjecture (hence Borel in dim $\geq 5$) for word-hyperbolic groups, and with Wegner for CAT(0) groups, via flow spaces and transfer reducibility. This dramatically enlarged the class of groups for which Borel is a theorem, without requiring a manifold-level nonpositively curved metric.

**Dimension-4 near-misses (Freedman–Quinn).** Topological surgery in dimension 4 (Freedman 1982; Freedman–Quinn 1990) yields Borel-type rigidity for aspherical 4-manifolds with **good** fundamental groups (amenable / subexponential growth). For general $\pi_1$ the **disk-embedding** problem blocks the argument, leaving 4-dimensional Borel rigidity genuinely open — a near-miss conditioned on a separate hard conjecture.

**Disputes and corrections.** The Borel Conjecture has not attracted famous *retracted* claimed proofs of the full statement — its difficulty has deterred premature global claims. The disputes that arise are narrower and technical:

- **Smooth vs. topological category.** Early informal statements of "rigidity" sometimes blurred categories; the Farrell–Jones counterexamples settled that only the **homeomorphism** statement can be true. Any claim of *smooth* Borel rigidity is false and is the standard objection raised against category-confused formulations.
- **Hypotheses on the assembly map.** Several published proofs of Farrell–Jones-type statements have required careful corrections to the decoration ($L^{\langle-\infty\rangle}$ vs. $L^h$, $L^s$) and to the family ($\mathcal{FIN}$ vs. $\mathcal{VCyc}$); the appearance of **Nil** and **UNil** terms (Cappell) showed naïve assembly statements over finite subgroups are *incorrect*, and that virtually cyclic subgroups are essential. This is a correction-of-record rather than a dispute, but it is the most common technical pitfall.
- **Mapping class groups / $\mathrm{Out}(F_n)$.** Announced extensions of FJC to these groups have at times outpaced complete published verification; the community treats such cases as "in progress" pending full refereeing rather than settled.

The honest summary: every confirmed advance is a theorem about a *restricted class of groups or dimensions*. No attempt to date resolves the conjecture for **all** closed aspherical manifolds, and the general statement remains open.
