## Notes

The active community sits at the intersection of Hamiltonian dynamics, the calculus of variations (Aubry–Mather and weak-KAM theory), and geometric/topological dynamics. Two schools have driven the modern progress: the **variational school** descending from Mather — Cheng and Yan in Nanjing, Bernard in Paris, and Kaloshin and Zhang — whose tools are minimizing measures, Mañé/Aubry sets, and pseudographs; and the **geometric school** around Delshams, de la Llave, and Seara, centered on Barcelona and Atlanta, whose tools are normally hyperbolic invariant manifolds, scattering maps, and (with Gidea, Capiński, and Zgliczyński) computer-assisted shadowing. Marco, Sauzin, Lochak, Berti, Biasco, and Bolle contributed key quantitative diffusion-time results; Treschev supplied separatrix-map techniques; Xia and Moeckel brought celestial-mechanics perspectives. The field is small but intensely collaborative, propelled by Arnold's own problem lists and by regular activity at IHÉS, IHP, the Fields Institute, and Maryland/ICTP programs. Its current energy is directed at pushing beyond two and a half degrees of freedom, replacing topological genericity with measure-theoretic prevalence, and confronting the analytic category — each regarded as substantially harder than the case now understood.
