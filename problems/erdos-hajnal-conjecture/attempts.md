# Attempts — The Erdős–Hajnal Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

## The original quantitative near-miss (1989)

Erdős and Hajnal themselves proved the strongest *general* statement that holds unconditionally for over three decades: every $n$-vertex $H$-free graph has a homogeneous set of size at least $\exp\big(c(H)\sqrt{\log n}\big)$. This is a genuine improvement over the $\Theta(\log n)$ of the unrestricted case, and it is "almost" the conjecture in the sense that the exponent is the square root of what would give a polynomial. Closing the gap between $\sqrt{\log n}$ in the exponent and $\log n$ (which would yield $n^c$) is the entire problem; no general method has crossed it.

## Substitution and cographs (Alon–Pach–Solymosi, 2001)

A celebrated partial success: the EH property is closed under vertex substitution, which settles the conjecture for every cograph ($P_4$-free graph) and, combined with verified prime seeds, for infinite families. This is not a near-miss but a definitive resolution of a large, structured slice of the problem — and it sharply isolated the prime-graph case as the locus of all remaining difficulty.

## The bull (Chudnovsky–Safra, 2015)

The first "interesting" prime graph beyond size 4 to be settled. The bull (a triangle with two pendant vertices) was resolved by a structural decomposition argument. It demonstrated that explicit case analysis can defeat individual primes, but the proof's specificity gave little hope of a uniform method.

## The five-cycle (Bucić–Nguyen–Scott–Seymour, 2023)

The most significant near-frontier result. $C_5$ had been the canonical open test case for decades (it is the smallest prime graph that is not a cograph and not previously settled). Its resolution, with a polynomial bound, is celebrated as a true breakthrough and validated the Nguyen–Scott–Seymour machinery. Crucially, it did *not* resolve $P_5$ or general $H$, so the conjecture remains wide open — but it ended a long stalemate.

## The general lower-bound improvement (Nguyen–Scott–Seymour, 2022–2023)

For thirty-three years the $\exp(c\sqrt{\log n})$ bound stood untouched. The improvement to $\exp\big(c\sqrt{\log n\,\log\log n}\big)$ is the first asymptotic advance on the universal bound. It is a near-miss of a different flavor: it shows the 1989 barrier is not absolute, while underscoring how far subpolynomial bounds remain from the target.

## Disputed / retracted claims

The Erdős–Hajnal conjecture has a relatively clean record: there is **no widely circulated, peer-reviewed claimed full proof that was subsequently retracted**. Like many famous open problems it has attracted informal or preprint "solutions" of low circulation, but none reached consensus scrutiny or publication in a leading venue, and none is regarded by the community as a serious attempt requiring formal rebuttal. Accordingly, this dossier records no specific disputed proof with a documented objection. Readers should treat any announcement of a complete resolution with appropriate caution and look for verification by the structural-graph-theory community (e.g. the Scott–Seymour circle, Chudnovsky, Fox–Sudakov) before regarding it as established. This neutrality reflects the present state of the record, not a judgment that such an attempt could not appear.
