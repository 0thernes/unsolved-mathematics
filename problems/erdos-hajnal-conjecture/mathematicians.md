# Top Mathematicians — The Erdős–Hajnal Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Paul Erdős | 1913–1996 | originator | Co-posed the conjecture (1989); the $\exp(c\sqrt{\log n})$ general bound; founder of the probabilistic method underpinning the logarithmic baseline. |
| 2 | András Hajnal | 1931–2016 | originator | Co-posed the conjecture; partition calculus and the structural Ramsey framework in which it lives. |
| 3 | Noga Alon | contemporary | breakthrough | With Pach and Solymosi, proved the EH property is preserved under substitution, settling all cographs. |
| 4 | János Pach | contemporary | breakthrough | Co-author of the substitution theorem and of bipartite/geometric EH-type results. |
| 5 | József Solymosi | contemporary | breakthrough | Co-author of the 2001 substitution closure theorem. |

## Notes

The active community is concentrated around structural and extremal graph theory. The Princeton/Oxford circle of **Maria Chudnovsky, Paul Seymour, Alex Scott, Tung Nguyen, Sophie Spirkl** and collaborators (including **Matija Bucić**) has produced the recent landmark results — the loglog improvement to the general bound and the resolution of $C_5$. A complementary thread led by **Jacob Fox and Benny Sudakov** supplies the density, dependent-random-choice, and bi-clique reformulations, with **Maria Axenovich, David Conlon, Andrew Suk** and others extending EH-type phenomena to hypergraphs, ordered graphs, and tournaments. The **Bonnet–Thomassé** twin-width school connects EH to algorithmic tameness and model-theoretic NIP regularity. The originators' Hungarian school (via Hajnal's students and Erdős's vast collaboration network) remains the historical spine. Progress is currently rapid by the standards of the problem, but each genuine new case still demands bespoke structural arguments.
