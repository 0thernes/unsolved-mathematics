# Approaches — The Erdős–Hajnal Conjecture

_Major strategies, partial results, and barriers._

## Substitution and closure operations

**Core idea.** Build the class of EH graphs from a few base cases using operations that preserve the property. The foundational result (Alon, Pach, Solymosi 2001) is that if $H$ is obtained from $H_1$ by *substituting* a graph $H_2$ for a vertex, and both $H_1, H_2$ have the EH property, then so does $H$. This immediately yields the full conjecture for *cographs* — the closure of $K_1$ under disjoint union and join — since these are exactly the $P_4$-free graphs.

**Best result reached.** All graphs on $\le 4$ vertices, all cographs, and large families generated by substitution from verified prime "seeds" (including, post-2023, those built from $C_5$).

**Barrier.** Substitution cannot reach *prime* graphs (those with no nontrivial modular decomposition). The conjecture's true difficulty lives entirely in prime graphs, and substitution is silent there. Every new genuine case requires settling a prime seed by other means.

## Structural / explicit case analysis

**Core idea.** For a specific small $H$, prove EH directly by exploiting the structure forced on $H$-free graphs, often via a "polynomial Rödl" step: find a linear-size induced subgraph that is very dense or very sparse, then recurse.

**Best result reached.** The "bull" graph (Chudnovsky–Safra 2015), and decisively the five-cycle $C_5$ (Bucić, Nguyen, Scott, Seymour 2023), the first prime non-cograph beyond size 4 to fall, with a genuine polynomial bound $n^{c}$.

**Barrier.** These proofs are intricate and graph-specific; they do not obviously generalize. $P_5$, the seemingly "next" path, remains open despite enormous effort, showing the methods are not yet uniform.

## Pseudorandomness and density (Fox–Sudakov)

**Core idea.** Reformulate EH in terms of bi-cliques: $H$-free graphs should contain large complete or empty bipartite pairs. Using dependent random choice and regularity-type arguments, one shows that $H$-free graphs are "far from pseudorandom," which can be leveraged toward order.

**Best result reached.** Equivalences among the clique/stable-set, bi-clique, and polynomial Rödl formulations; quantitatively improved bounds in several special structured cases; tight understanding of when a single regularity step suffices.

**Barrier.** Regularity-based arguments incur tower-type or at best $\exp(c\sqrt{\log n})$ losses, which is exactly the wall the conjecture asks to break. The "polynomial Rödl property" is itself open in general and is essentially equivalent in difficulty.

## Iterated sparsification and the Nguyen–Scott–Seymour program

**Core idea.** Combine tree-decomposition and "asymptotic dimension"-style structure theory for induced subgraphs with careful counting to extract polynomially large homogeneous sets. This program also targets the *general* lower bound, not just special $H$.

**Best result reached.** The first asymptotic improvement on the 1989 universal bound: every $n$-vertex $H$-free graph has a homogeneous set of size $\exp\big(c\sqrt{\log n \log\log n}\big)$ (Nguyen–Scott–Seymour, 2022–2023). New infinite families and the $C_5$ breakthrough emerged from the same toolkit.

**Barrier.** The improvement is only by a $\sqrt{\log\log n}$ factor in the exponent — still subpolynomial. Pushing from $\exp(c\sqrt{\log n \log\log n})$ to $n^{c}$ for *all* $H$ appears to require a genuinely new idea, not a refinement.

## VC-dimension and model-theoretic tameness

**Core idea.** $H$-free graph classes have bounded VC-dimension (they are NIP / "tame"), which yields strong regularity (Alon–Fischer–Newman, Lovász–Szegedy) with polynomial parameters. One hopes tameness forces polynomial homogeneity.

**Best result reached.** Strong stable/NIP regularity lemmas; the *stable* EH theorem — for classes that are stable (forbidding a half-graph / order property), homogeneous sets of *linear* size exist (a clean unconditional success). This places EH in a broader model-theoretic landscape.

**Barrier.** Forbidding a single induced $H$ gives NIP but not stability; the unstable (order-bearing) part is precisely where polynomial bounds are not known. The half-graph is the canonical obstruction.

## Negative and barrier results

- **No subexponential general bound from current methods.** Every general technique to date is stuck at the $\exp(c\sqrt{\log n\,\cdot\,\mathrm{poly}\log\log n})$ ceiling; there is no theorem ruling out the conjecture, but also no method that crosses into $n^{o(1)}\cdot\,$polynomial territory uniformly.
- **Tightness of constants.** For specific $H$ the exponent $c(H)$ cannot exceed simple bounds; e.g. random-like $H$-free constructions show $c(H)$ must shrink, so a uniform constant independent of $H$ is impossible.
- **Substitution barrier.** As noted, the prime-graph case is provably untouched by the only operation known to preserve EH in general — a structural obstruction explaining why progress is graph-by-graph.
- **Regularity wall.** Any proof routed through Szemerédi-type regularity inherits its quantitative losses; a polynomial bound likely demands an essentially regularity-free argument, a recognized methodological barrier.
