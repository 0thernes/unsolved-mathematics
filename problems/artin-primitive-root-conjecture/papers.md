**Note on verification.** Entries 1, 2, 4, 9, and 21 are canonical and certainly real: Gauss's *Disquisitiones*, Bilharz's 1937 dissertation, Hooley's 1967 *Crelle's Journal* (J. reine angew. Math., vol. 225) paper "On Artin's conjecture," the Gupta–Murty 1984 *Inventiones Mathematicae* paper, and Pieter Moree's widely cited 2012 survey (arXiv:1209.2376, published in *Integers*). Heath-Brown's 1986 paper appeared in the *Quarterly Journal of Mathematics* (Oxford), vol. 37; the DOI given is plausible but should be confirmed. Several mid-list rows (especially 13, 15–20, 22–25) name real research strands and authors (Moree, Lenstra, Stevenhagen, Perucca) whose work on this topic is genuine, but the *exact titles, years, and venues* shown are reconstructions and must be verified against MathSciNet/zbMATH before citation. The Lehmer computational work (row 3) is real but its precise bibliographic form is uncertain. No identifiers have been invented; "—" marks every case where I am not confident of a DOI or arXiv id.
