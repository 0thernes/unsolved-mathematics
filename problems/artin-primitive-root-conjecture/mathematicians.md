# Top Mathematicians — Artin's Primitive Root Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Emil Artin | 1898–1962 | originator | Posed the conjecture (qualitative and density form) in 1927; supplied the Galois-theoretic framework via Kummer extensions. |
| 2 | Carl Friedrich Gauss | 1777–1855 | originator | *Disquisitiones Arithmeticae* (1801): theory of primitive roots and the period of $1/p$; the prehistory of the problem (case $a=10$). |
| 3 | Helmut Hasse | 1898–1979 | expositor | Recorded Artin's 1927 formulation; shaped the class-field-theoretic context and mentored Bilharz. |
| 4 | Herbert Bilharz | 1910s–? | breakthrough | Proved the function-field analogue (1937), conditional on RH for curves — the first theorem in the circle. |
| 5 | D. H. Lehmer & Emma Lehmer | 20th c. | breakthrough | 1957 computations exposing the density discrepancy, forcing the correction factor. (Counted as one entry.) |
| 6 | Christopher Hooley | 1928–2018 | breakthrough | 1967 proof of the full conjecture under GRH — the definitive conditional result and benchmark constant. |
| 7 | M. Ram Murty | b. 1953 | breakthrough | With Gupta (1984), first major unconditional progress via sieves; prolific surveys and elliptic analogues. |
| 8 | D. R. Heath-Brown | b. 1952 | breakthrough | 1986: at most two prime exceptions; the strongest unconditional theorem to date. |
| 9 | Pieter Moree | b. 1965 | frontier | Definitive surveys; systematic computation of corrected constants and variants; central organizer of the field. |
| 10 | H. W. Lenstra & P. Stevenhagen | contemporary | frontier | Conceptual "entanglement of Kummer extensions" framework explaining the correction factor uniformly. (Counted as one entry.) |

## Notes

The contemporary active community is small but vigorous, centered on analytic and algebraic number theorists who study **multiplicative orders, Kummer theory, and reductions of algebraic numbers**. **Pieter Moree** (Max Planck Institute for Mathematics, Bonn) is the field's chronicler and a constant source of refinements and variants. The **Lenstra–Stevenhagen** entanglement program (Leiden) gives the modern structural understanding of the correction constants, and **Antonella Perucca** (Luxembourg) and collaborators (e.g. Sgobba) have pushed the Kummer-theoretic and "reductions" machinery in recent years. **M. Ram Murty** (Queen's University) and his school continue work on sieve methods and elliptic-curve analogues (the Lang–Trotter and elliptic Artin problems). The unifying theme remains that every individual case waits on GRH, so much current effort targets *unconditional partial results*, *generalizations* (number fields, function fields, matrices, abelian varieties), and *precise constant computation* rather than the (apparently GRH-hard) original statement.
