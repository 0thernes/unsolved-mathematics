# Top Mathematicians — Polignac's Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Alphonse de Polignac | 19th c. | originator | Posed the conjecture (1849): every even number is a gap between consecutive primes infinitely often. |
| 2 | Viggo Brun | early 20th c. | breakthrough | Created sieve theory; proved $\sum 1/p$ over twin primes converges (Brun's constant), the first analytic handle. |
| 3 | G. H. Hardy | early 20th c. | breakthrough | With Littlewood, the $k$-tuple conjecture (1923) giving conjectural densities implying Polignac. |
| 4 | J. E. Littlewood | early 20th c. | breakthrough | Co-author of the $k$-tuple conjecture and the circle-method program for additive prime problems. |
| 5 | Atle Selberg | mid 20th c. | breakthrough | The Selberg sieve and articulation of the parity barrier limiting sieve lower bounds. |
| 6 | Chen Jingrun | mid–late 20th c. | breakthrough | Proved infinitely many $p$ with $p+2$ prime or semiprime (1973), the strongest near-twin result. |
| 7 | Daniel Goldston | modern | breakthrough | Co-creator of the GPY method proving gaps infinitely often far below average. |
| 8 | János Pintz | modern | breakthrough | Co-creator of GPY; extensive work on small gaps and the structure of prime gaps. |
| 9 | Yitang Zhang | modern | breakthrough | Proved the first unconditional bounded gap ($<7\times10^7$), a weak form of Polignac (2013). |
| 10 | James Maynard | frontier | frontier | Multidimensional sieve giving bounded gaps $\le 600$ (later 246 via Polymath) and many primes in bounded intervals; Fields Medal 2022. |

## Notes

The active community is concentrated in analytic number theory, with the post-2013 "bounded gaps" program as its organizing center. Cem Yıldırım (the third name of GPY) and Terence Tao—whose independent multidimensional sieve and coordination of the Polymath8 collaborations were decisive—are central figures alongside those tabled above; Tao would belong in any top-ten by influence and is omitted here only to surface the chronological originators. Kannan Soundararajan and Andrew Granville have written the standard expositions and surveys that frame the problem for the wider field. Current frontier work pushes in two directions: lowering the unconditional gap below 246 (which stalls against the level-of-distribution barrier), and attacking the parity barrier that separates every existing result from a specific gap such as $k=2$. The field remains highly active, with new sieve refinements, results on chains of primes and large gaps (Ford–Green–Konyagin–Maynard–Tao), and ongoing conditional improvements under Elliott–Halberstam.
