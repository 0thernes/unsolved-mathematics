# Top Mathematicians — The Cherlin–Zilber Algebraicity Conjecture

|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Gregory Cherlin | 1979–present | originator | "Groups of small Morley rank" (1979); isolated the algebraicity question; co-author of the even-type classification. |

## Notes

The community working on the Cherlin–Zilber conjecture is small, tightly knit, and centered on the model theory of groups. Its institutional hubs have included Rutgers (Cherlin), Oxford (Zilber), Manchester (Borovik), Lyon (Altınel, Poizat, Martin-Pizarro), and Paris/Sorbonne (Deloro, and historically Jaligot, who died in 2013 and is widely commemorated in the field). Beyond the named ten, important contributors include Adrien Deloro (involutions and small odd-type identification), Eric Jaligot (Carter subgroups, genericity), Frank Wagner, Martin Hils, Amador Martin-Pizarro, and Andreas Baudisch (the bad-fields construction), and Pınar Uğurlu, Joshua Wiscons, and others on representation- and action-theoretic refinements. The work proceeds largely through the careful porting of finite-group-theory techniques — Sylow theory, signalizer functors, genericity — into the rank setting, and through the model-theoretic study of Hrushovski-type constructions that police the boundary of what counts as a possible counterexample. Activity remains steady, concentrated now on odd-type identification theorems and the persistent mystery of degenerate type.
