# Attempts — The Cherlin–Zilber Algebraicity Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

The conjecture has not been the subject of high-profile *claimed* full proofs followed by public collapse; its difficulty is instead recorded in a series of partial completions and in the discovery that certain "soft" routes are provably blocked. The honest picture is one of steady, hard-won fragments rather than dramatic near-misses.

### Even type: a genuine completion

The single largest realized chunk of the conjecture is the even-type classification. Altınel, Borovik, and Cherlin's 2008 monograph "Simple Groups of Finite Morley Rank" (AMS Surveys and Monographs 145) is the culmination of two decades of effort by many authors and is broadly accepted in the model-theory community. It is a near-miss only in the sense that it solves one of four cases; within its scope it is a theorem, not a claim. Mixed type was likewise eliminated, so the live conjecture concerns odd and degenerate types.

### The Trichotomy Conjecture and Hrushovski's constructions

Zilber's broader **Trichotomy Conjecture** for strongly minimal sets — that their geometry is disintegrated, group-like, or field-like — was *refuted* by Ehud Hrushovski's amalgamation method (1993), which builds "new" strongly minimal sets with no definable group at all. This was a cautionary near-miss for the surrounding philosophy: it showed that the fMr universe contains structures with no algebraic-geometric coordinates. Crucially, Hrushovski's counterexamples are *not* counterexamples to the Algebraicity Conjecture for simple groups — but they demonstrated that one cannot expect a purely geometric proof, and they seeded the techniques later used against the conjecture's edges.

### Bad fields: a barrier realized, not a disproof

A pivotal "attempt-adjacent" result is the **construction of bad fields** of characteristic $0$ by Baudisch, Hils, Martin-Pizarro, and Wagner (2009), again via Hrushovski amalgamation, conditional on a number-theoretic statement following from Schanuel-type conjectures. This did not refute the Algebraicity Conjecture — bad fields are not simple groups — but it killed the hope of disposing of bad multiplicative subgroups for free, showing the universe of fMr structures is strictly larger than the algebraic one. It stands as the most important demonstration of what *cannot* be assumed.

### Bad groups: low-rank cases settled negatively (good news)

The feared simplest counterexample — a **bad group** of small rank — has been progressively excluded. Cherlin's original 1979 analysis already constrained ranks $1$–$3$. The capstone is **Olivier Frécon's 2018 theorem** that there is **no bad group of Morley rank $3$** (Journal of the European Mathematical Society / Annals of Mathematics; the result was announced and circulated around 2017–2018), closing the smallest open instance and confirming the rank-$3$ case of the conjecture. No retraction attaches to this; it is a clean positive step.

### Degenerate type

Borovik, Burdges, and Cherlin showed connected degenerate-type groups have no involutions, eliminating a class of feared configurations. This is a partial result, not a resolution: whether *any* infinite simple degenerate-type fMr group exists remains the principal open problem, and no convincing candidate (nor proof of impossibility) is known.

In sum, the literature contains no widely circulated *erroneous* claimed proof of the full conjecture; the record is one of legitimate partial theorems and of barrier results (bad fields, Hrushovski constructions) clarifying that the remaining cases are genuinely hard.
