# Status & Frontier — The Cherlin–Zilber Algebraicity Conjecture

_Where the problem stands and what a resolution would require._

**Status: active progress, open.** The conjecture — every infinite simple group of finite Morley rank is an algebraic group over an algebraically closed field — is unproved in general. It is, however, far from untouched: an entire structural case has been settled unconditionally.

### What is known (unconditional)

- **Even type is fully classified.** Altınel, Borovik, and Cherlin (2008), *Simple Groups of Finite Morley Rank* (AMS Surveys and Monographs 145), prove that a simple fMr group whose Sylow $2$-subgroup has nontrivial connected $2$-unipotent part is a Chevalley group over an algebraically closed field of characteristic $2$.
- **Mixed type is eliminated** — no simple fMr group has Sylow $2$-subgroup mixing a $2$-torus and a $2$-unipotent part.
- **Low rank is verified.** Connected fMr groups of small Morley rank are algebraic (Cherlin 1979 for ranks $\le 3$ in the relevant cases), and **Frécon (2018)** proved there is **no bad group of Morley rank $3$**, confirming the rank-$3$ instance and removing the simplest feared counterexample.
- **Degenerate-type groups have no involutions** (Borovik–Burdges–Cherlin), constraining but not excluding them.
- A mature toolkit exists: Sylow theory, decent/good tori, Carter subgroups (Frécon–Jaligot), unipotence parameters (Burdges), Zilber's field theorem and indecomposability theorem.

### What is known (conditional)

Under **tameness** hypotheses (no bad fields interpretable) and genericity assumptions, many odd-type configurations — especially minimal connected simple groups of small Prüfer $2$-rank — are identified as $\mathrm{PSL}_2$ or related Chevalley groups. These results are conditional precisely because bad fields are now known to be possible.

### What a full resolution requires

1. **Odd type in full generality**, including unbounded Prüfer rank, via uniform identification theorems.
2. **Degenerate type**: prove no infinite simple fMr group with trivial Sylow $2$-subgroup exists — the hardest piece, with no finite-group analogue (every finite simple group has even order).
3. **Bad groups and bad fields**: rule out simple bad groups and resolve the role of bad fields. The 2009 construction of bad fields of characteristic $0$ by Baudisch–Hils–Martin-Pizarro–Wagner (conditional on a Schanuel-type number-theoretic statement) shows the fMr universe strictly exceeds the algebraic one, so no purely soft argument can succeed.

### Plausible routes

- Complete the Borovik program by finishing odd-type identification and then attacking degenerate type with new genericity or definable-amenability ideas.
- Develop intrinsic obstructions to bad simple groups (extending Frécon's rank-$3$ method to higher rank).
- Import further finite-group machinery (amalgam method, fusion systems analogues) into the rank category.
