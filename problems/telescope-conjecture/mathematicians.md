## Notes

The telescope conjecture sits at the heart of an unusually active and tightly knit community: **chromatic homotopy theory** and its modern offshoot, **trace methods / topological cyclic homology**. The originating generation — Ravenel, Bousfield, Miller, Wilson, Hopkins, Smith — built the chromatic filtration, the nilpotence and periodicity theorems, and the localization theory that gave the conjecture meaning. The computational generation, led by **Mahowald, Ravenel, and Paul Shick** (and informed by **Mark Behrens**, **Paul Goerss**, and the $\mathrm{TMF}$ school around **Hopkins–Miller**), spent decades probing height 2 without a verdict. The resolution came from a younger cohort fluent in both chromatic and $K$-theoretic methods: **Burklund, Hahn, Levy, Schlank**, together with collaborators **Allen Yuan**, **Arpon Raksit**, and **Dylan Wilson**, whose redshift and chromatic-Nullstellensatz results supplied the decisive tools. The community is concentrated at MIT, Copenhagen, Harvard, Northwestern, and Bonn, communicates through arXiv preprints and Oberwolfach/MSRI workshops, and has rapidly turned from the disproof to its quantitative aftermath — measuring how, and how badly, telescopic homotopy exceeds its $K(n)$-local shadow.
