**Note on shortfall and reliability.** Only a handful of works on this problem are truly canonical: the 1960 Erdős–Rado founding paper (#1), Kostochka's 1997 improvement (#11), the 2019 ALWZ breakthrough (#18, arXiv:1908.08483), Rao's "Coding for sunflowers" (#19, arXiv:1909.04774), Tao's expository treatment (#20), and Bell–Chueluecha–Warnke "Note on sunflowers" (#22, arXiv:2105.06561) — these carry **high-confidence** flags. Several rows (e.g. #4, #6, #7, #9, #14, #17, #23, #25) are **plausible leads whose exact titles, authorship, or existence the author could not verify**; they are flagged **ai-suggested** and should be checked before citation. The Park–Pham threshold work (#24) is real and closely related technology but is not strictly a sunflower paper. Identifiers are given only where confidence is reasonable; all others are "—" rather than fabricated. The genuine literature dedicated specifically to this conjecture is small, so the list mixes core sunflower papers with adjacent extremal-set-theory and spread-method works to reach 25 entries honestly.
