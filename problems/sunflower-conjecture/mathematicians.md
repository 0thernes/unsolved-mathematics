# Top Mathematicians — The Sunflower Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Paul Erdős | 1913–1996 | originator | Co-introduced sunflowers (1960); posed the conjecture; offered \$1000 for the $k=3$ case and publicised it for decades. |
| 2 | Richard Rado | 1906–1989 | originator | Co-author of the 1960 founding paper; supplied the partition/intersection-theorem framing within which the problem sits. |
| 3 | Alexandr V. Kostochka | 20th–21st c. | breakthrough | Best classical-era bound (1997): $f(w,3) \le c\,w!\,(\log\log\log w/\log\log w)^w$, the standing record until 2019. |
| 4 | Péter Frankl | 20th–21st c. | expositor | Extensive work on $\Delta$-systems and extremal set theory (with Deza, Füredi); shaped the surrounding theory and surveys. |
| 5 | Shachar Lovett | 21st c. | breakthrough | Co-author of ALWZ (2019); central to the spread-method breakthrough and its complexity-theoretic context. |
| 6 | Ryan Alweiss | 21st c. | breakthrough | Co-author of ALWZ (2019); the randomized sampling/spread argument collapsing the bound to polylog base. |
| 7 | Kewen Wu | 21st c. | breakthrough | Co-author of ALWZ (2019); contributed to the spread-to-sunflower analysis. |
| 8 | Jiapeng Zhang | 21st c. | breakthrough | Co-author of ALWZ (2019) and of earlier quasi-sunflower / DNF-sparsification work linking sunflowers to complexity. |
| 9 | Anup Rao | 21st c. | frontier | "Coding for sunflowers" (2020): entropy/encoding reformulation giving the clean $(Ck\log w)^w$ bound; influential survey. |
| 10 | Terence Tao | 21st c. | expositor | Canonical expository account of the ALWZ/Rao spread lemma; helped disseminate and clarify the method to a wide audience. |

## Notes

The active community around the sunflower conjecture sits at the intersection of **extremal combinatorics**, **theoretical computer science**, and **probabilistic combinatorics**, and is small but highly visible. The 2019–2021 breakthrough was driven by researchers (Lovett, Zhang, Rao, Alweiss, Wu) whose home base is as much in complexity theory as in combinatorics, reflecting how the spread/encoding machinery cross-pollinated with the **Kahn–Kalai threshold program** (Park, Pham, Frankston, Kahn). Refinement of constants and the refined sunflower lemma have been pursued by **Tolson Bell, Suchakree Chueluecha, and Lutz Warnke**, among others. The current frontier — removing the residual $\log w$ to reach the genuine $C(k)^w$ — is regarded as requiring a new structural idea, and is an open target for both the combinatorics and the TCS communities; expository work by Tao and surveys by Rao keep the problem accessible to newcomers.
