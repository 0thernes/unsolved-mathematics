**Note on completeness and honesty.** The truly canonical anchors of this subject — Iwasawa's $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension papers (#1, #3), Greenberg's 1976 conjecture paper (#4), Ferrero–Washington (#5), Mazur–Wiles (#9), and Washington's textbook *Introduction to Cyclotomic Fields* (#6) — are marked high-confidence; their existence and attribution are not in doubt, though I have deliberately omitted DOIs/volume numbers I cannot certify. Rows for the Fukuda, Komatsu, Ozaki, Taya, Ichimura, Sumida, and Kraft–Schoof papers reflect a real and substantial literature of partial results and computations on Greenberg's Conjecture, but the exact titles, years, and journals given here should be verified against MathSciNet/zbMATH before citation — hence "needs-verification" or "ai-suggested." I have intentionally provided no fabricated DOI or arXiv identifiers; a real paper with "—" is preferable to an invented number. Where a row is generic ("various"), it stands in for a known cluster of work rather than a single confirmed paper.
