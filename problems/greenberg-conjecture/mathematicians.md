# Top Mathematicians — Greenberg's Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Kenkichi Iwasawa | 1959–1990s | originator | Created Iwasawa theory; the growth formula and $\lambda, \mu, \nu$ invariants that the conjecture concerns; Greenberg's doctoral advisor. |
| 2 | Ralph Greenberg | 1976–present | originator | Posed the conjecture (1976) for totally real fields; gave the first criteria via units and $p$-adic $L$-values; shaped modern algebraic Iwasawa theory. |
| 3 | Bruce Ferrero | 1979 | breakthrough | With Washington, proved $\mu_p = 0$ for all abelian fields, reducing the abelian conjecture to $\lambda_p = 0$. |
| 4 | Lawrence C. Washington | 1979–present | breakthrough | Co-author of the $\mu = 0$ theorem; author of the standard textbook *Introduction to Cyclotomic Fields*; broad influence on the field. |
| 5 | Barry Mazur | 1984 | breakthrough | With Wiles, proved the Iwasawa Main Conjecture over $\mathbb{Q}$, giving the analytic interpretation of $\lambda$ in terms of $p$-adic $L$-functions. |
| 6 | Andrew Wiles | 1984 | breakthrough | Co-author of the Main Conjecture, foundational to the $L$-function-theoretic reformulation of $\lambda = 0$. |
| 7 | Takashi Fukuda | 1980s–present | frontier | Effective stabilization criterion for finiteness of $X_\infty$; extensive numerical and theoretical verification (often with Komatsu). |
| 8 | Manabu Ozaki | 1990s–present | frontier | Deep $\Lambda$-module-structure results; constructions of real fields with prescribed Iwasawa module structure clarifying obstructions. |
| 9 | Hiroki Sumida (Sumida-Takahashi) | 1990s–present | frontier | Algorithms and theorems on $\lambda$-invariants of real quadratic fields (often with Ichimura). |
| 10 | Humio Ichimura | 1990s–present | frontier | Numerous criteria and computations for Greenberg's Conjecture via units, regulators, and cyclotomic-unit indices. |

## Notes

The community around Greenberg's Conjecture sits squarely within Iwasawa theory and computational algebraic number theory, and it remains active. Its character is distinctive: progress comes less from single dramatic theorems than from a steady interplay between structural results (module theory of $X_\infty$, control theorems, the Main Conjecture) and rigorous computation (Fukuda's stabilization criterion turning an infinite question into a finite check). A strong Japanese school — Fukuda, Komatsu, Ozaki, Taya, Itoh, Ichimura, Sumida-Takahashi, Nishino — has driven much of the partial-result and verification literature, alongside North American and European contributors in the broader Iwasawa community (Greenberg, Washington, and the schools around Coates, Sharifi, and others working on related Main Conjectures, Gross–Kuz'min, and $p$-adic regulators). The conjecture is frequently discussed at Iwasawa-theory conferences and in survey volumes, where it is treated as a benchmark open problem whose eventual resolution is expected to require new control over non-vanishing of $p$-adic $L$-functions or regulators rather than purely algebraic input.
