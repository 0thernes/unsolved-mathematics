## Notes

The active community spans several overlapping camps. The **constructive / renormalization-group** tradition (descended from Glimm, Jaffe, Bałaban, and Rivasseau, with Vincent Rivasseau, Jacques Magnen, and Jonathan Dimock among contributors) continues to pursue the Euclidean-measure construction. A vigorous **probabilistic / stochastic-quantization** front has emerged around Martin Hairer's regularity structures, with Ajay Chandra, Ilya Chevyrev, Hao Shen, and Rongchan/Xiangchan Zhu constructing the 2D and 3D Yang–Mills measures and studying their Langevin dynamics; Sourav Chatterjee and Scott Sheffield contribute lattice and large-$N$ analysis, and Thierry Lévy and Bruce Driver underpin the 2D theory. On the **physics-rigor interface**, Edward Witten, Nathan Seiberg, and the lattice-QCD community (e.g. Morningstar, Peardon, and the large simulation collaborations) supply the expected spectrum and confinement picture that any proof must reproduce. The four-dimensional construction with a mass gap remains the shared target; the probabilistic methods are widely viewed as the most active current avenue, though the critical dimension $d=4$ is not yet within their reach.
