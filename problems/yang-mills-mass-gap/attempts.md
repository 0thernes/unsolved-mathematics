# Attempts — Yang–Mills Existence and Mass Gap

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

## Established partial results (genuine progress, not solutions)

- **Balaban's ultraviolet stability (1984–1989).** A multi-paper renormalization-group tour de force proving uniform UV bounds for 4D lattice Yang–Mills in finite volume. Widely regarded as the deepest rigorous step toward existence, but it does not reach the infinite-volume continuum theory or the mass gap.
- **Magnen–Rivasseau–Sénéor (1993).** A phase-space multiscale construction of 4D Yang–Mills in finite volume with a running coupling, complementary to Balaban. Same limitation: finite volume only.
- **Strong-coupling lattice gap (Osterwalder–Seiler, 1978).** A rigorous mass gap and confinement at strong coupling on the lattice via convergent expansions — but the continuum limit lives at weak coupling, so this is not the physical regime.
- **2D and 3D constructions.** Two-dimensional Yang–Mills is essentially solvable (Driver, Sengupta, Lévy; Witten's exact partition functions). Chandra–Chevyrev–Hairer–Shen (2020–2022) rigorously built the 2D and 3D Yang–Mills measures via stochastic quantization. These are real existence results in lower dimensions; the Clay problem is specifically $d=4$.
- **Chatterjee (2015–2019).** Rigorous results on Wilson loop expectations, the $1/N$ limit, and lattice gauge theories, including a rigorous formulation of gauge–string duality at strong coupling.

## Near-miss expectations from physics

The mass gap is overwhelmingly supported by **lattice Monte-Carlo** computations of the glueball spectrum (e.g. Morningstar–Peardon, 1999) and by the success of asymptotic freedom and dimensional transmutation. These are not proofs: lattice results are numerical and rely on extrapolations, and no argument converts them into a theorem within the OS/Wightman framework.

## Disputed and unaccepted claimed proofs

Yang–Mills, as a famous Millennium Problem, has attracted numerous claimed solutions, essentially none of which are accepted by the mathematical-physics community. Representative cases (stated neutrally):

- **Dynin (Alexander Dynin), various preprints (2010s).** Claimed constructions of the quantized Yang–Mills energy-mass spectrum and a positive mass gap via a Hamiltonian/antinormal-symbol calculus. The work was not accepted: critics noted that the construction does not establish the required OS/Wightman axioms for a genuine relativistic QFT on $\mathbb{R}^4$ and that the spectral claims rest on formal manipulations rather than a controlled nonperturbative construction. No peer-reviewed consensus endorses it.
- **Various arXiv "solutions."** Periodic claimed proofs (using, e.g., specific gauge choices, BRST cohomology arguments, or formal path-integral manipulations) circulate; the recurring objection is that they either assume existence of the very measure that must be constructed, ignore renormalization and the continuum limit, or fail to verify reflection positivity. The Clay Institute does not maintain a list of refuted attempts, and none has cleared peer review with community acceptance.

A general caution applies: the **triviality of $\phi^4_4$**, proved rigorously by Aizenman (1981) and completed by Aizenman–Duminil-Copin (2021), shows that plausible four-dimensional field-theory constructions can collapse to a free (Gaussian) theory. Any claimed Yang–Mills construction must demonstrate non-triviality (a genuinely interacting theory) *and* a gap — a bar that no claimed proof to date has met to the community's satisfaction.

## Status of the problem

As of the present compilation the problem is **open**. No construction satisfying the Clay criteria — a four-dimensional quantum Yang–Mills theory for a compact simple group on $\mathbb{R}^4$ obeying the Wightman (or OS) axioms with a positive mass gap — has been produced and accepted.
