# Top Mathematicians — Hadwiger's Conjecture (Graph Coloring)

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Hugo Hadwiger | 1943 | originator | Stated the conjecture for all $t$ and proved the case $t=4$. |
| 2 | Klaus Wagner | 1937 | breakthrough | Structure theorem for $K_5$-minor-free graphs, equating the $t=5$ case with the Four Color Theorem. |
| 3 | Gabriel A. Dirac | 1952 | breakthrough | Independent proof of $t=4$; modern graph-theoretic framing that popularized the conjecture. |
| 4 | Wolfgang Mader | 1960s–70s | breakthrough | Edge-density bounds forcing complete minors, founding the extremal approach. |
| 5 | Alexander V. Kostochka | 1980s | breakthrough | $O(t\sqrt{\log t})$ average-degree bound forcing $K_t$ minors; the long-standing general coloring bound. |
| 6 | Andrew Thomason | 1984, 2001 | breakthrough | Independent extremal bound and exact extremal constant $c=0.6382\ldots$ for forcing $K_t$. |
| 7 | Neil Robertson | 1993 | breakthrough | Co-proved the $t=6$ case; co-architect of the Graph Minors structure theory. |
| 8 | Paul Seymour | 1993, 2005 | frontier | Co-proved $t=6$; fractional Hadwiger (with Reed); leading surveyor and problem-shaper. |
| 9 | Robin Thomas | 1993 | breakthrough | Co-proved the $t=6$ case via the apex reduction to the Four Color Theorem. |
| 10 | Luke Postle | 2019–present | frontier | With Norin and Song, broke the $\sqrt{\log t}$ barrier; with Delcourt drove the bound to $O(h\log\log h)$. |

## Notes

The historical spine runs Hadwiger → Wagner/Dirac → the extremal-density school (Mader, Kostochka, Thomason) → the structural triumvirate (Robertson, Seymour, Thomas) who settled $t=6$. The contemporary frontier is dominated by a closely connected group working on the *quantitative* Hadwiger problem: **Sergey Norin**, **Luke Postle**, **Zi-Xia Song**, and **Michelle Delcourt**, whose 2019–2021 papers ended a near-four-decade stall on the exponent and brought the general bound within a $\log\log$ factor of linear. Adjacent contributors include **Ken-ichi Kawarabayashi** and **Bjarne Toft** (the $K_7$/$K_{4,4}$ case and authoritative surveys), and the broader odd-minor and list-coloring communities (work associated with Geelen, Gerards, Reed, and others). Surveys by Toft and by Seymour remain the standard entry points, and the problem is a recurring fixture in extremal and structural graph-theory programs at venues such as the Graph Minors and probabilistic-combinatorics workshops. The community consensus is that the conjecture is true; effort concentrates on the Linear Hadwiger Conjecture and on the first open exact case, $t=7$.
