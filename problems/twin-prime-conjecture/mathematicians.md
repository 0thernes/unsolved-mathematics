# Top Mathematicians — The Twin Prime Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Alphonse de Polignac | 19th c. (1826–1863) | originator | Stated the 1849 conjecture on prime gaps $2k$; twin primes are the case $k=1$. |
| 2 | Viggo Brun | early 20th c. (1885–1978) | breakthrough | Invented the Brun sieve; proved convergence of $\sum 1/p$ over twins (Brun's constant) and the first upper bound $O(x/\ln^2 x)$. |
| 3 | G. H. Hardy | early 20th c. (1877–1947) | breakthrough | With Littlewood, gave the quantitative conjecture and twin prime constant $C_2$ via the circle method (1923). |
| 4 | J. E. Littlewood | early 20th c. (1885–1977) | breakthrough | Co-author of *Partitio Numerorum III*; co-founder of the $k$-tuple density heuristic. |
| 5 | Atle Selberg | 20th c. (1917–2007) | breakthrough | Developed the Selberg sieve and articulated the parity problem that obstructs sieves from reaching true primes. |
| 6 | Chen Jingrun | 20th c. (1933–1996) | breakthrough | Proved infinitely many primes $p$ with $p+2$ a $P_2$ — the strongest almost-twin result (1973). |
| 7 | Daniel Goldston | modern | frontier | With Pintz and Yıldırım, proved $\liminf (p_{n+1}-p_n)/\ln p_n = 0$ (GPY method). |
| 8 | János Pintz | modern | frontier | Co-architect of GPY; deep contributions to small-gap distribution and tuples. |
| 9 | Yitang Zhang | modern | breakthrough | Proved the first finite bound on prime gaps ($<7\times10^7$, 2013) via new equidistribution estimates. |
| 10 | James Maynard | contemporary | frontier | Multidimensional GPY sieve giving bounded gaps and $m$ primes in bounded intervals; Fields Medal 2022. |

Honorable mentions inseparable from the story: **Cem Yıldırım** (the third name in GPY); **Terence Tao** (independent multidimensional sieve and leader of the Polymath8 effort); **Enrico Bombieri, John Friedlander, and Henryk Iwaniec** (large-moduli distribution results underpinning Zhang); and **Erdős**, whose 1940 work launched the modern small-gaps line.

## Notes

The active community sits at the intersection of analytic number theory and sieve theory. Following the 2013–2014 revolution, momentum continues through **James Maynard** (Oxford, Fields Medal 2022) and **Terence Tao** (UCLA), whose Polymath8 project demonstrated large-scale open collaboration on the problem. **Kannan Soundararajan**, **Kaisa Matomäki**, **Ben Green**, **John Friedlander**, and **Henryk Iwaniec** remain central to the surrounding distribution-of-primes theory, and **Goldston** and **Pintz** continue work on gaps. The frontier effort is now less about lowering the constant $246$ (which is essentially optimized within current methods) and more about confronting the parity barrier directly — the genuine obstacle between bounded gaps and the twin prime conjecture itself.
