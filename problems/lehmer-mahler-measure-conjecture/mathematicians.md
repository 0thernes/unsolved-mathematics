# Top Mathematicians — Lehmer's Mahler Measure Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Derrick Henry Lehmer | 1905–1991 | originator | Posed the question (1933); exhibited $L(x)$ and Lehmer's number $\lambda_0 = 1.17628\ldots$. |
| 2 | Leopold Kronecker | 1823–1891 | originator | 1857 theorem: measure $1$ forces cyclotomic, fixing the baseline the conjecture starts from. |
| 3 | Kurt Mahler | 1903–1988 | originator | Introduced and systematized the Mahler measure in height theory; the measure bears his name. |
| 4 | Raphaël Salem | 1898–1963 | breakthrough | Theory of Salem and Pisot numbers, the structures realizing the smallest measures. |
| 5 | Christopher J. Smyth | 1944– | breakthrough | Proved Lehmer's conjecture for non-reciprocal polynomials (1971), bound $\theta_0 = 1.3247\ldots$. |
| 6 | Edward Dobrowolski | 20th–21st c. | breakthrough | The standard unconditional lower bound (1979), best general result to date. |
| 7 | David W. Boyd | 1941– | breakthrough | Salem-number framework, foundational computations and surveys on small measures. |
| 8 | Hugh L. Montgomery | 1944– | breakthrough | With Blanksby, the first explicit unconditional lower bound (1971). |
| 9 | Francesco Amoroso | 20th–21st c. | frontier | Degree-independent bounds in abelian extensions (with Dvornicich) and higher-dimensional Lehmer (with David). |
| 10 | Vesselin Dimitrov | 21st c. | frontier | Proved the sibling Schinzel–Zassenhaus conjecture (2019), reviving metric approaches to Lehmer. |

## Notes

The active community sits at the intersection of Diophantine geometry, analytic number theory, and explicit computation. **Francesco Amoroso**, often with **Roberto Dvornicich**, **Sinnou David**, and **Umberto Zannier**, drives the height-theoretic frontier — uniform bounds under Galois or field constraints and the $\mathbb{G}_m^n$ generalizations. **Vesselin Dimitrov** and **Philipp Habegger** brought new metric/equidistribution machinery that cracked Schinzel–Zassenhaus and is being probed for transfer to the measure problem. On the computational and structural side, **Michael Mossinghoff**, **Georges Rhin**, **Valérie Flammang**, and **Qiang Wu** maintain the exhaustive searches and trace-problem techniques that keep $\lambda_0$ the empirical champion. **Christopher Smyth** remains the field's principal expositor, his survey articles the standard entry points. The work of the elder figures (Lehmer, Boyd, Dobrowolski) is still cited in essentially every paper; the conjecture is small enough in statement, and resistant enough in practice, that the community is tightly knit and the open frontier is well-defined.
