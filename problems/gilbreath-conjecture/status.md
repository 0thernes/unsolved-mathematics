# Status & Frontier — Gilbreath's Conjecture

_Where the problem stands and what a resolution would require._

**Status: open.** The conjecture is believed true, is supported by overwhelming numerical evidence, and admits a clean structural reduction — yet no unconditional proof exists, and none appears within reach of current methods.

## What is known (unconditional)

- **Massive verification.** Andrew Odlyzko (1993) verified the leading-$1$ property for the difference triangle generated by all primes up to $10^{13}$ — over $3 \times 10^{11}$ primes — to depths vastly exceeding any plausible counterexample. No exception has ever been found.
- **A finite sufficiency criterion.** Odlyzko showed that the conjecture (up to a given prime bound) reduces to checking a *bounded-width, bounded-depth* condition on the difference triangle: once a row consists of $0$s and $2$s within a window and starts with $2$, the leading-$1$ property is self-propagating. This converts an infinite statement into a finite check on each range — but not across all ranges at once.
- **Generic-sequence theorems.** Cairns (2008), Stoll, and others proved that Gilbreath-type laws hold for wide classes of sequences whose gaps are bounded and equidistributed mod small powers of $2$. These establish that the phenomenon is *structural*, not special to the primes.

## What is known (conditional / heuristic)

- Under natural randomness models for the low-order binary digits of prime gaps, the Gilbreath law holds with probability $1$. The conjecture is therefore "true on heuristic grounds" with very high confidence.
- No nontrivial *conditional* theorem (e.g., "assume the Riemann Hypothesis, then Gilbreath follows") is known to close the problem; even strong hypotheses about prime gaps are not known to imply the required uniform mod-$2^k$ control.

## What a full resolution would require

A proof must establish that the $\{0,2\}$ structure of the difference rows, once it appears, **never breaks for arbitrarily large primes** — i.e., a *uniform* statement about the parity (mod $2$, then mod $4$, $8$, …) of prime gaps, holding for the entire infinite triangle simultaneously. This is precisely the territory of the **parity barrier**: sieve and elementary methods are structurally blind to the mod-$2$ information the conjecture depends on. Bridging that gap would likely demand genuinely new input on the fine arithmetic of prime gaps, beyond even the post–Zhang–Maynard–Tao bounded-gaps technology, which controls small gaps but not the parity structure of all gaps at once.

## Plausible routes

1. **Strengthen the reduction to a uniform mechanism.** Show that Odlyzko's finite window has *provably bounded width for all heights*, turning the per-range check into a single uniform argument. The empirical stability of the window is encouraging but unproven.
2. **Transfer generic-sequence results to the primes.** Prove the equidistribution-of-gaps hypotheses used by Cairns-type theorems *for the primes specifically* — itself a problem of comparable depth.
3. **New analytic control of gap parity.** Develop methods that see past the parity barrier for prime gaps, a long-shot but the only route that addresses the true obstruction.

The realistic assessment, echoing Erdős, is that the conjecture is essentially certainly true but that a proof is far off; the gap is not a missing trick but a missing theory of prime-gap parity.
