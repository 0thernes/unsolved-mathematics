# Top Mathematicians — The Andrews–Curtis Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | James J. Andrews | 1960s | originator | Co-posed the conjecture (1965); co-developed the free-group/handlebody framework linking presentations to $2$-complexes. |
| 2 | Morton L. Curtis | 1960s–1980s | originator | Co-posed the conjecture; Rice University topologist who connected balanced presentations of $\{1\}$ to $3$-deformations of $2$-complexes. |
| 3 | J. H. C. Whitehead | 1930s–1950s | originator | Founded simple-homotopy theory and the theory of $3$-deformations of $2$-complexes — the conceptual substrate of AC. |
| 4 | Selman Akbulut | 1980s–present | frontier | With Kirby, introduced the $AK(n)$ candidate counterexamples from $4$-manifold/Dehn-surgery topology. |
| 5 | Robion Kirby | 1980s–present | frontier | Co-creator of the Akbulut–Kirby presentations; broader influence via the Kirby calculus and $4$-manifold topology. |
| 6 | Charles F. Miller III | 1970s–present | frontier | With Schupp, produced influential balanced presentations of $\{1\}$ used as test cases for decades. |
| 7 | Paul E. Schupp | 1970s–2010s | frontier | Combinatorial group theorist; co-author of the Miller–Schupp family and of foundational text *Combinatorial Group Theory*. |
| 8 | Wolfgang Metzler | 1970s–2000s | expositor | Co-editor of *Two-Dimensional Homotopy and Combinatorial Group Theory*; organized the geometric theory around AC. |
| 9 | Martin R. Bridson | 2010s–present | breakthrough | Proved non-elementary lower bounds on trivialization length, showing true instances can be astronomically hard. |
| 10 | Alexei D. Miasnikov / Alexander Ushakov | 1999–present | frontier | Drove the computational program (genetic algorithms; large-scale search), culminating in the trivialization of $AK(2)$ and many Miller–Schupp presentations. |

## Notes

The current active community spans three overlapping camps. **Topologists** (in the lineage of Whitehead, Metzler, and the *Two-Dimensional Homotopy* school edited by Hog-Angeloni, Metzler, and Sieradski) maintain the geometric reformulation in terms of $2$-complexes, $3$-deformations, and the Zeeman conjecture. **Computational and combinatorial group theorists** — the Miasnikov/Myasnikov circle, Panteleev, Ushakov, and adjacent workers using GAP/KBMAG-style tools and term-rewriting (e.g. Lisitsa) — push the explicit-trivialization frontier and maintain the catalogue of open candidates. **Machine-learning researchers** (Shehper, Halverson, and collaborators since roughly 2021) have recast trivialization as a reinforcement-learning problem and absorbed large parts of the Miller–Schupp family. Bridson's hardness theorems anchor all three by bounding what any of these methods can hope to certify. The shared frontier today is concrete: settle $AK(3)$, and either prove the conjecture for a structurally complete class or exhibit the first genuine counterexample.
