# Attempts — The MLC Conjecture (Mandelbrot Locally Connected)

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

## Yoccoz's theorem (1990) — the decisive partial result

Jean-Christophe Yoccoz proved that $M$ is locally connected **at every parameter that is at most finitely renormalizable**, simultaneously establishing local connectivity of the corresponding Julia sets. The proof introduced the **Yoccoz puzzle** and **parapuzzle** and the principal-nest modulus estimates. Because non-renormalizable and finitely renormalizable parameters form a large, explicitly characterized set, this also yields **density of hyperbolicity** restricted to them. Yoccoz never published a full monograph; the argument circulated through Hubbard's exposition ("Local connectivity of Julia sets and bifurcation loci") and Milnor's accounts. It remains the benchmark against which all later work is measured, and it precisely isolates the infinitely renormalizable case as the hard residue.

## Real density of hyperbolicity (1997)

Two independent routes settled the **real** quadratic family. Graczyk–Świątek and, via renormalization, Lyubich proved that hyperbolic maps are dense among real $f_c$. This is the full real-line analogue of the principal consequence of MLC; it does **not** establish MLC itself, because MLC is a two-dimensional parameter-plane statement and real density does not control transverse (complex) directions. It is the most celebrated near-miss: the consequence is theorem, the conjecture is not.

## Lyubich's bounded-type MLC (1997)

Mikhail Lyubich proved MLC at **infinitely renormalizable parameters of bounded type** satisfying *a priori* bounds, using complex bounds and the hyperbolicity of the renormalization horseshoe (building on Sullivan and McMullen). This pushed local connectivity past the Yoccoz wall for an important infinite family but left unbounded/satellite types open.

## Kahn–Lyubich a priori bounds (mid-2000s)

Jeremy Kahn's thesis and the Kahn–Lyubich **Quasi-Additivity Law** and **Covering Lemma** delivered complex bounds for broad classes of **primitive** infinitely renormalizable maps (the "decorations" outside the molecule), thereby proving MLC for those parameters. This is the largest extension of the unconditional region since Yoccoz; the residual obstruction is sharpened to the **molecule** — satellite combinatorics of unbounded type.

## Near-parabolic renormalization program (2010s)

Building on Inou–Shishikura's invariant class, Davoud Cheraghi, Arnaud Chéritat, Xavier Buff and Mitsuhiro Shishikura controlled the geometry of Julia sets and parameter-plane structure for **high-type and satellite** rotation numbers, where the linear Yoccoz theory degenerates. These works yield fine results (e.g. on hairiness, dimension, and the failure of certain regularity) and advance, without yet completing, *a priori* bounds for satellite renormalization — the path most likely to close MLC.

## On claimed full proofs

As of the knowledge cutoff, **no complete proof of MLC in full generality has been accepted**, and there is **no widely circulated retracted or disputed "full proof"** of comparable notoriety to those in other open problems. The field's caution reflects the precise, well-understood location of the obstruction: any claimed resolution must supply uniform *a priori* bounds across **all** infinitely renormalizable combinatorics, including the unbounded satellite ("molecule") regime where bounded geometry is known to fail. Partial claims are routinely scrutinized for whether their estimates remain uniform as one approaches the molecule; this uniformity is exactly where prior approaches break, and where any honest assessment should reserve judgment.
