# Top Mathematicians — The MLC Conjecture (Mandelbrot Locally Connected)

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Adrien Douady | 1980s | originator | Structure theory of $M$; connectedness, parameter rays, polynomial-like maps; posed MLC and the pinched-disk model |
| 2 | John H. Hubbard | 1980s–present | originator | Co-author of the Orsay Notes; straightening/renormalization theory; expositor of Yoccoz's theorem |
| 3 | Jean-Christophe Yoccoz | 1990s | breakthrough | Yoccoz puzzle/parapuzzle; MLC at finitely renormalizable parameters (Fields Medal 1994) |
| 4 | Dennis Sullivan | 1980s–90s | breakthrough | Quasiconformal rigidity; renormalization as a dynamical operator; no-wandering-domains methods |
| 5 | Curtis T. McMullen | 1990s | breakthrough | Complex bounds and rigidity of renormalization; *Complex Dynamics and Renormalization* |
| 6 | Mikhail Lyubich | 1990s–present | breakthrough | MLC at bounded-type infinitely renormalizable parameters; real density of hyperbolicity; a priori bounds program |
| 7 | Mitsuhiro Shishikura | 1990s–present | frontier | $\dim_H \partial M = 2$; near-parabolic (Inou–Shishikura) renormalization for satellite combinatorics |
| 8 | Jeremy Kahn | 2000s–present | frontier | Quasi-Additivity Law and Covering Lemma; complex bounds for primitive infinitely renormalizable maps |
| 9 | William Thurston | 1980s | breakthrough | Quadratic minor lamination; combinatorial/abstract model underlying the pinched-disk picture |
| 10 | John Milnor | 1990s–present | expositor | Foundational text *Dynamics in One Complex Variable*; tuning, hairiness, and parameter-plane combinatorics |

## Notes

The active community is small but intense, centered on a handful of groups: Stony Brook (Lyubich's renormalization school, IMS preprint series), Kyoto (Shishikura and the near-parabolic program), Cornell/Provence (Hubbard), Imperial College London (Cheraghi), Toulouse (Buff, Chéritat), and IMPA/Paris (Avila and collaborators). Beyond the named ten, Artur Avila, Weixiao Shen, Davoud Cheraghi, Xavier Buff, Arnaud Chéritat, Tan Lei, Carsten Petersen, Dzmitry Dudko, and Alexandre Dezotti have driven recent progress on *a priori* bounds, rigidity, and satellite renormalization. The consensus is that MLC is true and that the remaining obstruction is the infinitely renormalizable, unbounded-satellite ("molecule") regime; the near-parabolic renormalization machinery is the most actively pursued route to closing it. Work is disseminated largely through the Stony Brook IMS preprints and arXiv (math.DS).
