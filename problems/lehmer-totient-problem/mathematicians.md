# Top Mathematicians — Lehmer's Totient Problem

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Derrick Henry Lehmer | 1932 | originator | Posed the problem; proved any composite solution is odd, squarefree, with $\omega(n) \ge 7$. |
| 2 | E. Lieuwens | 1970s | breakthrough | Showed $3 \mid n \Rightarrow \omega(n) \ge 212$, $n > 5.5 \times 10^{570}$, nearly eliminating that class. |
| 3 | Carl Pomerance | 1977–2011 | breakthrough | Counting bound $L(x) \le x^{1/2+o(1)}$; later, with Luca, the sharpened density estimate. |
| 4 | Graeme L. Cohen | 1980 | breakthrough | With Hagis, raised the floor to $\omega(n) \ge 14$, $n > 10^{20}$. |
| 5 | Peter Hagis | 1980s | breakthrough | Joint and solo strengthenings of the $\omega(n)$ lower bound and prime-factor constraints. |
| 6 | Masao Kishore | 1980 | breakthrough | Independent lower bounds on $\omega(n)$ and on the smallest prime factors of a solution. |
| 7 | Florian Luca | 2007–2011 | frontier | Eliminated Lehmer numbers in Fibonacci/recurrence families; co-author of the sharpest density bound. |
| 8 | William D. Banks | 2007–2008 | frontier | With Luca and others, proved non-existence/finiteness in structured sequences. |
| 9 | Andrew Granville | 1994 | frontier | Co-proved the infinitude of Carmichael numbers, framing the superset within which Lehmer numbers must lie. |
| 10 | Richard K. Guy | 1981–2004 | expositor | Codified the problem (B37) in *Unsolved Problems in Number Theory*, fixing its canonical statement and status. |

## Notes

The active community is small and overlaps heavily with researchers in multiplicative number theory, Carmichael numbers, and totient/iterate problems. **Florian Luca** and **Carl Pomerance** are the central modern figures, both for the strongest density bounds and for the systematic elimination of structured families; their collaborators (Banks, Güloğlu, Nevans, and others) carry much of the special-sequence work. The problem is also kept alive by the **computational number theory** community — searches for Carmichael numbers and lower-bound certifications (in the tradition of Richard Pinch's Carmichael tabulations) repeatedly confirm that no Lehmer number appears in reachable ranges. There is no single "Lehmer problem group"; rather, the question recurs as a benchmark in papers on the distribution of $\varphi$, on $\varphi(n) \mid n-1$ and its relatives (e.g. $\varphi(n) \mid n+1$, Lehmer-type conditions on $\sigma$), and in survey literature (Guy; Sándor–Crstici). Progress is steady but incremental, and no current program claims a route to full resolution.
