# Top Mathematicians — The Weinstein Conjecture

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Alan Weinstein | 1970s–present | originator | Isolated the contact-type hypothesis and posed the conjecture (1979); foundational figure of symplectic geometry. |
| 2 | Paul H. Rabinowitz | 1970s–2000s | originator | Variational existence of periodic orbits on star-shaped hypersurfaces (1978), a founding case and method. |
| 3 | Claude Viterbo | 1980s–present | breakthrough | Proved the conjecture for all contact-type hypersurfaces in $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ (1987), the first general theorem. |
| 4 | Mikhail Gromov | 1980s–present | breakthrough | Introduced pseudoholomorphic curves (1985), the analytic engine behind Hofer's and later attacks. |
| 5 | Helmut Hofer | 1990s–present | breakthrough | Holomorphic-curve proof in dimension three for $S^3$, overtwisted, and $\pi_2\neq 0$ cases (1993); SFT co-founder. |
| 6 | Clifford H. Taubes | 2000s–present | breakthrough | Proved the full three-dimensional conjecture via Seiberg–Witten theory (2007); built ECH$\cong$SW Floer. |
| 7 | Michael Hutchings | 2000s–present | frontier | Created embedded contact homology (ECH); multiplicity theorems and the geometric counts of Reeb orbits. |
| 8 | Yakov Eliashberg | 1980s–present | frontier | Co-founder of symplectic field theory and contact-homology methods; overtwisted/fillability theory. |
| 9 | Daniel Cristofaro-Gardiner | 2010s–present | frontier | With Hutchings, "from one Reeb orbit to two" and the two-or-infinitely-many results in dimension three. |
| 10 | Eduard Zehnder | 1980s–present | expositor | Hofer–Zehnder capacities and the standard text *Symplectic Invariants and Hamiltonian Dynamics*; HWZ analysis. |

## Notes

The community working on the Weinstein conjecture overlaps almost entirely with the modern symplectic/contact topology school. The center of gravity in dimension three is the embedded contact homology and Seiberg–Witten Floer circle around Hutchings, Taubes, Cristofaro-Gardiner, Kutluhan, Lee, and their students, where the active questions are now multiplicity, the structure of the ECH spectrum, and quantitative refinements (the "two or infinitely many" program). In higher dimensions the relevant community is the symplectic field theory and Rabinowitz–Floer homology groups — Eliashberg, Cieliebak, Frauenfelder, Oancea, Wendl, Pardon and collaborators — whose foundational work on virtual/polyfold techniques (Hofer–Wysocki–Zehnder, Pardon) underpins any attempt at the unconditional case in dimensions $\geq 5$. The problem remains a focal point of conferences in symplectic dynamics and of the broader Floer-theory community.
