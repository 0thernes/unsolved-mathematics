# Approaches — The Weinstein Conjecture

The conjecture has been attacked by every major technology of symplectic topology. The methods divide roughly into variational/analytic existence theory, pseudoholomorphic-curve methods, gauge-theoretic methods, and algebraic invariants (Floer-type homologies). Each succeeds on a class of contact manifolds; the higher-dimensional general case has so far resisted all of them.

## Variational and convexity methods

**Core idea.** Periodic Reeb orbits are critical points of an action functional on a loop space. For hypersurfaces in $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ one minimizes or applies minimax (mountain-pass, $S^1$-equivariant) arguments to the symplectic action, exploiting the linear structure of the ambient space.

**Best result.** This route gave the founding theorems — Rabinowitz (star-shaped, 1978), Weinstein (convex, 1978), and decisively **Viterbo (1987)**, who proved the conjecture for *every* hypersurface of contact type in $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$. Hofer–Zehnder and Floer–Hofer–Viterbo extended the variational picture and produced capacity-based existence results.

**Barrier.** The arguments lean essentially on an ambient $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ (or an exact symplectic filling) where the action functional is defined and its critical points controlled. On a general closed contact manifold with no such filling there is no global primitive and no comparable variational setup, so the method does not transfer.

## Pseudoholomorphic curves (Hofer's method)

**Core idea.** Following Gromov's introduction of $J$-holomorphic curves, **Hofer (1993)** studied finite-energy holomorphic planes and spheres in the symplectization $\mathbb{R}\times M$. A bubbling/compactness analysis forces the existence of a Reeb orbit as the asymptotic limit of a holomorphic curve; the *Bishop family* of disks is pushed until it degenerates onto an orbit.

**Best result.** Hofer proved the three-dimensional conjecture for $S^3$ and for all **overtwisted** contact structures, and whenever $\pi_2(M)\neq 0$. The overtwisted case generalizes to higher dimensions once an appropriate notion (plastikstufe / overtwisted disk of Borman–Eliashberg–Murphy) is available, yielding higher-dimensional families where orbits are guaranteed.

**Barrier.** For *tight* contact structures in dimension three the disk-filling argument need not degenerate the right way, and transversality/compactness for the relevant moduli spaces is delicate. Hofer's method alone did not settle the general tight three-dimensional case.

## Contact homology and Symplectic Field Theory

**Core idea.** Eliashberg, Givental and Hofer (2000) proposed an algebraic invariant generated by Reeb orbits with a differential counting holomorphic curves in the symplectization. A nonzero invariant forces orbits to exist; richer structure (linearized contact homology, cylindrical contact homology) gives existence and multiplicity statements.

**Best result.** Where the theory is defined and nonvanishing, it yields existence and lower bounds on the number of orbits, and it underlies many higher-dimensional results for fillable contact manifolds (e.g., via Bourgeois–Oancea and the link to symplectic homology).

**Barrier.** Foundational transversality problems — multiply covered curves and the failure of generic regularity — obstructed rigorous definition for years; **polyfold** (Hofer–Wysocki–Zehnder) and **virtual** techniques were developed precisely to repair this. The machinery typically requires an exact/strong **symplectic filling**, which is exactly what a general closed contact manifold may lack.

## Gauge theory: Seiberg–Witten and ECH (the dimension-three solution)

**Core idea.** **Taubes (2007)** reduced the three-dimensional conjecture to gauge theory. A contact form determines a perturbation of the Seiberg–Witten equations on $M$; nontriviality of the Seiberg–Witten Floer (monopole) invariant, together with a Weitzenböck/curvature estimate, forces solutions whose zero sets, in a singular limit, converge onto a closed Reeb orbit. **Embedded contact homology** (Hutchings) reinterprets these counts geometrically, and the **Taubes–Kutluhan–Lee–Hutchings** isomorphism $\mathrm{ECH}\cong \widehat{HM}$ makes the picture canonical.

**Best result.** A complete, unconditional proof of the conjecture in **dimension three** — the only full solution to date. The same circle of ideas later gave the "two or infinitely many Reeb orbits" theorems (Cristofaro-Gardiner–Hutchings and others).

**Barrier.** Seiberg–Witten theory and ECH are intrinsically **four/three-dimensional** (ECH lives on 3-manifolds via 4-dimensional symplectic cobordisms). There is no known gauge-theoretic invariant of the same power for contact manifolds of dimension $\geq 5$, so this decisive method does not generalize.

## Holomorphic-curve methods in higher dimensions

**Core idea.** For closed contact manifolds of dimension $\geq 5$ that admit symplectic fillings, one uses moduli of holomorphic curves in the filling (or symplectic homology / Rabinowitz–Floer homology) to detect orbits.

**Best result.** Existence is known for many fillable cases, for Boothby–Wang prequantization bundles, for subcritically Stein-fillable manifolds, and via the displaceability/Rabinowitz–Floer arguments of Cieliebak–Frauenfelder–Paternain and collaborators.

**Barrier.** No general higher-dimensional structural theorem is known; results are conditional on fillability, contact-homology well-definedness, or special topology. The unconditional case in dimensions $\geq 5$ remains open, and no negative result (counterexample) is known either.
