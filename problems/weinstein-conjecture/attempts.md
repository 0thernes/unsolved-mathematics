# Attempts — The Weinstein Conjecture

## Founding partial results

The conjecture was born already half-confirmed. Rabinowitz (1978, star-shaped) and Weinstein (1978, convex) supplied the first cases in $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$. The first decisive *general* progress was **Claude Viterbo (1987)**, who proved existence of a closed characteristic on every hypersurface of contact type in $\mathbb{R}^{2n}$ — settling the ambient-Euclidean version in all dimensions and confirming that the contact-type hypothesis Weinstein had isolated was the operative one.

## Hofer's holomorphic-curve breakthrough

**Helmut Hofer (1993)** proved the three-dimensional conjecture for $S^3$, for all overtwisted contact structures, and for any closed contact 3-manifold with $\pi_2(M)\neq 0$. This introduced pseudoholomorphic curves into the problem and reframed the entire program. The remaining three-dimensional case — tight contact structures on rational homology spheres — proved the hardest and resisted holomorphic-curve methods alone for over a decade.

## Taubes's resolution in dimension three

**Clifford Taubes (2007)**, in *The Seiberg–Witten equations and the Weinstein conjecture*, proved the full three-dimensional conjecture using Seiberg–Witten Floer theory. The argument leverages the nontriviality of the monopole Floer invariant of a closed oriented 3-manifold (a consequence of Kronheimer–Mrowka's work) to extract, through a careful singular-perturbation limit of the Seiberg–Witten equations, a closed Reeb orbit. This is the only complete proof of any nontrivial general case and is universally accepted; it has been verified, extended, and absorbed into the ECH program.

## Multiplicity refinements

Once existence was settled in dimension three, attention turned to *counting*. **Cristofaro-Gardiner and Hutchings (2016)** proved that every contact form on a closed 3-manifold has **at least two** distinct embedded Reeb orbits. Cristofaro-Gardiner, Hutchings, Pomerleano and others later showed that for many contact forms there are **infinitely many** orbits, and that infinitely many hold generically. These are not full resolutions of any conjecture beyond Weinstein's existence statement but are the live near-frontier in dimension three.

## Higher-dimensional and conditional results

In dimensions $\geq 5$, existence has been established under hypotheses: overtwistedness (after Borman–Eliashberg–Murphy made the higher-dimensional notion available), symplectic fillability via Rabinowitz–Floer homology (Cieliebak–Frauenfelder and collaborators), prequantization/Boothby–Wang bundles, and displaceable contact-type hypersurfaces. None removes the conditional hypotheses; the unconditional higher-dimensional statement remains open.

## Disputed and withdrawn claims

The Weinstein conjecture is unusual in that its central three-dimensional case was settled cleanly and without lasting controversy. The principal disputes have been **foundational rather than over the main theorems**: the rigorous definition of **contact homology** and **symplectic field theory** — invariants whose nonvanishing was repeatedly invoked to assert existence of Reeb orbits — was challenged because of transversality failures for multiply covered holomorphic curves. Beginning around 2007, expository and critical work (notably by Hofer–Wysocki–Zehnder, who introduced polyfolds to fix the foundations, and pointed critiques of premature regularity claims) showed that several early arguments using contact homology were incomplete as stated. These objections concerned the *general machinery*, not Taubes's gauge-theoretic proof, which is independent of contact-homology transversality and stands unaffected. There is no widely circulated claimed proof of the *higher-dimensional* conjecture that the community regards as a serious contender; the problem there is simply open.
