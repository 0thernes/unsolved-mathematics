# Top Mathematicians — The Hadwiger–Nelson Problem

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Edward Nelson | 1950s | originator | Posed the problem (1950) and proved the lower bound $\chi \ge 4$. |
| 2 | John Isbell | 1950s | originator | Proved the upper bound $\chi \le 7$ via a $7$-coloring of a hexagonal tiling — still the record. |
| 3 | Hugo Hadwiger | 1940s–60s | originator | Geometry of tilings/coverings and expository work that attached his name and popularized the question. |
| 4 | Paul Erdős | 1950s–90s | expositor | Relentlessly publicized the problem, offered prizes, and raised the axiom-dependence question. |
| 5 | Leo Moser & William Moser | 1961 | breakthrough | Introduced the **Moser spindle**, a $7$-vertex $4$-chromatic unit-distance graph. |
| 6 | Kenneth Falconer | 1981 | breakthrough | Proved the *measurable* chromatic number of the plane is at least $5$. |
| 7 | Alexander Soifer | 2000s | expositor | Authored *The Mathematical Coloring Book*; reconstructed the history and co-studied axiom dependence. |
| 8 | Saharon Shelah | 2000s | frontier | With Soifer, showed related distance-graph chromatic numbers can depend on set-theoretic axioms. |
| 9 | Aubrey de Grey | 2018 | breakthrough | Constructed a $1581$-vertex unit-distance graph proving $\chi \ge 5$, the first improvement in 68 years. |
| 10 | Marijn Heule | 2018– | frontier | SAT-based verification and record-small $5$-chromatic graphs with machine-checkable certificates. |

## Notes

The active community today is unusually open and computationally driven. The **Polymath16** project — a public, online collaboration launched within days of de Grey's 2018 announcement — remains the organizing hub, coordinating the hunt for smaller $5$-chromatic unit-distance graphs and probing whether a $6$-chromatic graph exists. Contributors span professional combinatorialists, SAT/automated-reasoning specialists (notably **Marijn Heule** and collaborators, who supply both record constructions and formal UNSAT proofs), geometers such as **Geoffrey Exoo** and **Dan Ismailescu** working on explicit small graphs, and **Alexander Soifer**, who continues to curate the historical and conceptual record and to press the question of axiom dependence. The field also overlaps the broader Euclidean Ramsey theory tradition descending from **Erdős, Graham, Montgomery, and Spencer**. The frontier work is split between the construction side (driving the lower bound toward $6$ and the upper bound below $7$) and the measure-theoretic / set-theoretic side (clarifying whether $\chi$ is even fully determined by ZFC).
