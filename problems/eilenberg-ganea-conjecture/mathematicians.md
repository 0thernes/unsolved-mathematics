# Top Mathematicians — The Eilenberg–Ganea Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Samuel Eilenberg | 1913–1998 | originator | Co-founder of group cohomology and category theory; co-stated the conjecture (1957) and built the homological framework of $\operatorname{cd}$ vs. $\operatorname{gd}$. |
| 2 | Tudor Ganea | 1922–1971 | originator | Co-author of the 1957 paper; supplied the LS-category and CW-construction methods linking geometric dimension to category. |
| 3 | Saunders Mac Lane | 1909–2005 | originator | Co-developed Eilenberg–MacLane spaces $K(\pi,n)$ and group cohomology, the substrate in which the conjecture is posed. |
| 4 | John R. Stallings | 1935–2008 | breakthrough | Proved finitely generated groups of cohomological dimension $1$ are free (1968), settling the dimension-$1$ boundary case. |
| 5 | Richard G. Swan | 1933– | breakthrough | Removed finite generation: $\operatorname{cd}(G)=1 \iff G$ free (1969); deep work on $\widetilde{K}_0(\mathbb{Z}G)$ controlling the dimension-$2$ obstruction. |
| 6 | C. T. C. Wall | 1936– | frontier | Finiteness obstruction theory and the $D(2)$-problem framing that reduces Eilenberg–Ganea to module realizability. |
| 7 | Kenneth S. Brown | 1945– | expositor | *Cohomology of Groups* (1982), the standard reference fixing the modern statement and consequences of the conjecture. |

## Notes

The active community sits at the intersection of **geometric group theory**, **low-dimensional/two-dimensional homotopy theory**, and **algebraic $K$-theory of group rings**. Two strands dominate current work. The first, descending from Bestvina and Brady, probes finiteness properties of subgroups of right-angled Artin groups (and their cube-complex geometry) for a finitely-presented witness to a $\operatorname{cd}=2,\ \operatorname{gd}=3$ gap; researchers around Brady, Bestvina, and the broader RAAG/cube-complex school keep this alive. The second, led by F. E. A. Johnson and collaborators (e.g. W. H. Mannan), pursues the algebraic $D(2)$-problem, testing whether stably-free relation modules realize $2$-complexes group-by-group. The problem also sits inside the well-known cluster of Bestvina's "Questions in Geometric Group Theory" and is regularly restated in survey volumes on two-dimensional homotopy (Hog-Angeloni–Metzler–Sieradski) and combinatorial group theory. There is broad consensus that the conjecture is *probably true*, but no decisive method, and the field treats its entanglement with the Whitehead asphericity conjecture as the central structural obstacle.
