## Note on verification

DOIs are given only where I am confident the identifier is correct for a canonical work (entries 1, 2, 5, 6, 7, 16). The Eilenberg–Ganea conjecture has a relatively thin *direct* literature — most of its weight is carried by a handful of foundational papers (Eilenberg–Ganea 1957; Stallings 1968; Swan 1969; Bestvina–Brady 1997) and by textbook/survey treatments (Brown's *Cohomology of Groups*; the Hog-Angeloni–Metzler–Sieradski volume on two-dimensional homotopy; F.E.A. Johnson's *Stable Modules and the D(2)-Problem*). Several rows (8, 10, 12–14, 17–25) name real books, lecture-note volumes, or research strands whose exact titles/years/DOIs I have not verified; these are flagged "needs-verification" and carry "—" rather than a fabricated identifier. Entries 12–14 and 23–24 describe genuine research areas (cd-vs-gd surveys; Whitehead/asphericity volumes; cohomological dimension of soluble groups; Bestvina–Brady follow-ups) but the specific bibliographic line should be confirmed against MathSciNet before citation. No identifier in this table was invented; where I lacked confidence I used "—".
