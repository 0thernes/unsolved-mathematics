## Notes

The active community is small, highly interconnected, and spans pure operator theory and numerical linear algebra. The analytic frontier is driven by the **Crouzeix–Palencia–Ransford–Schwenninger** circle (Rennes, Valladolid, Université Laval, Twente/Hamburg), focused on shrinking the universal constant from $1+\sqrt2$ toward $2$ and on identifying exactly why the current boundary method stalls. The **computational frontier**, led by Greenbaum, Overton (Courant), and collaborators such as Daeshik Choi, supplies extremal examples, certifies special cases, and stress-tests every proposed improvement. Dilation-theoretic perspectives connect the problem to the broader spectral-set and complete-bound program associated with Badea, Beckermann, and the operator-algebras community. There is broad consensus that the conjecture is true with sharp constant $2$; the open challenge is a uniform proof closing the gap, and most recent activity targets either low-dimensional ($3\times 3$) generality or a structurally new boundary/dilation argument.
