# Top Mathematicians — The Baum–Connes Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Alain Connes | 1980s–present | originator | Co-founder of noncommutative geometry; index-theoretic side of the conjecture; Connes–Kasparov framing |
| 2 | Paul Baum | 1980s–2024 | originator | Geometric $K$-homology, classifying space $\underline{E}G$, the topological side of the assembly map |
| 3 | Gennadi Kasparov | 1980s–present | breakthrough | Bivariant $KK$-theory and the Dirac–dual-Dirac method; Novikov for Lie-group lattices |
| 4 | Nigel Higson | 1990s–present | breakthrough | Modern $\underline{E}G$ formulation; Higson–Kasparov theorem for a-T-menable groups |
| 5 | Vincent Lafforgue | 2000s–present | breakthrough | Banach $KK$-theory; conjecture for Property (T) and all hyperbolic groups (Fields Medal 2002) |
| 6 | Georges Skandalis | 1980s–present | breakthrough | Longitudinal index theorem; counterexamples with coefficients (HLS, STY) |
| 7 | Guoliang Yu | 1990s–present | frontier | Coarse Baum–Connes; coarse embedding/Property A criterion for injectivity and Novikov |
| 8 | Siegfried Echterhoff | 2000s–present | frontier | Almost-connected and $p$-adic groups (Chabert–Echterhoff–Nest); Going-Down techniques |
| 9 | Ryszard Nest | 1990s–present | frontier | Categorical (Meyer–Nest) reformulation; almost-connected groups |
| 10 | Jean-Louis Tu | 1990s–present | frontier | Groupoid/coarse methods; bolic spaces; expander counterexamples (STY) |

## Notes

The active community sits at the intersection of operator algebras, $K$-theory, coarse/large-scale geometry, and geometric group theory, and is unusually international. French operator-algebra schools (Connes, Skandalis, Lafforgue, Tu, and students), the Münster group around **Siegfried Echterhoff** and the categorical program of **Ralf Meyer** and **Ryszard Nest**, and the U.S./Penn State lineage (Baum, **Nigel Higson**, and students such as **Erik Guentner** and **Rufus Willett**) remain central. **Guoliang Yu** (Texas A&M) and collaborators drive the coarse-geometry and Novikov-injectivity frontier, while **Vincent Lafforgue** continues to push the Banach-$KK$ and strong-Property-(T) machinery that is the only known route past higher-rank obstructions. Recent activity (Hochs, Wang, and others) has revisited the **Connes–Kasparov isomorphism** for reductive groups with cleaner proofs, and survey/textbook efforts by **Willett–Yu** and **Valette** keep the field's standards explicit. The open frontier — higher-rank lattices like $SL_3(\mathbb{Z})$ — keeps the problem squarely in the hands of this same community.
