# Top Mathematicians — The Lonely Runner Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Jörg M. Wills | 1960s– | originator | Posed the conjecture (1967) within simultaneous Diophantine approximation; proved the first small cases and identified the sharp constant $\tfrac{1}{n+1}$ with extremizer $1,2,\dots,n$. |
| 2 | T. W. Cusick | 1970s– | originator | Independently formulated the equivalent **view-obstruction problem** for cubes (1973); foundational geometry-of-numbers framing. |
| 3 | Carl Pomerance | 1980s | breakthrough | With Cusick, settled $n=4$ (1984), a decisive early case beyond the originators' reach. |
| 4 | Luis Goddyn | 1990s | expositor | Coined the "lonely runner" name; with collaborators connected the problem to flows, colourings, and combinatorics (1998). |
| 5 | András Sebő | 1990s | breakthrough | Co-author of the unifying 1998 paper linking view obstructions, flows, and the runner picture. |
| 6 | Michael Tarsi | 1990s | breakthrough | Co-author of the 1998 synthesis; brought combinatorial and matroidal perspectives. |
| 7 | Javier Barajas | 2000s | breakthrough | With Serra, proved $n=5$ (2008) via a refined regular-chromatic-number / view-obstruction argument. |
| 8 | Oriol Serra | 2000s– | breakthrough | Co-author of the $n=5$ proof; sustained contributions on distance graphs, correlations among runners, and structured cases. |
| 9 | Xuding Zhu | 2000s | frontier | Developed the circular/regular chromatic number dictionary for distance graphs that makes the colouring reformulation precise. |
| 10 | Terence Tao | 2010s | frontier | Proved an improved (sub-sharp) constant and the **finiteness reduction** (2017), turning each fixed $n$ into a finite computation. |

## Notes

The active community sits at the crossroads of **combinatorial number theory, the geometry of numbers, graph colouring, and additive combinatorics**. Spanish and Catalan combinatorialists around **Oriol Serra** (Barcelona) have been a center of gravity for the sharp-case program, while the **circular chromatic / distance-graph** school (Zhu and successors) supplies the colouring-theoretic machinery. Since Tao's 2017 reduction, momentum has shifted toward **computational and polyhedral methods** — the "lonely runner polytope," Ehrhart-theoretic and lattice-point feasibility formulations associated with **Matthias Beck, Serkan Hoşten, and Matthias Schymura** and collaborators — aimed at mechanizing the finite checks for the next values of $n$. Work on **shifted, rational, and dynamical variants** continues to attract attention both for its own sake and as a testing ground for techniques. Despite this breadth, the sharp constant for general $n$ has resisted every group, and the problem is regarded as a benchmark for how far elementary-plus-computational methods can be pushed.
