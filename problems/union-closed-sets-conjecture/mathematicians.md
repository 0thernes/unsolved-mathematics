# Top Mathematicians — The Union-Closed Sets Conjecture (Frankl)

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Péter Frankl | 1979– | originator | Posed the conjecture (~1979); central figure of the extremal set theory tradition in which it lives. |
| 2 | Bjorn Poonen | 1980s–90s | expositor | Clarified equivalent formulations (set / lattice) and characterized when fixed weightings certify the conjecture; foundational structural analysis. |
| 3 | J.-C. Renaud & D. G. Sarvate | 1980s–90s | frontier | Early bounds and the influential demonstration that natural strengthenings fail, ruling out the easy averaging routes. |
| 4 | David Reimer | 1990s | breakthrough | Theorem on the average set size of union-closed families, a celebrated adjacent result repeatedly used in attempts. |
| 5 | Theodore P. Knill | 1990s | frontier | Density-type lower bounds (an element in roughly $m/\log_2 m$ sets) for general families. |
| 6 | Jürgen Reinhold | 1990s–2000s | frontier | Proved the lattice form for important classes (e.g. lower-semimodular / relatively complemented lattices). |
| 7 | Gábor Czédli | 2000s | frontier | Lattice-theoretic results, including cases for large and semimodular lattices (with E. T. Schmidt). |
| 8 | Henning Bruhn & Oliver Schaudt | 2010s | expositor | Authored the standard survey "The journey of the union-closed sets conjecture"; with Charbit and Telle, the graph/stable-set reformulation. |
| 9 | Justin Gilmer | 2022 | breakthrough | Entropy method giving the first unconditional constant-fraction bound ($\approx 1\%$), reopening the problem. |
| 10 | Will Sawin | 2022– | frontier | Sharpened Gilmer's bound to (and slightly beyond) $\frac{3-\sqrt5}{2}\approx0.38$; clarified the one-step entropy barrier. |

## Notes

The active community splits into three overlapping camps. The **lattice / order-theory** group (descendants of Poonen, Reinhold, Czédli, Abe) continues to chip at structured classes and the irreducible-element formulation. The **graph-theoretic** group (Bruhn, Schaudt, Charbit, Telle and successors) works the stable-set reduction and tries to widen the size-$\le 2$ slice. Since November 2022 the **information-theoretic** group has been the most active and fast-moving: Gilmer's preprint triggered near-simultaneous optimizations by Sawin, Chase and Lovett, Alweiss–Liu–Sawhney–Wu, Pebody, and Ellis–Ivan–Leader, much of it conducted openly on arXiv and research blogs within days. The central open question now belongs to this group — whether entropy methods can in principle be pushed from $0.38$ to the full $1/2$, or whether a genuinely different idea is needed to close the gap. The problem also draws periodic Polymath-style and competition-mathematics interest because of its elementary statement.
