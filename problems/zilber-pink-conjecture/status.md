# Status & Frontier — The Zilber–Pink Conjecture

**Status: open.** The conjecture is unproven in essentially all of its higher-dimensional generality. What has been achieved is a sequence of decisive special cases and the construction of a coherent machine — o-minimal point counting plus functional transcendence plus height bounds — that *would* prove the general statement if its remaining arithmetic inputs were available.

## What is known (unconditional)

- **The founding multiplicative case.** Bombieri–Masser–Zannier (1999) and the anomalous-subvariety theory give Zilber–Pink for curves and for the structurally controlled cases in $\mathbb{G}_m^n$.
- **Manin–Mumford and Mordell–Lang** (the abelian "expected-dimension-0" specializations) are classical theorems (Raynaud; Faltings, Vojta, McQuillan), reproved via Pila–Zannier.
- **André–Oort for $\mathbb{C}^n$** (Pila, 2011) and, far more, **André–Oort for all Shimura varieties of abelian type** — a major special case of Zilber–Pink — is a theorem of **Pila–Shankar–Tsimerman (2021/2022)**, resting on Tsimerman's (2018) Galois lower bounds for $\mathcal{A}_g$ via the averaged Colmez conjecture (Andreatta–Goren–Howard–Madapusi Pera; Yuan–Zhang).
- **Functional transcendence is settled:** Ax–Schanuel for the $j$-function (Pila–Tsimerman, 2016), for Shimura varieties (Mok–Pila–Tsimerman, 2019), and for variations of Hodge structure (Bakker–Tsimerman, 2019).
- **Unconditional height bounds** in the non-degenerate regime and uniform Mordell-type results (Dimitrov–Gao–Habegger; Gao–Habegger), giving unconditional Zilber–Pink for curves in abelian varieties in many cases.

## What is known (conditional)

- Zilber–Pink for curves in $Y(1)^n$ and in abelian varieties (Habegger–Pila) holds under **large-Galois-orbit** and **height** hypotheses; these have been discharged in further cases (Daw–Orr) but not in general.
- General André–Oort beyond abelian type, and Zilber–Pink for surfaces and higher, remain conditional on analogues of the Galois-orbit and height conjectures.

## What a full resolution requires

1. **Galois lower bounds** for atypical/special points in full generality (beyond $\mathcal{A}_g$ and abelian type).
2. **Height bounds** for the atypical locus in the *degenerate* case (where the Betti map is not submersive) and for higher-dimensional $V$.
3. An **induction on optimal/atypical components** that survives passage to non-abelian and mixed Shimura settings — the geometric backbone (Ax–Schanuel) exists; the arithmetic closure does not.

## Plausible routes

- Extend Tsimerman-type Colmez/Galois-orbit methods beyond abelian type.
- Push the Gao–Habegger Betti-map / mixed-Ax–Schanuel height machinery into degenerate and higher-dimensional families.
- Effective o-minimality (Binyamini–Novikov–Schmidt–Yafaev) to obtain explicit counting matched with effective Galois bounds.

No credible complete proof is currently under community review; announcements of a full resolution should be checked against Zannier's monograph and the Pila–Shankar–Tsimerman paper before being relied upon.
