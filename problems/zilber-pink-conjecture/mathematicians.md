## Notes

The Zilber–Pink community is small but unusually interdisciplinary, sitting at the intersection of model theory (o-minimality), arithmetic geometry (Shimura varieties, heights), and complex/functional transcendence (Ax–Schanuel). Its center of gravity runs through Oxford (Zilber, Pila historically), the Scuola Normale Superiore in Pisa (Zannier and his school: Capuano, Corvaja, and others), ETH Zürich (Pink), Toronto (Tsimerman), Basel (Habegger), and a French–US network around Gao, Daw, Orr, Klingler, Ullmo, and Yafaev. The Binyamini–Novikov–Schmidt–Yafaev effort on *sharply o-minimal* structures and effective point-counting forms an active quantitative wing. Conferences and thematic semesters (e.g., at IHÉS, MSRI/SLMath, and Oberwolfach on "unlikely intersections" and "o-minimality and number theory") regularly convene the field. The post-2021 resolution of André–Oort for abelian-type Shimura varieties has redirected energy toward the genuinely higher-dimensional and non-abelian Zilber–Pink cases, where the missing ingredients are height bounds and large-Galois-orbit lower bounds.
