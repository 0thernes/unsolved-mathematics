# Attempts — The Zilber–Pink Conjecture

Zilber–Pink has not been the subject of widely publicized *false* proofs — its difficulty is well understood, and the community has largely advanced through honest partial results rather than premature claims. The notable history is one of conditional theorems steadily made unconditional, and of cases that came tantalizingly close before stalling on a missing arithmetic input.

## Conditional milestones later upgraded

- **André–Oort under GRH (Klingler–Ullmo–Yafaev, 2006–2014).** The first general André–Oort theorem held *conditionally on the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis*, used to obtain lower bounds on Galois orbits of special points. This was not a flawed proof but a genuine theorem with a hypothesis; it was rendered unconditional only after Tsimerman's 2018 lower bounds for $\mathcal{A}_g$ (via averaged Colmez) and the Pila–Shankar–Tsimerman synthesis (2021). The episode illustrates the field's pattern: the *geometry* is provable, the *arithmetic* lags.

- **Habegger–Pila for $Y(1)^n$ (2016).** Their Zilber–Pink theorem for curves was proved *modulo* a "large Galois orbits" hypothesis and a height conjecture. Subsequent work (Daw–Orr, and Gao–Habegger height bounds) discharged these hypotheses in further cases, but not in general — the conditional core remains the live obstruction for surfaces and higher.

## Near-misses and partial frontiers

- **Maurin's theorem and its gaps.** Guillaume Maurin (2008) proved a strong unlikely-intersections statement for curves in $\mathbb{G}_m^n$ (the "bounded height" plus finiteness), extending Bombieri–Masser–Zannier. Bombieri–Habegger–Masser–Zannier later clarified and reproved parts, and identified precisely where the naive dimension count fails (the role of *anomalous subvarieties*), showing that early optimistic formulations needed the correction now standard in the conjecture's statement.

- **The "defect condition" subtleties.** Several authors found that early statements of Zilber–Pink required careful restriction to *optimal* components; intersections that look atypical can be forced by a hidden special subvariety of positive dimension. This is a definitional near-miss rather than a proof error, but it derailed some informal expectations about what finiteness should mean.

## On disputed or retracted claims

To the compiler's knowledge there is **no prominent retracted or disputed claimed proof** of the full Zilber–Pink conjecture, nor of full André–Oort beyond the genuinely established abelian-type case. Claims occasionally circulate in preprint form proposing complete resolutions via novel transcendence or model-theoretic methods; as of this writing none has been accepted by the community, and readers should treat any announcement of a full proof with the standard caution — verify against the consensus literature (Zannier's monograph, the Pila–Shankar–Tsimerman paper, and surveys by Pila, Habegger, and Gao) before relying on it. The honest status is: *open in higher dimension, with no credible complete proof currently under review.*
