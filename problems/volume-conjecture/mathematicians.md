# Top Mathematicians — The Volume Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Rinat M. Kashaev | 1994–present | originator | Defined the quantum-dilogarithm invariant $\langle K\rangle_N$ and posed the original conjecture linking its growth to hyperbolic volume (1997). |
| 2 | Hitoshi Murakami | 2001–present | originator | Co-identified $\langle K\rangle_N$ with the colored Jones polynomial at $e^{2\pi i/N}$; prolific expositor and prover of small cases. |
| 3 | Jun Murakami | 2001–present | originator | Co-author of the colored-Jones reformulation and the simplicial-volume/complexified versions. |
| 4 | William P. Thurston | 1970s–1980s | originator | Created the hyperbolic geometry of $3$-manifolds, making hyperbolic volume a knot invariant — the geometric side of the conjecture. |
| 5 | Tomotada Ohtsuki | 2007–present | breakthrough | Rigorous saddle-point asymptotic expansions for $4_1$, $5_2$, and further knots — the model proofs of the conjecture. |
| 6 | Yoshiyuki Yokota | 2000s–present | breakthrough | Potential-function approach tying dilogarithm critical values to the complex volume of triangulated complements. |
| 7 | Stavros Garoufalidis | 2000s–present | frontier | $q$-holonomicity (with Lê), AJ conjecture, resurgence and quantum-modularity program for the Kashaev invariant. |
| 8 | Sergei Gukov | 2003–present | breakthrough | Generalized Volume Conjecture linking asymptotics to the $A$-polynomial and Chern–Simons theory. |
| 9 | Tian Yang | 2015–present | frontier | Chen–Yang Turaev–Viro volume conjecture; proofs for fundamental shadow links and Dehn fillings. |
| 10 | Don Zagier | 2010s–present | frontier | Quantum modular forms framework (with Garoufalidis) governing the full asymptotic transseries of the Kashaev invariant. |

## Notes

The community is small, international, and highly active, clustered at the interface of quantum topology, hyperbolic geometry, and mathematical physics. Japanese topology (Ohtsuki, the two Murakamis, Yokota, Takata) remains a center of gravity for the rigorous knot-by-knot asymptotics, while the Garoufalidis–Zagier–Mariño axis brings resurgence, state integrals, and quantum modularity from the physics-adjacent side. The Chen–Yang reformulation has energized a younger cohort (Detcherry, Kalfagianni, Belletti, Wong, Ohtsuki's students) working on Turaev–Viro and Reshetikhin–Turaev growth for general $3$-manifolds, broadening the problem well beyond knots in $S^3$. Activity is sustained by regular workshops (e.g. on quantum topology and hyperbolic geometry), strong numerical/experimental verification across the SnapPy census, and the problem's role as a concrete test case for the deeper "quantum = geometric" philosophy of Chern–Simons TQFT. Erik Andersen's Teichmüller TQFT (with Kashaev) supplies a parallel, more analytic line of attack on the same volume/Chern–Simons asymptotics.
