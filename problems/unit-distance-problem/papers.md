**Note on verification.** Identifiers are given only where I am confident. Several canonical works — most notably Spencer–Szemerédi–Trotter (1984), the original Józsa–Szemerédi improvement, and several Erdős problem-collection papers and survey chapters — are real and well-documented but I do not have a DOI/arXiv id I can assert with confidence, so they carry "—" and a "needs-verification" flag rather than a fabricated number. The Guth–Katz entry (#16) is the *distinct*-distances breakthrough (2010 preprint; Annals of Mathematics 2015); it is included because it is the decisive nearby result and the template for the polynomial method, though it does **not** resolve the unit-distance count. Rows 13–24 are plausible representatives of the modern incidence/polynomial-method literature; the exact titles and author lists for some should be checked against the published record before citation.
