**Note on shortfall and honesty.** Brocard's problem genuinely has very few dedicated primary papers; the *certainly real, on-target* core is small — Brocard's 1876/1885 notes, Ramanujan's 1913 Question 469, Dąbrowski (1996), Berndt–Galway (2000), and the Guy *Unsolved Problems* entry. Several rows above (marked **ai-suggested** or **needs-verification**) are plausible representative works in the surrounding factorial-Diophantine and abc literature whose exact titles, years, or authorship I cannot vouch for; they are included as research leads, not as confirmed citations, and identifiers were withheld ("—") rather than invented. The Mochizuki PRIMS DOI is the one numerical identifier given and even that pertains to the *disputed* abc proof, not to Brocard's problem directly. Treat every non-high-confidence row as a starting point requiring a human source check.
