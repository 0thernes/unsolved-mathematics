# Top Mathematicians — The Hadamard Matrix Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | James Joseph Sylvester | 1814–1897 | originator | First infinite family of $\{\pm1\}$ orthogonal matrices (orders $2^m$) via recursive doubling, 1867 |
| 2 | Jacques Hadamard | 1865–1963 | originator | Proved the determinant bound, identified the $4 \mid n$ condition, posed the existence conjecture, 1893 |
| 3 | Raymond Paley | 1907–1933 | originator | Quadratic-residue (Paley) constructions giving orders $q+1$ and $2(q+1)$; framed the modern existence program, 1933 |
| 4 | John Williamson | 1901–1949 | breakthrough | Array method reducing order $4n$ to four symmetric circulants and a sum-of-squares condition, 1944 |
| 5 | Marshall Hall Jr. | 1910–1990 | breakthrough | Co-discoverer (with Baumert, Golomb) of the order-$92$ matrix; design-theoretic framework, 1962 |
| 6 | Solomon W. Golomb | 1932–2016 | breakthrough | Co-discovered order $92$; sequence-design methods linking shift registers to Hadamard constructions |
| 7 | Richard J. Turyn | 20th c. | breakthrough | Baumert–Hall arrays, complex/Williamson-type families, and the asymptotic-existence program, 1970s |
| 8 | Jennifer Seberry | b. 1944 | breakthrough | Asymptotic existence theorem ($2^t m$ for $t \ge 2\log_2(m-3)$); decades of constructions and existence tables |
| 9 | Kathy J. Horadam | contemporary | frontier | Co-founder of the cocyclic theory of Hadamard matrices; monograph unifying constructions |
| 10 | Hadi Kharaghani | contemporary | frontier | Constructed order $428$ (with Tayfeh-Rezaie); co-author of the standard existence survey |

## Notes

The active community is concentrated in **combinatorial design theory**, with strong nodes in Australia (the school founded by **Jennifer Seberry**, including her many students and collaborators), Canada (**Hadi Kharaghani** and **Rob Craigen**), Iran (**Behruz Tayfeh-Rezaie**), and groups in Europe working on **cocyclic and group-cohomological** methods in the tradition of **Kathy Horadam** and **Warwick de Launey** (whose untimely death in 2010 was a significant loss to the field). Current work splits between (i) **asymptotic existence**, where number theorists and combinatorialists push the exponent in Seberry-type bounds toward the conjectural $t=2$, and (ii) **explicit settlement of small open orders** (notably $668$) using base sequences, Turyn-type constructions, and increasingly **SAT solvers and algebraic/Gröbner search**. The community maintains shared existence tables (descended from Seberry's and the Craigen–Kharaghani handbook survey) that record the status of every order, making it one of the better-bookkept open problems in combinatorics.
