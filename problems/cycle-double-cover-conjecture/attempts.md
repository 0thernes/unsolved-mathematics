# Attempts — The Cycle Double Cover Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

The CDC has no famous "claimed proof and refutation" episode of the kind that attaches to the Four Colour Theorem or the $abc$ conjecture, but its history is rich in instructive near-misses, partial theorems that almost generalize, and reductions that shift the difficulty without removing it.

**The flow near-miss.** Jaeger's 8-flow theorem (1979) implies that the edges of any bridgeless graph can be covered by even subgraphs in a controlled way, and yields a *cover* of every edge at most a bounded number of times. This came tantalizingly close to a double cover, and for decades raised the hope that strengthening the flow bound from 8 to a $\mathbb{Z}_2\times\mathbb{Z}_2$-style 4-flow on snarks would finish the job. It does not: snarks have no nowhere-zero 4-flow, so the flow route stalls exactly on the hard cases. This is the canonical example of a powerful tool that solves everything *except* the cases the conjecture is really about.

**Oddness ladders.** Huck and Kochol (1995) settled oddness 2, a genuine theorem, and it was widely expected that the same induction would climb to oddness 4, 6, and beyond. Progress on oddness 4 (Häggkvist–McGuinness; later refinements by Kaiser, Král', Lidický, Nejedlý, Šámal, and by Steffen) required substantial new ideas and additional hypotheses, and no uniform argument has reached all oddness. The "ladder" turned out to have rungs that grow combinatorially harder, a near-miss in the sense that the method clearly *wants* to generalize but resists.

**The Sabidussi compatibility detour.** Reductions to faithful/compatible covers (Fleischner, Zhang, Goddyn) recast CDC as a weighted-eulerian covering problem. The seductive feature is that compatible-cover statements are sometimes easier to verify on structured classes. The cautionary fact is that the *unrestricted* compatibility conjecture is **false** — there are weighted bridgeless graphs with no faithful cover (e.g. constructions around the Petersen graph) — so any successful version must build in the snark structure, and the difficulty re-emerges. This is a near-miss of method: a clean equivalent target that proves to be no softer.

**Genus and embedding attempts.** Proofs of CDC for graphs of bounded genus, and partial results toward the Strong Embedding Conjecture, repeatedly hit the wall that minimal-genus embeddings of cubic graphs need not be 2-cell with circuit faces. No embedding-based argument has been extended to all snarks, and the embedding conjectures remain open in their own right.

**Petersen graph as the universal obstruction.** Many partial proofs work "unless the graph contains a Petersen-like piece," and the Petersen graph itself is the smallest snark. It famously *does* have a cycle double cover (its five 5-cycles double-cover it, realized by its embedding as the hemi-dodecahedron on the projective plane), so it is not a counterexample — but it is the obstruction to nearly every shortcut, and Petersen-minor-free and $P_{10}$-free arguments are a recurring partial-result genre (cf. Alspach–Goddyn–Zhang).

**Disputed or withdrawn claims.** There is no widely recognized, peer-vetted full proof that was later retracted; CDC is not a magnet for the kind of high-profile claimed solutions seen elsewhere. From time to time preprints announcing a general proof appear (on arXiv and elsewhere), but as of the current frontier none has been accepted by the community, and the conjecture is universally regarded as open. The honest summary is that the difficulty has been *localized* (to snarks, to high oddness, to the absence of 4-flows) with great precision, but never eliminated. Readers should treat any claim of a complete proof with caution and check for independent verification before relying on it.
