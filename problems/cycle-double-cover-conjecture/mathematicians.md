# Top Mathematicians — The Cycle Double Cover Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | George Szekeres | 1973 | originator | Posed the cubic case via polyhedral decompositions; the geometric/embedding origin of CDC |
| 2 | Paul D. Seymour | 1979– | originator | Independently stated CDC in "Sums of circuits"; nowhere-zero 6-flow theorem |
| 3 | W. T. Tutte | 1950s–70s | originator | Nowhere-zero flow theory and the Circular Embedding Conjecture underlying CDC's topological form |
| 4 | François Jaeger | 1979–1985 | breakthrough | 8-flow theorem; the influential 1985 survey that organized the field and the snark reduction |
| 5 | Herbert Fleischner | 1980s–90s | breakthrough | Compatible circuit decompositions and faithful-cover theory; eulerian-graph framework |
| 6 | Luis A. Goddyn | 1988–1990s | breakthrough | Thesis on cycle covers; circumference/girth constraints on a minimal counterexample |
| 7 | Cun-Quan Zhang | 1990s–2010s | expositor | Authored the two standard monographs (1997, 2012); systematized partial results and reductions |
| 8 | Andreas Huck | 1990s–2000s | frontier | CDC for oddness 2 (with Kochol); girth bounds on counterexamples; strong-CDC work |
| 9 | Roland Häggkvist | 2000s | frontier | Kotzig-frame and spanning-minor methods; progress on higher oddness |
| 10 | Eckhard Steffen | 2000s–2020s | frontier | Snark structure, oddness and resistance parameters, links to Berge–Fulkerson |

## Notes

The active community sits at the intersection of structural and topological graph theory and is concentrated in a few centers. In Central Europe, schools at Charles University and Masaryk University (Brno) — Daniel Král', Robert Šámal, Tomáš Kaiser, with collaborators such as Bernard Lidický — drive the short-cycle-cover and flow side, while Martin Kochol (Bratislava) contributed key snark constructions. In Western Europe, Eckhard Steffen (Paderborn) and the Ghent computational group (Gunnar Brinkmann, Jan Goedgebeur, with Klas Markström in Umeå) supply both structural theory and exhaustive snark generation via *snarkhunter* and *House of Graphs*. In North America, Cun-Quan Zhang (West Virginia) and Luis Goddyn (Simon Fraser) anchor the cycle-cover and faithful-cover tradition, and Paul Seymour's Princeton circle keeps the flow and matching connections (Berge–Fulkerson, Petersen-colourings) in view. The field's culture is incremental and reduction-driven: progress is measured in narrowing the class a counterexample could inhabit (snarks of high oddness, large girth, small circumference) rather than in sweeping resolutions.
