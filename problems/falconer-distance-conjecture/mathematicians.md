# Top Mathematicians — The Falconer Distance Conjecture

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Kenneth J. Falconer | 1985–present | originator | Posed the problem; proved the $(d+1)/2$ threshold and built the dimension-$d/2$ null-distance-set examples that fix the conjectured sharp bound. |
| 2 | Paul Erdős | 1946 | originator | Posed the finite distinct-distances problem, the combinatorial ancestor that motivates the continuous formulation. |
| 3 | Pertti Mattila | 1987–present | breakthrough | Introduced the spherical-average / Mattila-integral framework reducing the problem to Fourier decay; authored the standard GMT reference. |
| 4 | Thomas Wolff | 1999 | breakthrough | Proved $\dim_H E > 4/3$ in the plane, the benchmark planar result for nearly two decades, via decay of circular means. |
| 5 | M. Burak Erdoğan | 2005–2006 | breakthrough | Used bilinear/extension estimates to improve higher-dimensional thresholds, advancing the restriction-based approach. |
| 6 | Larry Guth | 2015–present | breakthrough | Co-resolved the Erdős distinct-distances problem (with Katz) and co-proved the $5/4$ planar Falconer bound; central to the decoupling/polynomial-method era. |
| 7 | Alex Iosevich | 2000s–present | frontier | Drove group-action and pinned-distance approaches; coauthor of the $5/4$ breakthrough and many higher-dimensional results. |
| 8 | Hong Wang | 2018–present | frontier | Coauthor of the $5/4$ planar result; leading figure in restriction, radial projections, and ongoing progress toward $d/2$. |
| 9 | Xiumin Du | 2018–present | frontier | With Zhang and others, established weighted-restriction Falconer bounds of the form $d/2 + 1/4$ in higher dimensions. |
| 10 | Ruixiang Zhang | 2018–present | frontier | Co-developed the sharp Schrödinger-maximal / weighted-restriction machinery powering the best high-dimensional Falconer thresholds. |

## Notes

The active community sits squarely inside modern harmonic analysis and geometric measure theory, overlapping heavily with the restriction, decoupling, and Kakeya programs. The current frontier is driven by Hong Wang, Xiumin Du, Ruixiang Zhang, Yumeng Ou, Alex Iosevich, and collaborators such as Shengwen Gan, Kevin Ren, and Bochen Liu, with foundational geometric input from Tuomas Orponen and Pablo Shmerkin on radial projections and dimension of distance sets. The polynomial method and Bourgain–Demeter decoupling reshaped the field after 2015, and progress on Falconer now moves in lockstep with progress on restriction-type estimates. Much of the activity is concentrated at institutions in the US and Europe (MIT, NYU/Courant, Berkeley, Maryland, Wisconsin, and several UK and Finnish groups), and the problem regularly features in survey lectures as a barometer for how close the field is to optimal restriction estimates.
