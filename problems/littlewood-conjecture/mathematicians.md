# Top Mathematicians — The Littlewood Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | John Edensor Littlewood | 1885–1977 | originator | Posed the conjecture (c. 1930): $\liminf_n n\lVert n\alpha\rVert\lVert n\beta\rVert = 0$ for all real $\alpha,\beta$. |
| 2 | J. W. S. Cassels | 1922–2015 | breakthrough | With Swinnerton-Dyer, reformulated the problem via products of linear forms; proved conditional and special-case results; canonical Diophantine-approximation text. |
| 3 | H. P. F. Swinnerton-Dyer | 1927–2018 | breakthrough | Co-author of the 1955 reformulation linking Littlewood to lattices and diagonal-orbit rigidity. |
| 4 | Harold Davenport | 1907–1969 | breakthrough | Foundational work on badly approximable numbers and the geometry of products of linear forms. |
| 5 | Wolfgang M. Schmidt | 1933–2025 | breakthrough | Deep theory of simultaneous Diophantine approximation and badly approximable systems; influential surveys. |
| 6 | Grigory Margulis | b. 1946 | breakthrough | Rigidity of higher-rank diagonal actions; articulated the dynamical conjecture implying Littlewood. |
| 7 | Marina Ratner | 1938–2017 | breakthrough | Measure/orbit-closure rigidity for unipotent flows, the template for homogeneous-dynamics attacks. |
| 8 | Manfred Einsiedler | b. 1973 | frontier | Co-author of the 2006 dimension-zero theorem; leader of the measure-rigidity programme. |
| 9 | Anatole Katok | 1944–2018 | frontier | Co-author of the 2006 EKL theorem; pioneer of higher-rank smooth/measurable rigidity. |
| 10 | Elon Lindenstrauss | b. 1970 | frontier | Co-author of the 2006 EKL theorem; measure rigidity and entropy methods (Fields Medal 2010, citing this work). |

## Notes

The active community sits at the interface of **homogeneous dynamics**, **ergodic theory**, and **Diophantine approximation**. The center of gravity is the measure-rigidity school descended from Margulis and Ratner and crystallized by Einsiedler, Katok, and Lindenstrauss; current frontier work on effective equidistribution, entropy methods, and the zero-entropy gap involves Einsiedler and Lindenstrauss and their students and collaborators (including researchers such as Philippe Michel, Akshay Venkatesh, Uri Shapira, Barak Weiss, and Dmitry Kleinbock), based across ETH Zürich, the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, Tel Aviv University, and allied groups in Europe and the US. A parallel community works on explicit, metric, and $p$-adic/mixed variants — Velani, Pollington, Badziahin, Bugeaud, de Mathan, Adiceam, and Nesharim among them — probing where Littlewood-type statements are true versus false. Names listed with birth/death years reflect documented biographical record; any uncertainty in exact dates should be confirmed against primary sources before publication.
