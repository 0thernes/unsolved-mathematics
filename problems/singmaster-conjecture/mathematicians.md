# Top Mathematicians — Singmaster's Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | David Singmaster | 1971 | originator | Posed the conjecture (1971); identified the Fibonacci family giving $N(a)\ge6$ infinitely often and the record value $3003$. |
| 2 | Paul Erdős | 1970s–1990s | breakthrough | Co-author of the foundational 1974 upper bound; popularized the problem and conjectured the true maximal multiplicity is $\le 8$, attained finitely often. |
| 3 | Harvey L. Abbott | 1974 | breakthrough | Co-author of the first nontrivial bound $N(a)=O(\log a/\log\log a)$. |
| 4 | Denis Hanson | 1974 | breakthrough | Co-author of the 1974 Abbott–Erdős–Hanson upper bound. |
| 5 | Benjamin M. M. de Weger | 1980s–1990s | breakthrough | Solved individual equations $\binom{n}{2}=\binom{m}{4}$ and relatives using Baker's effective methods; explicit enumeration of solutions. |
| 6 | Tarlok N. Shorey | 1980s–2000s | frontier | Effective Diophantine analysis of equal binomial coefficients and exponential Diophantine equations (often with Tijdeman). |
| 7 | Robert Tijdeman | 1980s–2000s | frontier | Transcendence/linear-forms methods applied to powers and binomial coefficient equations. |
| 8 | Richard K. Guy | 1980s–2000s | expositor | Catalogued and disseminated the conjecture in *Unsolved Problems in Number Theory*, fixing its canonical statement. |
| 9 | Terence Tao | 2021 | frontier | Co-author of the 2021 power-saving average bound; expositor who reframed the reduction to Diophantine equations for a broad audience. |
| 10 | Kaisa Matomäki & Maksym Radziwiłł | 2021 | frontier | Drivers of the analytic machinery behind the 2021 result controlling the average and exceptional-set density of multiplicities (with Shao and Teräväinen). |

## Notes

The active research community around Singmaster's conjecture is small and sits at the intersection of analytic and Diophantine number theory rather than combinatorics proper. For decades after 1971 progress came from specialists in Diophantine equations — de Weger, Shorey, Tijdeman, and later Michael Bennett — who solved the underlying equations $\binom{n}{k}=\binom{m}{l}$ one curve at a time using Baker's theory and Frey-curve/modularity techniques. The 2021 paper of Matomäki, Radziwiłł, Shao, Tao, and Teräväinen marked the entry of high-powered analytic number theory (sieves, large values, multiplicative-function methods) and is the current center of gravity; its authors and their collaborators define today's frontier. Erdős's longstanding promotion of the problem, and Guy's and Singmaster's own expository work, keep it visible in the broader number-theory and recreational-mathematics communities, but no large school is devoted to it — the uniform pointwise bound that the conjecture demands is widely regarded as requiring an essentially new idea.
