**Note on verification.** Only three works are flagged `high-confidence`: Singmaster's founding 1971 *American Mathematical Monthly* problem (row 1), the Abbott–Erdős–Hanson 1974 upper-bound paper (row 2), the Matomäki–Radziwiłł–Shao–Tao–Teräväinen 2021 breakthrough (row 21, arXiv:2106.03335), plus Guy's *Unsolved Problems in Number Theory* (row 10), which canonically catalogues the conjecture. The literature directly devoted to Singmaster's conjecture is genuinely small; the field is dominated by a handful of landmark works plus scattered Diophantine-equation papers (de Weger, Shorey–Tijdeman, Bennett) that solve *individual* cases $\binom{n}{k}=\binom{m}{l}$. Several rows above are plausible representatives of that scattered literature whose exact titles, authors, and years I could not confirm; they are flagged `ai-suggested` or `needs-verification` accordingly, and identifiers are withheld rather than invented. A human source check is required before treating any non-`high-confidence` row as established.
