# Attempts — The Chowla Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

## Sign patterns: the first foothold

The earliest substantive progress did not touch correlations directly but the related question of **sign patterns** of $\lambda$. A natural-density Chowla would force all $2^k$ sign patterns of length $k$ to occur with equal density $2^{-k}$. Hildebrand (1986) proved that all eight patterns of length $3$ occur with positive (lower) density — far short of equality but a proof that $\lambda$ does not lock into a periodic pattern. Subsequent work by Matomäki, Radziwiłł and Tao (2015–2016) used the short-interval breakthrough to show that the number of distinct sign patterns of length $k$ grows superlinearly, and that consecutive sign patterns are not too constrained. These are genuine partial results, honestly weaker than the balanced-density statement Chowla predicts.

## The Matomäki–Radziwiłł near-miss

The 2015 Matomäki–Radziwiłł theorem on multiplicative functions in short intervals is the closest the field has come to the natural-density two-point case. Combined with Tao's 2016 work, it delivers the two-point conjecture **averaged over the shift $h$**. The gap to the full conjecture — removing the average and fixing a single $h$ — is small to state but, after a decade, has resisted every attempt. It is the canonical "near-miss": everyone agrees the obstruction is the inability to localize the short-interval gain to one shift.

## Tao's logarithmic breakthrough

Tao's 2016 proof of the **logarithmically-averaged two-point Chowla conjecture** via the entropy decrement argument is the signature success of the modern era, extended by Tao–Teräväinen (2017–2018) to all odd-order correlations and to the logarithmic Sarnak conjecture in many cases. These are fully verified, peer-reviewed results. Their honest limitation: the logarithmic weighting and the restriction to **odd** orders. The natural-density conjecture and the even-order correlations remain open, and there is no consensus route from logarithmic to natural density.

## Claimed proofs and disputed announcements

The Chowla conjecture has attracted occasional preprints claiming a full resolution of the natural-density or even-order cases. As of the present writing, **no such claim has been accepted by the research community**, and the conjecture is uniformly listed as open in surveys by Tao, Teräväinen, Sarnak, Ferenczi–Kułaga-Przymus–Lemańczyk, and others. Reported objections to circulating attempts typically fall into recurring categories:

- **Parity-barrier violations.** Several elementary or sieve-based arguments implicitly claim to distinguish even- from odd-factor integers without external input, which the Selberg parity obstruction forbids; reviewers identify the precise step where the claimed cancellation cannot follow from sieve weights alone.
- **Illegitimate passage from logarithmic to natural density.** Some attempts invoke Tao's logarithmic theorem and assert the natural-density statement "follows," but the implication is false in general; the missing control of thin scales is the standard objection.
- **Uniformity gaps in Gowers-norm input.** Ergodic-theoretic attempts at even orders have stumbled on unproven higher-order inverse theorems for multiplicative functions.

In keeping with this dossier's standards, these are described neutrally: the conjecture's natural-density and even-order forms are **not** known to be proved, the logarithmic odd-order forms **are** proved (Tao; Tao–Teräväinen), and any announcement to the contrary should be checked against the published record before being relied upon.
