# Top Mathematicians — The Chowla Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Sarvadaman Chowla | 1907–1995 | originator | Stated the higher-order correlation conjecture for the Liouville/Möbius function (1965), formalizing prime-factor-parity pseudorandomness. |
| 2 | Terence Tao | contemporary | breakthrough | Proved the logarithmically-averaged two-point Chowla conjecture (2016) via the entropy decrement argument; co-developed the modern toolkit. |
| 3 | Kaisa Matomäki | contemporary | breakthrough | With Radziwiłł, proved the short-interval theorem for multiplicative functions (2015), enabling the averaged Chowla conjecture. |
| 4 | Maksym Radziwiłł | contemporary | breakthrough | Co-author of the short-interval breakthrough and of the averaged two-point Chowla result; central to the analytic side. |
| 5 | Joni Teräväinen | contemporary | frontier | With Tao, extended logarithmic Chowla to all odd-order correlations (2018) and pushed quantitative refinements. |
| 6 | Peter Sarnak | contemporary | frontier | Reformulated Möbius randomness as dynamical disjointness (2010), creating the Sarnak conjecture and the ergodic research program. |
| 7 | Nikos Frantzikinakis | contemporary | frontier | With Host, developed the ergodic-structure theory of multiplicative functions and proved cases of the logarithmic Sarnak conjecture. |
| 8 | Bernard Host | contemporary | frontier | Co-architect (with Frantzikinakis) of the characteristic-factor / nilsequence approach to logarithmic correlations. |
| 9 | Adolf Hildebrand | 20th–21st c. | breakthrough | Proved positive-density occurrence of all length-3 sign patterns of $\lambda$ (1986), the first foothold on the sign-pattern problem. |
| 10 | Jean Bourgain | 1954–2018 | breakthrough | With Sarnak and Ziegler, proved Möbius disjointness for key dynamical systems, anchoring the disjointness program. |

## Notes

The community working on Chowla is small, deeply interconnected, and sits at the interface of **analytic (multiplicative) number theory** and **ergodic theory**. The analytic wing centers on Matomäki, Radziwiłł, Tao and Teräväinen, who supply the short-interval and entropy-decrement machinery; the dynamical wing — Sarnak, Frantzikinakis, Host, Lemańczyk and collaborators (Kułaga-Przymus, Ferenczi, El Abdalaoui) — translates correlation questions into disjointness and equidistribution statements on nilmanifolds. The two wings converge through the Tao–Teräväinen equivalence results linking logarithmic Chowla and logarithmic Sarnak. Activity is sustained by regular work on the **logarithmic-to-natural-density** gap and the **even-order** correlations, both still open. The late Jean Bourgain's contributions to Möbius disjointness remain foundational reference points for the dynamical side.
