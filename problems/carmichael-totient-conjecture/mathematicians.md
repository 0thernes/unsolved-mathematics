# Top Mathematicians — Carmichael's Totient Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Robert D. Carmichael | 1907–1922 | originator | Stated the conjecture (1907); retracted the flawed proof and gave the first lower bound $>10^{37}$ (1922). |
| 2 | Victor Klee | 1947 | breakthrough | Reformulated the conjecture into a finite, checkable criterion for a minimal counterexample; basis of all later computation. |
| 3 | Carl Pomerance | 1974– | breakthrough | Proved a counterexample $m$ must satisfy $p^2\mid m$ for every prime $p$ with $(p-1)\mid\varphi(m)$ — the strongest structural constraint. |
| 4 | Kevin Ford | 1998– | breakthrough | Developed the distribution theory of totients; proved Sierpiński's multiplicity conjecture; raised the counterexample bound to $\sim 10^{10^{10}}$. |
| 5 | Paul Erdős | 1935–1945 | frontier | Early analysis of totient multiplicities and the function $V(x)$; ideas later completed by Ford for the $k\geq 2$ case. |
| 6 | Wacław Sierpiński | 1950s | frontier | Posed the companion conjecture (every multiplicity $k\geq 2$ occurs), framing where Carmichael's $k=1$ case stands apart. |
| 7 | Andrzej Schinzel | 1950s–60s | frontier | Worked on $\varphi(x)=k$ and the equation's solution counts, sharpening understanding of totient fibers. |
| 8 | Stan Wagon | 1994 | frontier | With Schlafly, computationally certified no counterexample below $\sim 10^{10^{7}}$. |
| 9 | Sergei Konyagin | 1999– | frontier | Joint work with Ford on multiplicative structure and distribution of totient values. |
| 10 | Richard K. Guy | 1981–2004 | expositor | Catalogued and popularized the conjecture in _Unsolved Problems in Number Theory_, keeping it visible across generations. |

## Notes

The problem has no large, continuously active "community" in the way the Riemann Hypothesis or twin primes do; rather, it is sustained by the **analytic and computational number theory** circle around the distribution of arithmetic functions. The decisive modern figure is **Kevin Ford**, whose 1998 work on totients is the reference frame for everything that follows and who settled the neighboring Sierpiński problem outright. **Carl Pomerance** supplies the key structural constraints, and his broader program on shifted primes ($p-1$ smooth, the prime $k$-tuples for $p-1$) is where any future breakthrough is most likely to originate. Collaborations among **Ford, Konyagin, Luca, and Pomerance** on the multiplicative structure of $\varphi$- and $\lambda$-values form the contemporary nucleus. Computational verification — extending Klee's criterion to ever-larger bounds — is carried on intermittently by various authors, but no one expects computation to resolve the conjecture; the expectation is that resolution, if it comes, will be an analytic input controlling the simultaneous prime conditions Pomerance's theorem isolates.
