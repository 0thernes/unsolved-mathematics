# Attempts — The Slice–Ribbon Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

## Confirmed families (the positive record)

The conjecture's credibility rests on a steadily growing list of families for which it is **proved true**:

- **2-bridge (rational) knots** — Lisca (2007) settled the conjecture for this entire infinite family by classifying the lens spaces that bound rational homology balls (via Donaldson diagonalization and Heegaard Floer $d$-invariants) and matching them exactly to the ribbon 2-bridge knots. This is the landmark partial result.
- **Odd 3-stranded pretzel knots $P(p,q,r)$** — Greene and Jabuka (2011) verified the conjecture, again through lattice embeddings and correction terms, with computer assistance for the finite residual cases.
- **Many Montesinos knots** — Lecuona (2012, and later work) extended confirmation to large classes of Montesinos knots, with explicit conjectural lists for the remaining sub-cases.
- **Various families via lattice/$d$-invariant methods** — Aceto and collaborators, and others, have added further infinite families (certain cables, plumbed examples) over the 2010s.

These are genuine, refereed theorems, not partial heuristics — but each is restricted to knots whose double branched covers are tractable (lens or small Seifert spaces).

## Near-misses and candidate counterexamples

- **Whitehead doubles and the topological/smooth gap.** After Freedman (1982), the **untwisted positive Whitehead double of the trefoil** and similar knots became natural suspects: many are *topologically* slice but their *smooth* sliceness is obstructed (e.g., by $\tau$, Rasmussen $s$, or $\nu^+$). Because they fail to be smoothly slice, they do **not** furnish counterexamples — but the episode sharpened the insistence on the smooth category and showed the conjecture is false-flavored topologically.
- **The square knot.** $\mathrm{Tr}\#\,\mathrm{Tr}^{*}$ (granny vs. square knot) discussions are a standard teaching example: the square knot is ribbon, illustrating how connect-sums of a knot with its mirror are always ribbon — a sanity check that constrains where counterexamples could live.
- **Algebraically slice non-slice knots.** Casson and Gordon (1975) produced algebraically slice knots that are *not* slice. These are not counterexamples (they are not slice), but they demonstrate that classical (Levine) algebra cannot decide sliceness — establishing that any attack on slice–ribbon must use 4-dimensional (gauge/Floer) input.

## Disputed or retracted claimed proofs

There is **no widely circulated, peer-reviewed claimed proof of the full conjecture** (in either direction) that the community accepts or that has produced a notable retraction controversy. The conjecture is genuinely open, and the literature is dominated by *partial confirmations*, not by claimed complete solutions. Occasional preprints have proposed proofs of special cases or sketched strategies; these have either been absorbed as legitimate partial results or quietly withdrawn without becoming celebrated disputes. The honest status is: **no accepted general proof, no accepted counterexample, and no famous retracted "solution."**

A related cautionary tale worth flagging neutrally: the conjecture is sometimes conflated in popular accounts with the broader, hard question of detecting smooth sliceness (e.g., the resolution of the **Conway knot's** non-sliceness by Piccirillo in 2020, via the trace-embedding trick). That result is about *sliceness detection*, not slice–ribbon, and does not bear directly on Fox's question — a distinction worth keeping straight when surveying "knots and the 4-ball" headlines.
