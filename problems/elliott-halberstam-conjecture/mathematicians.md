# Top Mathematicians — The Elliott–Halberstam Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

|---|------|-----|------|------------------|

## Notes

The community around EH is the **analytic number theory / sieve theory** community, with strong overlap between the bounded-gaps and the distribution-in-progressions programs. Cem Yıldırım (the third author of GPY) and Terence Tao — whose blog organized the **Polymath8** collaborations — are central figures alongside those tabulated; Tao and Maynard developed the multidimensional sieve essentially simultaneously. The Bombieri–Friedländer–Iwaniec line draws on the automorphic-forms machinery of Jean-Marc Deshouillers and Iwaniec, and on Étienne Fouvry's work on primes and divisor sums to large moduli, so the active frontier sits at the interface of sieve theory, exponential sums (Weil/Deligne bounds), and the spectral theory of automorphic forms. The field remains highly active: any uniform improvement of the level of distribution past $1/2$ would be a landmark, and the parity obstruction (Selberg) frames what such an improvement could and could not deliver.
