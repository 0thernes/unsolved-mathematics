# Top Mathematicians — Giuga's Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Giuseppe Giuga | 1950 | originator | Posed the conjecture; proved the prime direction and reduced any counterexample to the squarefree condition $p\mid(n/p-1)$ for all $p\mid n$. |
| 2 | E. Bedocchi | 1985 | breakthrough | Sharpened structural constraints and size bounds on hypothetical counterexamples, bridging Giuga's original argument to the modern treatment. |
| 3 | Takashi Agoh | c. 1990 | breakthrough | Formulated the Bernoulli-number criterion $nB_{n-1}\equiv-1\pmod n$, giving the "Agoh" half of the Agoh–Giuga conjecture. |
| 4 | Jonathan M. Borwein | 1996 | breakthrough | Co-author of the definitive 1996 analysis proving the Agoh–Giuga equivalence and the 13-prime-factor / $10^{1700}$ lower bound. |
| 5 | Peter B. Borwein | 1996 | breakthrough | Co-author of the 1996 *Monthly* paper; co-developed the theory of Giuga numbers and Giuga sequences. |
| 6 | David Borwein | 1996 | breakthrough | Co-author of the 1996 analysis; contributed to the analytic and combinatorial structure results. |
| 7 | Roland Girgensohn | 1996 | breakthrough | Co-author of "Giuga's Conjecture on Primality"; contributed to the computational and structural bounds. |
| 8 | José María Grau | 2009– | frontier | With Oller-Marcén, generalized Giuga numbers and studied the congruence $\sum k^{n-1}\equiv-1\pmod n$ and its density/finiteness questions. |
| 9 | Antonio M. Oller-Marcén | 2009– | frontier | Co-developer of generalized Giuga numbers and Giuga-type primality criteria; active on the relaxed problem. |
| 10 | Carl Pomerance | 1994 | expositor | Co-author (with Alford, Granville) of the infinitude of Carmichael numbers, the structural backdrop for any Giuga counterexample. |

## Notes

The community around Giuga's conjecture is small and stable. The historical core is Giuga himself (1950) and Bedocchi (1985); the decisive modern reorganization was the 1996 paper by the three Borweins and Girgensohn, which both unified the Agoh and Giuga formulations and set the size/structure bounds that still frame the problem. The contemporary frontier is carried largely by **José María Grau and Antonio Oller-Marcén** (and occasional collaborators), who have driven the study of generalized Giuga numbers, the relaxed combinatorial relation $\sum_{p\mid n}1/p-\prod_{p\mid n}1/p\in\mathbb Z$, and related congruences. Surrounding this is the larger Carmichael-number community — **Alford, Granville, and Pomerance** above all — whose infinitude theorem and structure theory supply the ambient context, since every Giuga counterexample must be a Carmichael number. There is no large dedicated research program; progress comes in occasional sharp papers and in computational verification that steadily raises the lower bound on any counterexample. Listed roles ("breakthrough" for the bound/equivalence results, "frontier" for the active generalization line) reflect contribution type, not relative importance.
