# Top Mathematicians — The Graceful Tree Conjecture (Ringel–Kotzig)

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Gerhard Ringel | 1960s–2000s | originator | Posed the tree-decomposition conjecture for $K_{2n+1}$ that motivated graceful labeling; founder of topological graph theory. |
| 2 | Anton Kotzig | 1960s–1990s | originator | Advocated and sharpened the strong "every tree is graceful" form; foundational decomposition and labeling theory. |
| 3 | Alexander Rosa | 1960s–present | originator | Defined $\alpha,\beta,\sigma,\rho$ valuations (1967); proved the $\beta$-valuation/cyclic-decomposition equivalence; introduced $\alpha$-labelings. |
| 4 | Solomon W. Golomb | 1970s | expositor | Coined "graceful labeling"; reframed the problem for a broad mathematical audience. |
| 5 | Martin Gardner | 1970s | expositor | Popularized graceful labelings via *Scientific American*, drawing wide attention to the conjecture. |
| 6 | Jean-Claude Bermond | 1970s–1980s | frontier | Formulated the lobster sub-conjecture; advanced graceful-labeling decomposition theory. |
| 7 | Joseph A. Gallian | 1990s–present | expositor | Author/maintainer of the *Dynamic Survey of Graph Labeling*, the field's authoritative living bibliography. |
| 8 | Brendan D. McKay | 1990s–present | frontier | With Aldred, computationally verified gracefulness of all trees up to 27 vertices; tooling (nauty) for the search. |
| 9 | Pavel Hrnčiar & Alfonz Haviar | 2000s | breakthrough | Proved all trees of diameter at most 5 are graceful — a high rung of the diameter ladder. |
| 10 | Richard Montgomery, Alexey Pokrovskiy, Benny Sudakov | 2020s | breakthrough | Proved Ringel's conjecture for large $n$ via probabilistic absorption — resolving the motivating decomposition problem (adjacent to GTC). |

## Notes

The active community is concentrated in combinatorics groups working on **graph labeling** and **graph/design decompositions**. The labeling-theory wing — descendants of the Rosa–Kotzig school, plus large groups in India, Indonesia, and Iran — continues to extend graceful and $\alpha$-labeling results to new tree families (lobsters, banana/spider trees, bounded-diameter trees) and maintains the empirical record via Gallian's continuously updated survey. A second, more recent wing comes from **extremal and probabilistic combinatorics** (Montgomery, Pokrovskiy, Sudakov; the Glock–Joos–Kühn–Osthus decomposition program; Keevash and collaborators), whose absorption and iterative-absorption methods resolved the asymptotic decomposition questions but have not yet been adapted to produce the *exact* edge-difference bijection the GTC demands. The interesting frontier is whether these two cultures can be merged — using probabilistic structure to force an exact graceful labeling on all trees, or at least on all sufficiently large trees — which most experts regard as the most plausible route to an eventual proof.
