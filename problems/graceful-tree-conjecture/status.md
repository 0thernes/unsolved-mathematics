# Status & Frontier — The Graceful Tree Conjecture (Ringel–Kotzig)

_Where the problem stands and what a resolution would require._

**Status: open — active progress.** The Graceful Tree Conjecture — every tree on $n$ vertices admits distinct vertex labels in $\{0,\dots,n-1\}$ whose induced edge differences are exactly $\{1,\dots,n-1\}$ — remains unproven and undisproven. No counterexample is known, and the empirical and structural evidence in its favor is strong, but a general proof is not in sight.

### What is known (unconditional)

- **Caterpillars** are graceful (Rosa and successors); more generally many structured families — symmetrical trees, complete binary trees, certain spiders and bananas — are graceful, often with the stronger $\alpha$-labeling.
- **Diameter ladder:** all trees of diameter $\le 5$ are graceful (**Hrnčiar–Haviar, 2001**). Diameter $\ge 6$ is open in general.
- **Computational verification:** all trees on at most 27 vertices are graceful (**Aldred–McKay, 1998–99**), extended to roughly $29$–$35$ vertices by later searches (Fang and others) with no counterexample.
- **Closure results:** large infinite families are generated by product/composition/transfer constructions (Stanton–Zarnke; Koh–Rogers–Tan; numerous $\alpha$-composition theorems).
- **Range-relaxed labelings** always exist: every tree has distinct vertex labels in some interval $[0,k]$ with $k$ only modestly larger than $n-1$ inducing distinct edge differences. Tightening $k$ to exactly $n-1$ is the open core.

### Strongest adjacent / conditional results

The motivating **Ringel Conjecture** — $K_{2n+1}$ decomposes into $2n+1$ copies of any given tree on $n+1$ vertices — was **proved for all sufficiently large $n$** by **Montgomery, Pokrovskiy, and Sudakov** (2020; arXiv:2001.02665), using probabilistic absorption. This settles the structural dream that originally motivated graceful labeling. Crucially, it does **not** imply the GTC: the decomposition is built with an absorbing/defect-tolerant method and does not exhibit a graceful labeling of each individual tree. Related decomposition thresholds (Glock–Joos–Kühn–Osthus; Keevash–Staden–Su) similarly bypass exact gracefulness.

### What a full resolution would require

A proof must produce, for **every** tree (not merely large or structured ones), an exact bijection between the edge set and $\{1,\dots,n-1\}$ realized by an integer vertex labeling in $[0,n-1]$. The two principal obstructions any approach must overcome are: (1) the **compression problem** — converting an easily obtained near-/range-relaxed labeling into one using exactly the integers $\{0,\dots,n-1\}$; and (2) **uniformity across structure** — handling trees of arbitrary diameter and degree sequence in a single argument, since every known constructive method is tied to bounded diameter, specific spine structure, or composability that generic trees lack.

### Plausible routes

The most discussed routes are: (a) **merging probabilistic absorption with exactness**, adapting the Montgomery–Pokrovskiy–Sudakov machinery to force an exact graceful labeling for all large trees, then handling small trees by the existing computer verification; (b) pushing the **diameter ladder** past 5 with a genuinely inductive scheme; (c) resolving **Bermond's lobster conjecture** as a stepping stone; and (d) an **algebraic/Nullstellensatz** certificate distinguishing graceful trees, currently the least developed. Most experts view route (a) as the likeliest eventual path, while cautioning that the gap between approximate decomposition and exact labeling has so far proven stubborn.
