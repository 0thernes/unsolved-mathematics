# Attempts — The Grand Riemann Hypothesis

No correct proof or disproof of RH — and hence of GRH — exists. What follows are the most consequential partial results, near-misses, and notable disputed claims. The pattern is consistent: every genuine advance proves something *about* zeros (counts, proportions, statistics, zero-free regions) without ever excluding a single off-line zero.

## Famous partial results

- **Hardy (1914):** infinitely many zeros of $\zeta$ on the critical line — the first toehold, later extended to Dirichlet and automorphic $L$-functions.
- **Selberg (1942):** a *positive proportion* of $\zeta$-zeros on the line, the first quantitative breakthrough.
- **Levinson (1974):** at least $34\%$ on the line, via the mollified logarithmic-derivative method; **Conrey (1989)** raised this to $>40\%$, and modern work (Bui–Conrey–Young 2011, Pratt–Robles–Zeindler–Keating 2020) to roughly $41.7\%$.
- **Korobov–Vinogradov (1958):** the widest known zero-free region for $\zeta$, the best unconditional input to prime-counting; analogues exist for $\mathrm{GL}_n$ $L$-functions but are weaker.
- **Deligne (1974):** RH *proven* in the function-field analogue (Weil conjectures) — the one setting where a Grand-RH-type statement is a theorem.
- **Rodgers–Tao (2020):** proved the de Bruijn–Newman constant $\Lambda\ge 0$; since RH $\iff \Lambda\le0$, this shows RH, if true, is true by the narrowest margin.

## Conditional cascades and approximations to GRH

- **Bombieri–Vinogradov theorem (1965):** an unconditional "GRH on average" over moduli — strong enough to substitute for GRH in many analytic-number-theory applications.
- **Density theorems** (Montgomery, Huxley, Jutila): bound the number of possible off-line zeros, severely limiting how badly GRH could fail even if it does.
- **Numerical verification:** the first $\sim 10^{13}$ zeros of $\zeta$ (Gourdon 2004, building on Odlyzko) and large initial ranges for many Dirichlet and $\mathrm{GL}_2$ $L$-functions lie exactly on the line. This is evidence, not proof.

## Disputed and withdrawn claimed proofs (stated neutrally)

Because RH/GRH attracts the most claimed proofs of any open problem, the community treats announcements with extreme caution. Notable episodes:

- **Louis de Branges** has circulated, over several decades, a program based on Hilbert spaces of entire functions claiming to prove RH (and extensions toward GRH). **Objection:** Conrey and Li (2000) exhibited specific counterexamples to structural hypotheses required by de Branges' positivity argument, showing the approach as stated cannot work for $\zeta$; the proofs are not accepted.
- **Michael Atiyah (2018)** announced a short proof of RH at the Heidelberg Laureate Forum, deriving it from a claimed "Todd function." **Objection:** the argument relied on properties of that function that were never substantiated and contradicted known behavior; mathematicians (e.g., commentary by Krzysztof Pomorski, J. C. Lagarias, and others) judged it incomplete. It is not accepted; Atiyah died months later.
- A steady stream of arXiv/vixra preprints claim elementary proofs; none has survived expert scrutiny. The general failure modes are (i) mis-applying a positivity/spectral criterion, (ii) an unjustified interchange of limits, or (iii) proving a statement strictly weaker than RH.

## Why attempts stall

The recurring obstruction is structural, not computational. Critical-line methods are capped below $100\%$; spectral and positivity reformulations are *equivalent* to GRH and merely relocate the difficulty; and the one decisive proof technique we possess — Deligne's $\ell$-adic cohomology — has no known counterpart over $\mathrm{Spec}\,\mathbb{Z}$. Until such a counterpart (or a genuinely new mechanism) appears, partial results accumulate but the line is never closed.
