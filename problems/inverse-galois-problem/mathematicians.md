# Top Mathematicians — The Inverse Galois Problem

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | David Hilbert | 1862–1943 | originator | Irreducibility Theorem (1892); realized $S_n$, $A_n$; reduced the arithmetic problem to realization over $\mathbb{Q}(t)$. |
| 2 | Emmy Noether | 1882–1935 | originator | The rationality (Noether) problem; recast realizability as rationality of invariant fields and generic extensions. |
| 3 | Igor R. Shafarevich | 1923–2017 | breakthrough | Proved every finite **solvable** group is realizable over $\mathbb{Q}$ (1954), the largest positive theorem. |
| 4 | Richard G. Swan | b. 1933 | breakthrough | Showed the Noether problem fails for $C_{47}$ (1969), separating rationality from realizability. |
| 5 | John G. Thompson | b. 1932 | breakthrough | Rigid rational triples; realized the **Monster** sporadic group over $\mathbb{Q}$ (1984). |
| 6 | B. Heinrich Matzat | b. 1949 | frontier | Systematized constructive Galois theory and the rigidity method; realized many simple and sporadic groups. |
| 7 | Michael D. Fried | b. 1942 | frontier | Hurwitz spaces and the braid/fundamental-group framework for realizing groups via moduli of covers. |
| 8 | Gunter Malle | b. 1960 | expositor | Co-author of *Inverse Galois Theory*; extensive realizations and the Malle conjecture on counting extensions. |
| 9 | David Harbater | b. 1952 | frontier | Patching methods; proof (with Raynaud) of Abhyankar's conjecture; realizations over function fields. |
| 10 | Helmut Völklein | b. 1951 | expositor | *Groups as Galois Groups*; Fried–Völklein theory linking rational points on Hurwitz spaces to realizability. |

## Notes

The active community sits at the interface of arithmetic geometry, group theory, and computational algebra. Constructive realizations and Hurwitz-space techniques continue in the German school descended from Matzat and Malle (Heidelberg, Kaiserslautern) and around Fried's braid-orbit program. Patching and "large field" methods drive a parallel current through Harbater, Florian Pop, Moshe Jarden, and collaborators (Haran, Völklein), who have effectively settled the regular problem over ample fields while $\mathbb{Q}$ resists. Adjacent work on the Malle conjecture (counting number fields by discriminant), generic polynomials (Ledet, Jensen, Yui), and the structure of $\mathrm{Gal}(\bar{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$ keeps the problem connected to mainstream arithmetic. The standout concrete target remains realizing $M_{23}$ and, more broadly, removing the dependence on rigidity for non-solvable groups.
