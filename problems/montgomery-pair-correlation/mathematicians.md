# Top Mathematicians — Montgomery's Pair Correlation Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Hugh L. Montgomery | 1970s–present | originator | Derived $F(\alpha)$, the sine-kernel pair-correlation density, and posed the conjecture (1973). |
| 2 | Freeman J. Dyson | 1960s–2020 | originator | Developed GUE/RMT level statistics; identified the random-matrix connection to Montgomery's form factor. |
| 3 | Madan Lal Mehta | 1960s–2000s | breakthrough | Co-developed the Gaussian ensembles and computed the sine-kernel correlations underlying the GUE prediction. |
| 4 | Andrew M. Odlyzko | 1980s–present | breakthrough | Massive numerical verification of GUE statistics for zeros up to height $\sim10^{22}$ (Montgomery–Odlyzko law). |
| 5 | Peter Sarnak | 1990s–present | breakthrough | With Rudnick, extended to all $n$-level correlations; with Katz, the symmetry-type philosophy. |
| 6 | Zeev Rudnick | 1990s–present | breakthrough | Co-proved $n$-level correlations of $L$-function zeros match GUE for restricted support. |
| 7 | Nicholas M. Katz | 1990s–present | breakthrough | Proved function-field RMT analogues via monodromy and Deligne equidistribution. |
| 8 | Daniel A. Goldston | 1980s–present | frontier | Goldston–Montgomery equivalence with prime variance in short intervals; pair-correlation analysis. |
| 9 | Jonathan P. Keating | 1990s–present | frontier | Keating–Snaith CUE moment model; lower-order arithmetic corrections to correlations. |
| 10 | Nina C. Snaith | 1990s–present | frontier | Co-developed the random-matrix model for moments and statistics of $\zeta$ and $L$-functions. |

## Notes

The active community sits at the intersection of analytic number theory and random matrix theory. Beyond the figures above, sustained contributions come from J. Brian Conrey and the AIM/CFKRS group (David Farmer, Michael Rubinstein, and collaborators) on moments and the ratios conjecture; Henryk Iwaniec, Wenzhi Luo, and Sarnak on low-lying zeros in families; and Eugene Bogomolny and Keating on semiclassical/lower-order corrections. Steven Gonek, Kannan Soundararajan, Maksym Radziwiłł, and others work on moments, gaps between zeros, and conditional consequences, while the broader Katz–Sarnak "symmetry types" program continues to organize the subject. Numerical and heuristic work (Odlyzko-style computations, RMT modeling) remains tightly coupled to the analytic effort, keeping pair correlation a live testing ground for the spectral interpretation of the Riemann zeros.
