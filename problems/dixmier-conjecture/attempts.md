# Attempts — The Dixmier Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

## Special-case confirmations

No counterexample has ever been found, and the conjecture has been verified in numerous restricted settings, which collectively constitute the strongest positive evidence.

- **Filtered / graded endomorphisms.** Endomorphisms compatible with the Bernstein filtration (so that the associated graded map is a well-behaved endomorphism of the polynomial Poisson algebra) are surjective. This handles the "tame" or low-complexity regime descending from Dixmier's own 1968 analysis and Joseph's automorphism work.
- **Small-degree images.** If $\varphi(p)$ and $\varphi(q)$ have bounded degree under the standard filtration, surjectivity follows from explicit computation. The difficulty is always the absence of any a-priori degree bound on a hypothetical counterexample.
- **Endomorphisms fixing extra structure** (e.g. preserving a grading, a $*$-involution, or a specific holonomic module class) are automorphisms by direct argument.

## The Tsuchimoto and Belov-Kanel–Kontsevich equivalence (a near-miss of a different kind)

The most important "attempt" was not an attempt to *prove* the conjecture but to *locate* it. Tsuchimoto (2005, full account 2008) and Belov-Kanel–Kontsevich (2007) independently established that the stable Dixmier conjecture is equivalent to the stable Jacobian conjecture. This is a genuine theorem — not disputed — and it is a near-miss only in the sense that it converts the problem into another open problem rather than resolving it. Its effect on the field was decisive: after 2007, attacking Dixmier directly without engaging the Jacobian conjecture is generally regarded as unpromising.

## Claimed proofs of the Jacobian conjecture (relevant by transfer)

Because of the equivalence, any claimed proof of the Jacobian conjecture would bear on Dixmier, and the Jacobian conjecture has a notably long record of erroneous announcements. These should be treated neutrally and as **not accepted**:

- Numerous purported proofs of the two-dimensional Jacobian conjecture have circulated since the 1980s; the community consensus is that none is correct, and several contained identifiable gaps (e.g. unjustified degree or Newton-polygon control). None has been accepted by referees as complete.
- Wenhua Zhao and others have produced substantial *partial* and reformulation results (e.g. via the vanishing of higher-order traces, the Mathieu–Zhao subspace framework, and the image conjecture), which sharpen the problem but do not close it.

Because no accepted proof of the Jacobian conjecture exists, the transfer yields no accepted proof of Dixmier.

## Direct claimed proofs of the Dixmier conjecture

Occasional preprints have claimed direct resolutions of $\mathrm{DC}_1$. As of the present frontier, **none has been accepted** by the mathematical community, and where such preprints have been examined, objections have centered on:

- unverified degree or filtration bounds smuggled in as hypotheses,
- mis-handling of the non-filtered case (exactly the hard case),
- arguments that, if correct, would also prove the Jacobian conjecture by the known equivalence — a red flag prompting close scrutiny that the claims have not survived.

In the interest of honesty: this dossier does not endorse any specific named claimed proof. The status remains **open**, with strong special-case evidence in favor and no counterexample, but no complete, refereed, community-accepted resolution in any dimension — including the original case $n=1$.

## Net assessment

The pattern is consistent: partial results accumulate, equivalences clarify the difficulty, and every full claim either dissolves under scrutiny or silently assumes the missing degree control. The "easy" structural cases were essentially settled early; the residual difficulty is concentrated precisely in the non-tame, unbounded-degree regime that also defeats the Jacobian conjecture.
