# Top Mathematicians — The Dixmier Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Jacques Dixmier | 1968– | originator | Posed Problème 1 in *Sur les algèbres de Weyl* (1968): is every endomorphism of $A_1$ an automorphism? Established the structural facts (simplicity, domain) that frame the question. |
| 2 | Ott-Heinrich Keller | 1939– | originator | Originated the Jacobian conjecture (1939), the commutative twin to which Dixmier's problem was later proven equivalent. |
| 3 | Yoshifumi Tsuchimoto | 2000s | breakthrough | Proved the equivalence of the stable Dixmier and Jacobian conjectures via $p$-curvature / positive-characteristic Weyl algebras (2005, full version 2008). |
| 4 | Maxim Kontsevich | 2005– | breakthrough | With Belov-Kanel, gave an independent proof of the Dixmier–Jacobian stable equivalence and launched the symplectomorphism/tropical-limit program for $\mathrm{Aut}(A_n)$. |
| 5 | Alexei Belov-Kanel | 2005– | breakthrough | Co-author of the equivalence theorem and driving force of the long-running program on Weyl-algebra automorphisms and endomorphisms. |
| 6 | Anthony Joseph | 1970s | breakthrough | Developed the automorphism and primitive-ideal theory of $A_1$, clarifying the structure exploited in special-case confirmations. |
| 7 | Arno van den Essen | 1990s– | expositor | Authoritative monograph and surveys on the Jacobian conjecture, organizing the reductions that now govern the Dixmier problem. |
| 8 | Wenhua Zhao | 2000s– | frontier | Introduced the vanishing/image conjectures and Mathieu–Zhao subspaces, reframing the Jacobian (hence Dixmier) problem in operator-trace terms. |
| 9 | Jie-Tai Yu | 2010s | frontier | With Belov-Kanel and Elishev, advanced the augmentation-topology and lifting program connecting symplectomorphisms to Weyl-algebra endomorphisms. |
| 10 | Andrey Elishev | 2010s | frontier | Co-developed the approximation/augmentation-topology results aimed at the $\mathrm{Aut}(A_n)\cong\mathrm{Aut}(P_n)$ conjecture and the Dixmier problem. |

## Notes

The active community around the Dixmier conjecture is small and tightly coupled to the Jacobian-conjecture community, a consequence of the 2005–2007 equivalence theorems. The most sustained current effort is the **Belov-Kanel–Kontsevich–Elishev–Yu** program, which studies the group of polynomial symplectomorphisms, augmentation topologies, and a conjectural tropical (deformation-quantization) limit, hoping to control endomorphisms of $A_n$ through the symplectic side. A second, largely independent strand is **Wenhua Zhao's** Mathieu–Zhao-subspace and image-conjecture framework, which recasts the underlying invertibility question in terms of vanishing traces of differential operators. Surrounding both is the broader **Jacobian-conjecture community** (in the lineage of Bass–Connell–Wright and van den Essen), whose reductions and degree-estimate machinery transfer directly. Because no counterexample exists and every full proof attempt has so far failed or reduced to the still-open Jacobian conjecture, the field's mood is one of cautious structural progress rather than imminent resolution; activity is steady but modest, concentrated in a handful of seminars and the arXiv preprints of these groups.
