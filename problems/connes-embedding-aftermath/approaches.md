# Approaches — The Connes Embedding Problem (Resolved 2020)

_Major strategies, partial results, and barriers._

Because CEP was **resolved negatively in 2020**, the major "approaches" split into two eras: (i) the pre-2020 lines that sought either a proof of universal embeddability or a translation that would expose a counterexample, and (ii) the post-2020 frontier, which works out consequences and attacks the live successor questions. The decisive blow came from outside operator algebras entirely.

## Matricial / microstates approximation (Voiculescu free probability)

**Core idea.** A II$_1$ factor embeds in $R^\omega$ iff its generators admit *matrix microstates*: tuples of finite matrices whose mixed moments approximate the algebra's trace functionals. Voiculescu's free entropy $\chi$ and free entropy dimension are built precisely to count and constrain such microstates.

**Best result.** Free probability proved embeddability for large natural classes — free group factors $L(\mathbb{F}_n)$, free products of embeddable algebras, and any algebra generated by a "matricially approximable" family — and produced powerful invariants (free entropy obstructions to property Gamma, absence of Cartan subalgebras). It established that *most algebras people could write down* were embeddable.

**Barrier.** Microstate methods are inherently *constructive and positive*: they confirm embeddings but offer no mechanism to certify non-embeddability, since proving $\chi = -\infty$ or empty microstate spaces does not contradict embedding. The framework could not, even in principle, produce the needed counterexample.

## Kirchberg's QWEP / tensor-norm reformulation

**Core idea.** Kirchberg (1993) proved CEP equivalent to the **QWEP conjecture** (every $C^*$-algebra is a quotient of one with the weak expectation property) and to the equality of minimal and maximal tensor norms on $C^*(\mathbb{F}_\infty) \otimes C^*(\mathbb{F}_\infty)$. This moved the problem into the theory of operator spaces and exactness.

**Best result.** Reduced CEP to a sharply stated $C^*$-algebra question and supplied the bridge to local operator-space techniques (Pisier, Haagerup, Junge–Pisier). It is the reformulation that ultimately connected to quantum correlations.

**Barrier.** No internal method within $C^*$-tensor theory yielded a separating example; the relevant tensor norms resisted direct computation, and the conjecture's truth value remained genuinely open until imported complexity-theoretic input settled it.

## Tsirelson's problem / quantum correlation sets

**Core idea.** Bipartite quantum correlations come in two flavors: $C_{qa}$ (limits of finite-dimensional/tensor-product strategies) and $C_{qc}$ (commuting-operator strategies). CEP is equivalent to $C_{qa} = C_{qc}$. Thus a *physically meaningful gap* between two models of measurement would refute CEP.

**Best result.** Junge–Navascués–Palazuelos–Pérez-García–Scholz–Werner and Fritz (2011) made the equivalence precise. Slofstra (2017–2019) then proved the strict-inequality phenomenon was reachable: $C_q$ is **not closed**, and group-embeddability questions arising from non-local games are **undecidable**.

**Barrier turned breakthrough.** This is the line that succeeded — but only once it absorbed the computational machinery below. Pure quantum-information arguments narrowed the gap without closing the problem.

## Interactive proofs and undecidability ($\mathrm{MIP}^* = \mathrm{RE}$)

**Core idea.** Encode the halting problem into multiprover interactive games so that the maximum entangled value distinguishes halting from non-halting machines. If the *tensor* and *commuting* values could always be made to agree (CEP), the commuting value would be computable from above and the entangled value from below, yielding decidability — contradicting an undecidable problem.

**Best result — the resolution.** Ji, Natarajan, Vidick, Wright, Yuen (2020) proved $\mathrm{MIP}^* = \mathrm{RE}$, forcing $C_{qa} \neq C_{qc}$ and refuting Tsirelson's problem, the QWEP conjecture, and **CEP** in one stroke. This is a *negative result*: it certifies non-embeddability indirectly, via complexity, exactly where microstate methods were blind.

**Barrier (now on the successor questions).** The proof is non-constructive about the witness: it guarantees *some* non-hyperlinear-type obstruction without exhibiting an explicit non-embeddable factor or a concrete separating correlation. Closing that effectiveness gap is itself a barrier — analogous in spirit to the way undecidability results give existence without construction.

## Model-theoretic / continuous logic approaches

**Core idea.** Treat $R^\omega$ via continuous model theory: embeddability into $R^\omega$ is governed by the existential theory $\mathrm{Th}_{\exists}(R)$. CEP is equivalent to $R$ being existentially closed / to a single universal theory absorbing all II$_1$ factors.

**Best result.** Goldbring–Hart and collaborators recast CEP, and after 2020 derived that $\mathrm{Th}(R^\omega)$ is **not computable** and that the universal theory of II$_1$ factors is complicated — clean logical corollaries of $\mathrm{MIP}^* = \mathrm{RE}$.

**Barrier.** Model theory now describes the *shape* of the failure but does not yet hand over an explicit, finitely describable non-embeddable factor — the central open quantitative problem of the aftermath.
