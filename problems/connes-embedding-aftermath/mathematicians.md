# Top Mathematicians — The Connes Embedding Problem (Resolved 2020)

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Alain Connes | 1976– | originator | Posed the embedding conjecture within his Fields-Medal classification of injective factors; framed $R^\omega$ as the universal matricial limit. |
| 2 | Eberhard Kirchberg | 1990s | breakthrough | Proved CEP equivalent to the QWEP conjecture and to a $C^*(\mathbb{F}_\infty)$ tensor-norm identity, opening the operator-space route. |
| 3 | Dan-Virgil Voiculescu | 1985– | breakthrough | Founded free probability and free entropy; supplied microstate methods that confirmed embeddability for free group factors and broad classes. |
| 4 | Narutaka Ozawa | 2000s–2010s | expositor | Authored the canonical surveys mapping CEP $\Leftrightarrow$ QWEP $\Leftrightarrow$ Tsirelson; clarified that one counterexample topples all formulations. |
| 5 | Reinhard F. Werner | 2008– | frontier | With Scholz formulated Tsirelson's problem precisely, linking quantum correlation models to operator-algebra embeddability. |
| 6 | William Slofstra | 2016–2019 | frontier | Proved $C_q$ non-closed and undecidability of group-embeddability from non-local games — the decisive crack toward the refutation. |
| 7 | Thomas Vidick | 2012–2020 | breakthrough | Co-author of MIP* = RE; leading expositor of how interactive-proof complexity refutes CEP. |
| 8 | Henry Yuen | 2020 | breakthrough | Co-author of MIP* = RE; contributed to the compression/introspection architecture of the proof. |
| 9 | Anand Natarajan / Zhengfeng Ji / John Wright | 2020 | breakthrough | Co-authors of MIP* = RE establishing the halting-problem encoding that forces $C_{qa}\neq C_{qc}$. |
| 10 | Isaac Goldbring | 2010s–2020s | expositor | Recast CEP via continuous model theory; with Hart drew out logical consequences of its failure (non-computable $\mathrm{Th}(R^\omega)$). |

## Notes

The active community spans three traditions that the problem fused. **Operator algebraists** (in the lineage of Connes, Kirchberg, Ozawa, Pisier, Haagerup, Junge) continue to study QWEP's residual status, hyperlinearity of groups, and the structure of ultrapowers. **Quantum-information theorists** (Werner, Navascués, Pérez-García, Palazuelos, Coladangelo, Stark) pursue the correlation-set geometry, non-local games, and self-testing. **Complexity and logic** (Vidick, Yuen, Natarajan, Ji, Wright; Slofstra; Goldbring, Hart, Gao, Farah, Sherman in continuous model theory) drive the post-2020 frontier. The unifying open problem — an *explicit* non-hyperlinear group / effective non-embeddable factor — keeps all three groups in active dialogue, with workshops at IHÉS, the Fields Institute, and BIRS regularly convening them.
