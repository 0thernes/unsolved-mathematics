# Status & Frontier — The Hodge Conjecture

_Where the problem stands and what a resolution would require._

**Status: OPEN.** A Clay Mathematics Institute Millennium Prize Problem (\$1,000,000), unresolved in every codimension $p \ge 2$. No accepted proof or refutation of the rational, smooth-projective statement exists.

## What is known (unconditional)

- **Codimension 1 is settled:** the Lefschetz $(1,1)$-theorem realizes every integral $(1,1)$-class as a divisor class. This is the only complete case.
- **Algebraicity of Hodge loci** (Cattani–Deligne–Kaplan, 1995; reproved via tame geometry by Bakker–Klingler–Tsimerman, 2020): the locus where a Hodge class stays Hodge is algebraic — a prediction of the conjecture, confirmed without producing cycles.
- **Abelian varieties:** Deligne proved all Hodge classes there are **absolutely Hodge** (1982); the Hodge conjecture itself holds for many — but not all — abelian varieties (those with Hodge ring generated by divisors; numerous Mumford–Tate cases). Weil-type classes remain open.
- **Special families:** $K3$ and abelian surfaces, low-degree hypersurfaces and complete intersections, Fermat varieties of certain degrees, and various explicit Calabi–Yau threefolds are verified by exhibiting cycles directly.
- **Hard ceilings:** the **integral** Hodge conjecture is false (Atiyah–Hirzebruch, 1962; Kollár; Totaro), and the **Kähler** extension is false (Voisin, 2002) — pinning the live statement to *rational* coefficients on *projective* varieties.

## Strongest conditional results

- The **Lefschetz standard conjecture** (Grothendieck) would deliver major structural pieces (algebraicity of Künneth and Lefschetz operators).
- The **normal-function singularity program** (Green–Griffiths; Brosnan–Fang–Nie–Pearlstein) reduces the conjecture to producing singularities of admissible normal functions; the supporting algebraicity (zero loci) is proved, but the construction of cycles is not.
- **Motivated cycles** (André) realize many Hodge classes with cycle-like properties, conditionally bridging part of the gap.

## What a full resolution would require

A proof must supply, for an *arbitrary* Hodge class on an arbitrary smooth projective $X$, an **actual algebraic cycle** (a $\mathbb{Q}$-combination of subvarieties) with that cohomology class. The central difficulty is the **structural scarcity of cycle constructions** in codimension $\ge 2$: cohomology offers no general recipe for producing subvarieties. Unlike sieve theory's parity barrier or complexity's relativization, there is no identified *logical* obstruction — but also no known engine of cycles. A disproof, conversely, would need a *projective* variety with a *rational* Hodge class provably not algebraic; all current counterexamples evade projectivity or rationality.

## Plausible routes

1. **Tame geometry / o-minimality** pushing beyond algebraicity of Hodge loci toward effective cycle construction.
2. Settling the **standard conjectures** or the Tate conjecture in enough generality to transfer.
3. **Normal functions:** proving the predicted singularities exist.
4. New **motivic or derived-category** constructions producing high-codimension cycles.
