# Attempts — The Hodge Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

## Established partial results (genuine progress, not full proofs)

- **Lefschetz $(1,1)$-theorem (Lefschetz, 1924).** The codimension-1 case is a full theorem; it is the historical anchor and the reason the conjecture seemed plausible.
- **Abelian varieties (Deligne, 1982).** Deligne proved every Hodge class on an abelian variety is **absolutely Hodge**. The Hodge conjecture itself is known for many abelian varieties — e.g. when the Hodge ring is generated by divisors, and for various Mumford–Tate configurations — but **not all**; **Weil-type** abelian varieties with exotic Hodge classes remain open.
- **Cattani–Deligne–Kaplan (1995).** Hodge loci are algebraic. Widely viewed as the strongest unconditional structural evidence; it confirms a prediction of the conjecture without producing cycles.
- **$K3$ and abelian surfaces.** The conjecture holds for $K3$ surfaces and abelian surfaces (Hodge classes in $H^2$ are divisor classes by Lefschetz $(1,1)$, and there are no higher non-trivial codimensions to test).
- **Hypersurfaces and complete intersections.** For low degrees/dimensions the Hodge classes are spanned by the hyperplane class and its powers; the conjecture holds trivially or via explicit cycles in many such families.
- **Shioda, Schoen, and others** verified the conjecture for specific families (Fermat hypersurfaces of certain degrees, products of curves, some Calabi–Yau threefolds) by explicitly exhibiting the cycles.

## Conditional reductions and equivalences

- **Standard conjectures (Grothendieck).** The Lefschetz standard conjecture would settle major structural pieces; it remains open but is a recognized, respectable line.
- **Normal-function singularity program (Green–Griffiths; Brosnan–Fang–Nie–Pearlstein).** Reduces the Hodge conjecture to the existence of singularities of admissible normal functions. The supporting algebraicity inputs (zero loci of normal functions) were proved, but the reduction does not by itself yield a proof — the predicted singularities have not been produced in general.

## Disputed / withdrawn / unverified claimed proofs

The Hodge conjecture, being a Millennium Prize Problem, attracts purported proofs; **none has been accepted by the mathematical community**, and several have been formally withdrawn or refuted. Stated neutrally:

- Numerous **arXiv preprints** claiming a general proof have appeared over the years (e.g. periodically in the math.AG listings). In every case to date, experts have identified gaps — most commonly an unjustified leap from a Hodge class being *cohomologically* or *motivically* distinguished to it being represented by an *actual algebraic cycle* — and the claims have not survived refereeing. Because these are not published, peer-reviewed results and the specific authors' claims have not been validated, the Atlas does **not** endorse any of them.
- The Clay Mathematics Institute requires publication in a refereed venue and a two-year waiting period before a prize claim is considered; **no claim has reached that bar.**

The honest summary: there are deep, genuine partial results and several precise equivalent reformulations, but **no correct proof, and no widely credited near-miss that the community treats as "almost there."** The principal obstruction is not a known logical barrier (unlike, say, the parity barrier in sieve theory or relativization in complexity) but the structural **scarcity of constructions producing high-codimension algebraic cycles** from cohomological data.
