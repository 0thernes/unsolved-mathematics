# Top Mathematicians — The Caccetta–Häggkvist Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Louis Caccetta | 1978– | originator | Co-posed the conjecture in *On minimal digraphs with given girth* (1978); framed the directed-girth extremal question. |
| 2 | Roland Häggkvist | 1978– | originator | Co-posed the conjecture; extremal-graph-theory instinct for the exact tight threshold $n/k$. |
| 3 | Václav Chvátal | 1980s | breakthrough | With Szemerédi, proved every digraph of min out-degree $d$ has a cycle of length $\le n/d + 2500$, the first general near-proof. |
| 4 | Endre Szemerédi | 1980s | breakthrough | Co-author of the 1985 additive-constant theorem; brought heavy combinatorial machinery to the problem. |
| 5 | Jian Shen | 2000s | breakthrough | Proved the triangle case under min out-degree $\ge 0.3532\,n$ (2003), a landmark sharpening toward $1/3$. |
| 6 | Paul Seymour | 2000s | frontier | Championed the triangle case; linked it to the Second Neighbourhood Conjecture and structural digraph theory. |
| 7 | Maria Chudnovsky | 2000s | frontier | With Seymour and Sullivan, proved structural/local results on cycles in dense digraphs. |
| 8 | Blair D. Sullivan | 2000s | expositor | Authored an influential survey of the conjecture and proved local/edge-removal results on triangle-free digraphs. |
| 9 | Daniel Král' | 2000s–2010s | frontier | With Hladký and Norin, drove the flag-algebra bound to $\approx 0.3465\,n$, the current best for $k=3$. |
| 10 | Alexander Razborov | 2000s | frontier | Created the flag-algebra framework that underlies the strongest unconditional triangle-case bounds. |

## Notes

The active community sits at the intersection of **extremal/structural digraph theory** and **combinatorial optimization via flag algebras**. The Princeton/structural school around Paul Seymour and Maria Chudnovsky keeps the triangle case and its Second-Neighbourhood cousin in view, while the flag-algebra lineage (Razborov; Hladký, Král', Norin; and successors using semidefinite programming) supplies the strongest numerical bounds. Additional momentum comes from additive combinatorics (Hamidoune, Nathanson and the Cayley-digraph formulation) and from researchers such as Nicolas Lichiardopol working on disjoint-cycle and degree-constrained variants. Survey work by Sullivan and Bondy has kept the problem's many equivalent forms accessible. The consensus expectation is that the full conjecture, and even the triangle case, will require a genuinely new global idea: the present techniques appear to plateau just above the conjectured constant $1/3$.
