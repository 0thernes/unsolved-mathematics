> Note on the shortfall: I have listed 20 rows rather than 25. The moving sofa problem has a small genuine primary literature — essentially Moser (origin), Hammersley (reformulation), Gerver (the record shape), Romik and Kallus–Romik (modern reconstruction and the best rigorous upper bound), and Baek (the 2024 claimed optimality proof), surrounded by survey/problem-collection appearances. Rather than pad the table with fabricated titles, DOIs, or non-existent works, I stopped at the entries I can vouch for as real or plausibly real, flagging uncertain ones as "needs-verification" or "ai-suggested." The four entries most central and certain are Moser (#1), Gerver (#5), Kallus–Romik (#13), and Baek (#15). Several identifiers are left as "—" deliberately; the arXiv ids for Kallus–Romik (#14) and Baek (#15) and the DOI for Romik (#12) should be confirmed against the source before being treated as established.
