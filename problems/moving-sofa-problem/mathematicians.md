# Top Mathematicians — The Moving Sofa Problem

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|-----------------|
| 1 | Leo Moser | 1960s | originator | Posed the moving sofa problem (1966); defined the unit-corridor, right-angle, area-maximization formulation. |
| 2 | J. M. Hammersley | 1960s | breakthrough | Reformulated via the corridor-intersection/envelope picture; gave the bound $\pi/2 + 2/\pi \approx 2.2074$ and upper bound $2\sqrt 2$. |
| 3 | Joseph L. Gerver | 1990s | breakthrough | Constructed the 18-piece optimal-candidate shape (1992), area $\approx 2.2195$ — the standing lower-bound record. |
| 4 | Dan Romik | 2010s–present | frontier | Re-derived Gerver's sofa with symbolic methods (2017); solved the ambidextrous variant; co-proved the $2.37$ upper bound. |
| 5 | Yoav Kallus | 2010s | frontier | With Romik, proved the rigorous computer-assisted upper bound $\mu \le 2.37$ (2018). |
| 6 | Jineon Baek | 2020s–present | frontier | Posted the 2024 preprint claiming a proof that Gerver's sofa is optimal ($\mu = \mu_G$), now under verification. |
| 7 | Hallard T. Croft | 20th c. | expositor | Co-author of *Unsolved Problems in Geometry*; helped canonize the problem in the research-problem literature. |
| 8 | Kenneth J. Falconer | 20th c.–present | expositor | Co-author of *Unsolved Problems in Geometry*, a standard reference framing the problem. |
| 9 | Richard K. Guy | 20th c. | expositor | Co-author of *Unsolved Problems in Geometry*; chronicler who kept the problem in circulation. |
| 10 | Philip Gibbs | 2000s–2010s | frontier | Independent investigator of numerical bounds and variant ("ambidextrous") configurations, sharpening intuition for the optimum. |

## Notes

The active community for the moving sofa problem is small but currently energized. For most of its life the problem had no full-time specialists; progress came in isolated bursts from Hammersley, then Gerver, then a roughly two-decade pause until **Dan Romik** revived it around 2016–2018 with modern symbolic and certified-numerical tools, joined by **Yoav Kallus** on the upper-bound side. The 2024 preprint by **Jineon Baek** claiming optimality of Gerver's sofa has concentrated attention sharply: the open task now is community verification of that argument rather than the search for a larger shape. The problem also retains a healthy "amateur-accessible" following — its statement is elementary enough that independent contributors (e.g. Philip Gibbs) have produced genuine numerical and variant results — and it remains a fixture of recreational-mathematics exposition (Numberphile, MathWorld, problem collections). The expositor lineage of Croft, Falconer, and Guy ensured the problem never disappeared from the canon during its quiet decades.
