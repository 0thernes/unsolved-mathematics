## Notes

The active community around the UGC is small, deeply interconnected, and centered in theoretical computer science rather than classical mathematics, though it draws heavily on analysis, probability, and geometry. The current frontier is driven by the group around the **2-to-2 / Grassmann** breakthrough — Khot, Minzer, Safra, and collaborators such as Guy Kindler and Irit Dinur — who are pushing to lift completeness toward $1$. In parallel, the **Sum-of-Squares** school (Barak, Steurer, Raghavendra, and younger researchers including Pravesh Kothari and Tselil Schramm) probes whether constant- or polynomial-round SDP hierarchies might refute the conjecture instead. Bridging figures such as Oded Regev, Guy Kindler, and Madhur Tulsiani connect the hardness and algorithmic camps. The field's small size means progress tends to arrive in concentrated bursts (2004–2005, 2008, 2010, 2016–2018), each reorganizing expert opinion about whether UGC is ultimately true or false.
