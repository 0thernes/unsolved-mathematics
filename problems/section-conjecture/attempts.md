# Attempts — Grothendieck's Section Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

The section conjecture has resisted every direct assault for over four decades, but a number of partial results, conditional theorems, and instructive negative findings mark the terrain. Unlike some famous open problems, it has *not* attracted a steady stream of claimed full proofs; the difficulty is widely respected, so most work is honestly partial.

**Verified partial successes.** The injectivity ("at most one point per section") direction is fully established for hyperbolic curves over number fields, drawing on Nakamura, Tamagawa, and Mochizuki's anabelian rigidity. The **real section conjecture** is a complete theorem (Mochizuki; independently Pál, 2011), giving a precise correspondence over $\mathbb{R}$ including "sections at infinity". The **birational $p$-adic section conjecture** was proved by Jochen Koenigsmann (2005), a genuine landmark, and substantially strengthened over large fields by Florian Pop. These are near-misses only in the sense that they solve carefully chosen variants while the curve-level number-field statement stays open.

**Conditional reductions.** Hélène Esnault and Olivier Wittenberg (2009–2010) showed that surjectivity of the section map is closely tied to finiteness of the Tate–Shafarevich group $\Sha$ of the Jacobian and to cycle-theoretic conjectures; under such hypotheses one obtains partial geometricity. This is a reduction rather than a proof — it relocates the difficulty into other deep unproven statements.

**Counterexamples to over-optimistic forms.** A productive line has been *disproving* strengthenings. Hoshi exhibited explicit examples bearing on minimalist versions, and the metabelian/abelianized section map is known to fail to be injective or surjective in general — confirming that one cannot prove the conjecture by truncating $\pi_1$ to small quotients. Kirsten Wickelgren's work on $n$-nilpotent obstructions (Massey products) produced concrete non-geometric sections detectable at finite nilpotency level, sharpening intuition about which sections can be ruled out and which resist all known invariants.

**Anabelian "sections at the boundary".** Stix and others clarified that affine (punctured) curves admit *cuspidal* sections coming from the missing points and from decomposition groups at the boundary; early hopes that all sections of affine curves are geometric had to be tempered by carefully accounting for these. This is not a retracted claim but a correction of naïve expectation that reshaped the affine formulation.

**Disputed / unaccepted frameworks.** Shinichi Mochizuki's inter-universal Teichmüller (IUT) theory, while not a claimed proof of the section conjecture per se, sits in the same anabelian milieu and aims at deep Diophantine statements (the $abc$ conjecture). The IUT papers remain **disputed**: a substantial part of the community, following the 2018 objections of Peter Scholze and Jakob Stix to a key inequality (Corollary 3.12), does not accept the arguments, while Mochizuki and collaborators maintain the proof is correct. No part of this dispute amounts to a claimed resolution of the section conjecture, but it is the most prominent controversy adjacent to the field and is noted here neutrally: the section conjecture itself is regarded by essentially all experts as **open**.

**Status of "near-proofs".** There is, as of the present frontier, no widely circulated full proof — correct or refuted — of the section conjecture over a number field. The honest summary is that the problem has yielded its local, real, and birational shadows but not its global substance.
