# Attempts — The abc Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

## The Mochizuki claim (2012–present) — disputed

The dominant episode in the conjecture's history is **Shinichi Mochizuki's** 2012 announcement of a proof via **Inter-universal Teichmüller theory** (IUT), a body of work spanning roughly 500 pages across four papers ("Inter-universal Teichmüller Theory I–IV"). Mochizuki, a number theorist of high standing (Princeton PhD under Faltings, professor at the RIMS, Kyoto), claimed to prove a Szpiro-type inequality strong enough to yield abc, the Vojta conjecture for hyperbolic curves, and effective Mordell.

The work resisted assimilation. Multiple workshops (Oxford 2015; Kyoto 2016) failed to transmit the proof to the broader community; experts reported being unable to locate the load-bearing inferential step in concrete terms. The crux is **Corollary 3.12** of IUT III. In March 2018, **Peter Scholze and Jakob Stix** visited Kyoto and afterward circulated a manuscript, *"Why abc is still a conjecture,"* arguing that the inequality of Corollary 3.12 is unjustified: in their reconstruction, the identifications used to relate the deformed copies of the number field cause the claimed quantitative gain to vanish, leaving at most a trivial bound. Mochizuki responded with detailed rebuttals asserting that Scholze–Stix had over-simplified — specifically, that they collapsed distinct labelled copies that IUT must keep distinct — and thereby misread the theory.

The dispute remains **unresolved**. The papers were published in *Publications of the RIMS* in 2020/2021, but because Mochizuki is the journal's chief editor and the review occurred within his home institute, publication did not confer the independent verification the community expects. The neutral statement of record: the proof is **not accepted as established** by the mainstream arithmetic-geometry community, and the specific Scholze–Stix objection to Corollary 3.12 has not been answered to that community's satisfaction. The abc conjecture is therefore still regarded as open. (The institute classifies its status as a "disputed-claim" precisely to capture this state of affairs.)

## Near-misses and genuine partial results

- **Stewart–Yu (1991, 2001).** The strongest *unconditional* statement: $\log c \ll_\varepsilon \mathrm{rad}(abc)^{1/3+\varepsilon}$ via $p$-adic linear forms in logarithms. A real theorem, but exponentially weaker than the conjecture.
- **Mason–Stothers (1981/1984) and the holomorphic abc.** Complete proofs of the *function-field* and *complex-analytic* analogues. These show the conjecture is "morally true" and isolate exactly which tool (a derivative/Wronskian) the integer case lacks.
- **Elkies (1988).** Not an attempt on abc but a landmark consequence: abc $\Rightarrow$ effective Mordell, dramatizing the stakes.

## Erroneous and withdrawn elementary "proofs"

As with many famous, easily stated conjectures, abc has attracted numerous short purported proofs and disproofs, almost always claiming an elementary inequality or a counterexample. The most-cited cautionary point is structural: any "proof" with $\varepsilon=0$ is *necessarily wrong*, because infinitely many triples have quality $q>1$ — the constructive families (e.g. those exploiting $2^{6n}\equiv 1 \pmod{9}$) furnish explicit counterexamples to the $\varepsilon$-free statement. Claimed counterexamples to the full conjecture, by contrast, have never withstood the extensive computational record (ABC@home and successors), which verifies abc for all triples below large bounds and locates the genuine extremal triples, of which the record quality is only $\approx 1.6299$.
