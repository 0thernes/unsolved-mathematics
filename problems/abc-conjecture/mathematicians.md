## Notes

The active community sits at the intersection of arithmetic geometry, transcendence theory, and Diophantine analysis. Three currents are visible. First, a small group around **Mochizuki** at the RIMS (Kyoto), together with collaborators and expositors such as **Ivan Fesenko**, **Yuichiro Hoshi**, and **Go Yamashita**, continues to develop, defend, and explain IUT. Second, a critical mainstream — anchored by **Peter Scholze** and **Jakob Stix**, and broadly the arithmetic-geometry community trained in perfectoid and $p$-adic Hodge-theoretic methods — maintains that the proof's Corollary 3.12 is unjustified and that abc remains open. Third, an independent strand led by **Kirti Joshi** is attempting alternative "arithmetic Teichmüller" foundations that might either reconstruct or supersede the IUT machinery. Surrounding all of this, **Andrew Granville**, **Michel Waldschmidt**, **Andrew Sutherland**, and the computational community (descendants of the ABC@home / Reken-mee-met-ABC projects) keep the empirical and expository record current. The result is a field in unusual epistemic tension: a high-profile claimed proof coexisting with a widely held view that the central inequality is still conjectural.
