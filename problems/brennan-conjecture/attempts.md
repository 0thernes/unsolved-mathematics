# Attempts — Brennan's Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

Brennan's conjecture has the unusual profile of a sharply stated problem with *no* prominent retracted "proofs" in the literature — its difficulty has channeled effort into incremental, honestly-bounded improvements rather than claimed full solutions. The notable work is a sequence of near-misses that push the provable exponent toward $4$ (equivalently, the universal-spectrum bound $B(-2)$ toward $1$).

## Incremental exponent improvements

The dominant mode of progress has been raising the largest $p_0 < 4$ for which $\int_\Omega |\varphi'|^p\,dA < \infty$ is proven for all simply connected $\Omega$, via upper bounds on the universal integral means spectrum $B(t)$.

- **Carleson–Makarov (1994)** and related analyses bounded the universal spectrum and the dimension of harmonic measure, giving partial integrability well short of the endpoint but establishing the modern framework.
- **Bertilsson (1999, thesis)** obtained explicit numerical bounds on $B(t)$ across a range of $t$, including near $t=-2$, sharpening the provable exponent. These remain reference values for the best rigorous estimates.
- **Hedenmalm–Shimorin (2005)** introduced a weighted-Bergman / heat-flow method yielding one of the strongest unconditional lower bounds on the admissible exponent; widely regarded as a genuine near-miss in that it advanced the threshold more than any single prior step, yet still stalled before $4$.
- **Beliaev–Smirnov (2005–2010)** computed and estimated the integral means spectrum for random and conformally dynamical (Julia-set, SLE-type) families, producing numerical values strongly consistent with $B(-2)=1$ and lower bounds approaching it.

## Numerical and extremal-domain evidence

Extensive computation on candidate extremal domains — snowflake-type self-similar boundaries and Julia sets where harmonic measure's multifractal spectrum is accessible — has repeatedly returned values consistent with the conjectured endpoint and *never* exceeded it. Such experiments (Kraetzer's conjecture that $B(t)=t^2/4$ for $|t|\le 2$, and subsequent simulations) are near-misses in the evidential sense: they make $B(-2)=1$ overwhelmingly plausible but certify nothing about the universal supremum over all univalent functions.

## Partial and conditional results

- The **lower endpoint** ($p>4/3$) and the full sub-critical range up to the best provable $p_0$ are theorems, not conjecture.
- Several results are **conditional** on the universal-spectrum conjecture $B(t)=t^2/4$ for $|t|\le 2$: granting it, Brennan's conjecture follows immediately at $t=-2$. Thus Brennan's conjecture is a corollary of a single, widely believed but unproven, sharp identity.
- Conversely, **large-$|t|$ obstructions** (Pommerenke; Kayumov-type lower bounds showing $B(t) > t^2/4$ for sufficiently large $|t|$) prove the parabolic law cannot hold globally, so any solution must locate $t=-2$ inside the parabolic regime — a near-miss-style constraint that has not been turned into a proof.

## Disputed or retracted claims

No widely circulated claimed full proof of Brennan's conjecture has survived scrutiny, and the dossier records none as established. Occasional preprints asserting the sharp endpoint have appeared but have not been accepted into the refereed literature or reproduced; in the absence of a verifiable, peer-validated argument, this dossier treats the conjecture as **open** and does not attribute resolution to any author. Readers encountering a solution claim should check for refereed publication and independent confirmation before relying on it.
