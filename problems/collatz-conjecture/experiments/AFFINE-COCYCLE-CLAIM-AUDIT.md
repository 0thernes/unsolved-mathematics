## Claims under audit

- **(C1)** "Repulsion events" — high-alignment odd steps where the alignment `A = v2(n+1)` changes — are a dynamical discovery; their counts ("0 for trivial, 14 for 27, 22 for 703, …, 224 for 71-bit") and rate (">0.98 on large examples") evidence a repulsion mechanism.
- **(C2)** The mechanism is ε-sensitive and positive-cone specific: repulsion forces any would-be survivor to drop odd-density below θ, so S ∩ ℤ>0 = ∅, and would break nontrivial cycle equations.
- **(C3)** V = M − λ·log1p(D) − μ·(A/(d+1)) is a candidate Lyapunov function; its strict-decrease rate ("62%–98%, increasing with size") evidences forced descent.
- **(C4)** The tracked D approximates the affine cocycle defect c_d / 2^d.
- **(Status bullet)** "(P1-P7) ⇒ S ∩ ℤ>0 = ∅ for positives (uniform escape O(b)). Only tiny finite scan gap + formal carry lemma left."

## Findings

### F1 — The detector is a tautology (kills C1)

For **every** odd v of either sign, T(v) + 1 = (3v+3)/2 = 3(v+1)/2, hence **v2(T(v)+1) = v2(v+1) − 1 exactly, on every odd step, at every alignment** — the alignment to −1 decays by precisely one bit per odd step, deterministically, with no dependence on sign, defect, or carry behavior. The analyzer's fire condition (`alignment >= 3 and post_align != alignment`) is therefore satisfied on 100% of high-alignment odd steps and is logically equivalent to `alignment >= 3` alone.

Worse, the sketch's causal story is backwards: alignment A to −1 forces the next A−1 shortcut steps to be **odd** (a density-1 burst that mimics the boundary path), and even steps appear only after the alignment is exhausted. "Repulsion inserts even parities where the −1 path would continue with 1s" describes the opposite of what the arithmetic does.

Measured: 633,444 high-alignment odd steps checked (exhaustive odd v < 2^20 plus 2,000,000 random 96-bit odd integers of both signs): fired 633,444, fire fraction **1.0**, post-alignment equal to A−1 in every case, zero counterexamples. The fire condition is logically equivalent to `alignment >= 3` alone; a "repulsion event" is definitionally a visit to an odd residue ≡ −1 mod 8, nothing more. The sketch's headline event counts are reproduced exactly by that trivial counter: 27 → **14**, 703 → **22**, 71-bit example (2358909599867980429759, 1027 steps, 603 odd) → **224**. All three published figures are visit counts, and the statistic contains no dynamical information beyond orbit length and alignment distribution.

Three instrument defects found in passing: **(a)** `detect_repulsion_events` breaks at `current <= 1` and `simulate_positive_lift_repulsions` skips `n < 2`, so the analyzer has never actually executed on a negative integer — the sketch's implicit "positive-cone specificity" was never tested against the cone where it fails; **(b)** at true valuation ≥ 65 the 64-cap makes pre- and post-alignment both read 64 and the detector silently does not fire (latent bug, irrelevant in the tested range); **(c)** the `--frontier-hard-r` population labeled "d=30 hard frontier rs" is just the Mersenne numbers 2^j − 1, j = 1…30, with five small lifts — not a sample of the ≈2^28.5 actual frontier residues at depth 30, so the label oversells the tested population.

### F2 — The mechanism fails the repo's own sibling control (kills C2)

Under n ↔ −n, shortcut 3n+1 on negatives is conjugate to shortcut 3n−1 on positives — the exact control system SIBLING-CONTROL proved mandatory. Measured on the negative cone:

- **−17 cycle** (members −17, −25, −37, −55, −82, −41, −61, −91, −136, −68, −34): period d = 11, o = 7, odd-density 7/11 ≈ 0.636 > θ = 0.6309 (**supercritical**), alignments 4 (−17), 3 (−25), 3 (−41), and the detector *predicate* fires **exactly 3 times per period, forever** (the shipped script itself refuses negative starts — see F1 defect (a) — so this is the predicate applied to the orbit, which is the mathematically relevant object) — while the multiplier debt **increases** by 7·log₂3 − 11 = +0.0947 per period, diverging to +∞. An eternal supercritical periodic orbit whose debt grows forever despite indefinite repulsion events falsifies "repulsion events force odd-density below θ / drive multiplier debt negative" in the strongest possible way. Nothing in the sketch's divergence mechanism (sections 2–3) uses positivity of the orbit value, so nothing in it distinguishes this orbit from a positive one.
- **−5 cycle** (members −5, −7, −10): period 3, odd-density 2/3 > θ (**supercritical**), max alignment **2** — the detector **never fires**. The mechanism is thus also incomplete: bounded-alignment supercritical families are invisible to any −1-alignment instrument. This spine is exactly the `x ≡ 59 mod 64` growth gate already isolated in [`LOW-ALIGNMENT-STRUCTURE.md`](LOW-ALIGNMENT-STRUCTURE.md).
- **Positive lifts** n = 2^k − 5 ride the −5 spine detector-silently while supercritical for ~k steps (measured silent supercritical prefixes: 18, 52, 62, 109, 198 at k = 20, 40, 60, 100, 200; growing trend, non-monotone in k — small-k cross-checks give 3, 6, 14, 21, 18 at k = 5…20). Detector-silent supercritical prefixes are unbounded as k → ∞ **in the positive cone itself**.

### F3 — The potential's descent is an instrument artifact (kills C3)

The minimizer computes D = |c|/2^min(d,60): the denominator **freezes at 2^60** while c grows like 3^o, so beyond d = 60 the term −λ·log1p(D) decreases by ≈ λ·ln 3 ≈ 2.75 on every odd step (swamping the M increase of log₂3 − 1 ≈ 0.585) and M decreases by 1 on every even step. V therefore strictly decreases on essentially every step past d = 60 **regardless of what the orbit does**.

Implementation disclosure: the audit's figures use **exact big-integer c with the instrument's 2^min(d,60) freeze retained** — i.e., the minimizer's own V, computed without float failure. (The minimizer's float c overflows to `inf` past 646 odd steps for 3n±1 and 442 for 5n+1; this corrupts none of its published Collatz figures, whose largest example has o = 603, but it makes the float instrument unable to evaluate exactly the long-orbit regime the Lyapunov claim targets — with float c the control rates below would be spuriously deflated by NaN deltas.) Measured:

| orbit | behavior | strict-decrease rate | rate d ≤ 60 | rate d > 60 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Collatz 27 | converges | 0.623 | 0.559 | **1.0** |
| Collatz 703 | converges | 0.785 | 0.610 | **1.0** |
| Collatz 63,728,127 | converges | 0.966 | 0.661 | **1.0** |
| Collatz 71-bit hard | converges | 0.983 | 0.712 | **1.0** |
| 5n+1 from 7 | **grows ×2.4·10⁷³** | 0.982 | 0.542 | **1.0** |
| 5n+1 from 13 | grows | 0.982 | 0.542 | **1.0** |
| 3n−1 from 5 | **cycles forever** | 0.992 | 0.610 | **1.0** |

The sketch's "62%–98%, increasing with size" is reproduced exactly by the Collatz rows and is explained entirely by the d ≤ 60 / d > 60 regime mix: the zero-free-parameter mixture `frac_post·1.0 + (1−frac_post)·rate_pre` reproduces all four of the minimizer's own reported rates to three decimals (27 → 0.623 at frac_post 0.145; 703 → 0.785 at 0.449; 35655 → 0.877 at 0.709; 71-bit → 0.983 at 0.942). The per-step arithmetic is likewise closed-form: past d = 60, even steps give ΔV ≤ −1 unconditionally and odd steps give ΔV ≈ 0.585 − λ·ln3 ≈ −2.16 whenever D > 0.1518 (D ≫ 1 by d = 60 on every tested orbit). The same V descends at the same rate on a diverging sibling orbit and on a periodic sibling orbit; V → −∞ on the 5→7→10 cycle. **A quantity that tends monotonically to −∞ on convergent, divergent, and periodic orbits alike is not a Lyapunov function for anything; its decrease is mechanical (−λ(o·ln3 − 60·ln2) + o(1)), not dynamical.**

Two robustness notes. **(i)** The post-60 rate is exactly 1.0 in this audit's implementation and in the independent T3 verification (532/532, 1440/1440, 2940/2940); a third independent implementation (fairness referee) measured 0.9936 on the 5n+1 control from a different alignment-term handling on even steps — immaterial to the conclusion under either reading. **(ii)** Removing the freeze entirely (fully exact D = c/2^d) collapses the strict-decrease rates to 0.49–0.67 on all three maps, with the *divergent* 5n+1 orbit lowest (0.49) — so the "repaired" potential separates nothing either; there is no nearby fix.

### F4 — The tracked c is not the affine cocycle (kills C4)

If n_d = (3^o·n₀ + c)/2^d, the exact intercept obeys: even step c → c, odd step **c → 3c + 2^d**. The invariant n_d·2^d = 3^o·n₀ + c was verified exactly along test orbits. The analyzer instead updates c → 3c + 1, which is off by an unboundedly growing factor (already 2.8–4.4× by d = 40). The instruments never measured the object the sketch reasons about.

## What survives (steelman findings)

- **Correct targeting.** The ε-barrier motivation (P6) stands: proofs must consume the intercept, and this sketch is the repo's only attack aimed squarely at c_d. That framing insight is real — but it is recalled prior work, not new to the sketch. The instruments then fail to consume the intercept they aim at (F4).
- **One genuine positivity theorem — for the cycle half only.** The exact identity n_d·2^d = 3^o·n₀ + c_d with c_d > 0 (ε = +1) plus n₀ > 0 gives: a positive start on a supercritical word satisfies n_d > (3^o/2^d)·n₀ → ∞, so positive supercritical orbits grow — no positive cycle above the critical line. This is exactly the (P4)/sibling dichotomy already proved in-repo. For n₀ < 0 the identity is satisfied with bounded n_d (the −17 cycle), and for divergence it says nothing, since divergence *is* n_d → ∞.
- **The alignment-burn ladder is exact** — T^k(2^a·q − 1) = 3^k·2^(a−k)·q − 1 — but it is the already-proved high-alignment fact, it is what the detector tautologically counts (F1), and it holds identically for negative values, so it cannot separate the positive cone.
- **Positivity is mentioned but never consumed.** The sketch's one positivity-flavored sentence in the divergence chain — "its binary representation is a finite cylinder" — is the correct germ of a real distinction: positive integers are the *finite-support* points of ℤ₂, while every known alignment-regenerating supercritical spine (−1, −5, −17, and every negative-cycle word) is *cofinite-support*. No lemma, inequality, or measured quantity downstream consumes this; the detector, the potential, and every reported number compute identically on negative-value orbits. Making finite support do actual work is the honest reformulation of the open problem here.
- **Constraint inherited by any future "carry lemma":** it must **(i)** consume value-positivity (finite support) essentially in the divergence half, and **(ii)** control every bounded-alignment supercritical spine, not just the −1 boundary, with a quantitative statement about when events must recur. F2 proves the lemma cannot have the stated form (alignment deviation ⇒ eventual density drop), since alignment deviation is unconditional (F1) and coexists with eternal supercriticality (−17).
- The remaining measurements not covered by F1–F4 (exit depths, debt crossings, cert flags, final M values) are real but generic: final M is negative for *any* terminating orbit, and the exit depths duplicate the escape-envelope measurements (P7). Nothing mechanism-specific survives.

## Verdict

Fairness note, first: the sketch's own Status line does hedge ("Not a complete formal proof … requires formalization of carry lemma"), and §3 discloses the 62%–98% range honestly. Those hedges are credited. But the same document contradicts them ("The conjecture is resolved in outline", "AI did it", §4 asserting the divergence contradiction as established), and the status.md bullet stripped even the hedge into an unconditional "(P1-P7) ⇒ S ∩ ℤ>0 = ∅ … Only tiny finite scan gap + formal carry lemma left." The overclaim verdict lands primarily on that bullet, secondarily on the sketch's §4/§6 assertions.

**The status.md claim "(P1-P7) ⇒ S ∩ ℤ>0 = ∅ … only tiny finite scan gap + formal carry lemma left" is withdrawn.** The affine-cocycle repulsion idea is demoted to: a restatement of finite 2-adic alignment, currently carrying no evidential support from its instruments (tautological detector, artifact potential, mis-tracked cocycle), and refuted as a mechanism by the sibling/negative-cone controls. The live theorem targets remain those of [`ALIGNMENT-DICHOTOMY.md`](ALIGNMENT-DICHOTOMY.md) and [`LOW-ALIGNMENT-STRUCTURE.md`](LOW-ALIGNMENT-STRUCTURE.md): a low-alignment quick-descent lemma (repeat-gate induction on the −5 spine) and a high-alignment descent lemma with an honest carry analysis.
