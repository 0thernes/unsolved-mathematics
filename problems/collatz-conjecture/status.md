# Status & Frontier — The Collatz Conjecture

_Where the problem stands and what a resolution would require._

**Status: OPEN.** No proof or disproof exists. The conjecture has been verified computationally for all $n < 2^{71} \approx 2.36 \times 10^{21}$ (Bařina, 2025, continuing the earlier Oliveira e Silva / Bařina line of distributed verification), and no counterexample — neither a divergent orbit nor a nontrivial cycle — has ever been found.

## What is known (unconditional)

- **Almost-all descent (density).** Terras (1976) and Everett (1977): natural density $1$ of integers have finite stopping time.
- **Counting bound.** Krasikov–Lagarias (2003): $\#\{n \le x : n \to 1\} \gg x^{0.84}$.
- **Tao's theorem (2019).** For any $f(n)\to\infty$, almost all $n$ (in logarithmic density) satisfy $\min_k T^k(n) < f(n)$ — "almost all orbits attain almost bounded values." This is the strongest known unconditional result and the current high-water mark. Reference: Terence Tao, *Almost all orbits of the Collatz map attain almost bounded values*, arXiv:1909.03562 (2019).
- **Cycle constraints.** Nontrivial cycles, if any, must be enormously long and are excluded for small "cycle complexity" $m$; Hercher (2023, arXiv:2201.00406, *J. Integer Seq.* **26**, art. 23.3.5) proves there are no Collatz $m$-cycles with $m \le 91$, extending the Steiner / Simons–de Weger line of linear-forms-in-logarithms and continued-fraction arguments. Hercher further shows that verifying convergence below $3 \cdot 2^{69}$ raises the minimum number of odd terms in any nontrivial cycle to $\ge 1.375 \times 10^{11}$; Bařina's $2^{71}$ verification (2025) exceeds that threshold, so the $1.375 \times 10^{11}$ odd-term floor holds unconditionally as an immediate corollary of the two results.

## What is known (conditional / heuristic)

The standard probabilistic model treats each odd step as multiplying by roughly $3/4$ on average (one $3n+1$ followed by an expected two halvings), giving multiplicative drift $\big(\tfrac{3}{4}\big)$ per cycle and hence almost-sure descent. This heuristic predicts that **(a)** no orbit diverges and **(b)** the only cycle is trivial — but it is a model, not a proof, and applies to typical rather than specific integers.

## What a full resolution would require

A complete proof must close two independent gaps simultaneously:

1. **No divergent orbit.** Upgrade Tao's *logarithmic-density, almost-bounded* statement to a *every-integer, genuinely-bounded* statement. This means controlling the thin exceptional set that all density methods discard — precisely where a divergent trajectory could hide.
2. **No nontrivial cycle.** Exclude cycles of *all* lengths, not just bounded complexity. Present methods (computation + transcendence bounds) only push the threshold; an infinite family needs a structural argument, plausibly a new Diophantine input controlling $2^\ell - 3^k$ uniformly.

A disproof would require exhibiting either a cycle or a provably divergent orbit — both ruled out below $2^{71}$, so any counterexample is astronomically large or structurally subtle.

The remaining hard subcase is therefore the retimed-pressure class: prove directly from event timing why these `5,677` positive-pressure transitions keep zero prefix deficit even when parent-to-child total-credit delta is insufficient.
- **Retimed-pressure timing audit** (in-repo, [`experiments/BRANCH-PREFIX-RETIMED-PRESSURE.md`](experiments/BRANCH-PREFIX-RETIMED-PRESSURE.md)): the `5,677` retimed-pressure transitions were traced to the first child prefix where required credit exceeds the parent's requirement. Result: `0` trace deficits, minimum threshold surplus `2`, max threshold lag `19`, and every case was prepaid by visible structural credit: `5,565` by prior high-ladder credit and `112` by prior low-repeat credit. This gives the next exact local lemma: a retimed-pressure child receives high-ladder or low-repeat credit before above-parent pressure appears.
- **Sharper transcendence theory** on $\log_2 3$ to settle the cycle problem unconditionally.
- **Ergodic / $2$-adic rigidity** results that transfer measure-theoretic typicality to the (measure-zero) integers.

The sobering backdrop is the undecidability of *generalized* Collatz maps (Conway; Kurtz–Simon), which warns that any sufficiently general technique must fail — a proof must exploit the specific arithmetic of $3$ and $2$. Erdős's assessment, *"mathematics is not yet ready for such problems,"* remains the field's honest summary.

**Formalization frontier.** The [Collatz Conjecture Challenge](https://ccchallenge.org/) project ("formalising the Collatz literature, one paper at a time") maintains a 369-entry literature database and is machine-verifying the key partial results, with Tao's 2019 theorem among the formalizations in progress (as of mid-2026: 1 of 361 papers completed). Machine-checked partial results will not close the conjecture, but they harden the foundation any future proof must build on — and give claimed proofs a venue for adversarial verification.
