## Note on this table

The genuinely canonical, certainly-real works on this problem are few: **Fisher (1996)** ("Squaring a tournament: a proof of Dean's conjecture", *Journal of Graph Theory*), **Havet–Thomassé (2000)** on median orders, **Chen–Shen–Yuster** on the $\approx 0.657$ constant-factor bound, and the textbook treatment by **Bang-Jensen and Gutin** (*Digraphs: Theory, Algorithms and Applications*). These four are marked high-confidence in attribution. The remaining rows reflect the real shape of the literature — a patchwork of subclass-verification papers by authors such as **Salman Ghazal**, **Moussa Daamouch**, and **Fidler–Yuster**, plus problem lists and surveys — but exact titles, years, and venues for several of them are uncertain, so they are marked "needs-verification" and carry no fabricated identifier. Where a specific title or author could not be confidently reconstructed, the cell is left generic rather than invented. No DOI or arXiv number is asserted unless reliably known; here none could be asserted with confidence, so all identifiers read "—" per the project's honesty rule (a real famous paper with "—" is preferred over a fabricated number).
