# Top Mathematicians — P versus NP

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Kurt Gödel | 1906–1978 | originator | 1956 letter to von Neumann anticipating the search-vs-verification question for proof-finding. |
| 2 | Stephen A. Cook | b. 1939 | originator | Cook–Levin theorem (1971): $\mathrm{SAT}$ is $\mathrm{NP}$-complete; defined polynomial-time reducibility; Turing Award 1982; authored the Clay problem statement. |
| 3 | Leonid A. Levin | b. 1948 | originator | Independent universality theorem for search problems (1973); founder of average-case completeness. |
| 4 | Richard M. Karp | b. 1935 | originator | "Reducibility Among Combinatorial Problems" (1972): 21 $\mathrm{NP}$-complete problems; Turing Award 1985. |
| 5 | Jack Edmonds | b. 1934 | originator | Articulated the polynomial-time = "good algorithm" thesis (1965), the precursor concept of $\mathrm{P}$. |
| 6 | Alexander A. Razborov | b. 1963 | breakthrough | Monotone circuit lower bounds (1985); co-discoverer of the natural-proofs barrier (1994). |
| 7 | Steven Rudich | b. 1961 | breakthrough | Co-author of "Natural Proofs" (1994), delimiting which lower-bound techniques can possibly work. |
| 8 | Avi Wigderson | b. 1956 | frontier | Algebrization barrier (2008); pervasive contributions to derandomization and circuit complexity; Turing Award 2023 (Abel Prize 2021). |

## Notes

The contemporary community spans several overlapping schools. Circuit and proof complexity remains active around figures such as Razborov, Williams, Rahul Santhanam, Avishay Tal, and Igor Carboni Oliveira, with recent excitement around "hardness magnification" and the meta-complexity of the Minimum Circuit Size Problem ($\mathrm{MCSP}$). The Geometric Complexity Theory program continues under Mulmuley, with sharp obstruction-theoretic results from Peter Bürgisser, Christian Ikenmeyer, and Greta Panova that have constructively narrowed what GCT can deliver. Scott Aaronson and Lance Fortnow are the field's leading expositors, periodically surveying the status and the barrier landscape. The broad consensus—reflected in repeated informal polls of theorists—is that $\mathrm{P} \neq \mathrm{NP}$, but no one expects a proof imminently; the working assumption is that genuinely new, non-relativizing, non-naturalizing, non-algebrizing mathematics will be required.
