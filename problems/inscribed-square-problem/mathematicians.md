# Top Mathematicians — The Inscribed Square Problem (Toeplitz)

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Otto Toeplitz | 1911 | originator | Posed the inscribed square conjecture in 1911 and proved the convex case; gave the problem its name and framing. |
| 2 | Arnold Emch | 1913–1916 | breakthrough | First rigorous proofs: smooth convex curves and certain piecewise-analytic curves, via median/chord constructions. |
| 3 | Lev Schnirelmann | 1929 | breakthrough | Proved the conjecture for sufficiently smooth ($C^2$-type) curves using a parity (mod-2) count of inscribed squares. |
| 4 | Heinrich Guggenheimer | mid-20th c. | expositor | Clarified and corrected the smoothness hypotheses in the Schnirelmann-type parity argument. |
| 5 | R. P. Jerrard | 1961 | breakthrough | Established inscribed squares for analytic curves via a transversality/parity argument. |
| 6 | Walter Stromquist | 1989 | breakthrough | Proved the conjecture for locally monotone curves (all piecewise-$C^1$ curves) — a major general result. |
| 7 | Mark J. Nielsen | 1990s–2000s | expositor | Results on inscribed quadrilaterals and triangles; widely cited surveys "Figures inscribed in curves." |
| 8 | Benjamin Matschke | 2014 | frontier | Authoritative modern survey; conditional results via equivariant topology of configuration spaces. |
| 9 | Terence Tao | 2017 | frontier | Integration/Lipschitz-graph approach proving a square-peg theorem for unions of Lipschitz graphs. |
| 10 | Joshua Greene & Andrew Lobb | 2020–2021 | frontier | Symplectic-geometry breakthrough: every smooth Jordan curve inscribes rectangles of all aspect ratios (hence a square). |

## Notes

The active community is small but vibrant and currently centered on the symplectic and topological frontier opened by **Joshua Greene** (Boston College) and **Andrew Lobb** (Durham), whose Lagrangian-torus method settled the smooth rectangular peg problem and triggered a wave of follow-up work on cyclic quadrilaterals and aspect-ratio questions. **Cole Hugelmeyer** advanced the knot-theoretic/$\mathbb{R}^4$-embedding viewpoint, and **Richard Schwartz** has pursued the structure of inscribed rectangles in general (non-smooth) Jordan loops, probing exactly the wild-curve regime where the conjecture remains open. **Benjamin Matschke** and **Terence Tao** remain key reference points for the configuration-space and analytic approaches respectively. The historical pillars — Toeplitz, Emch, Schnirelmann, Jerrard, and Stromquist — define the special-case ladder that any general proof must ultimately subsume. The open problem now is essentially a single hard step: removing all smoothness and controlling the degeneration of inscribed squares for arbitrary continuous curves.
