# Top Mathematicians — The Kakeya Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Sōichi Kakeya | 1886–1947 | originator | Posed the 1917 needle-rotation problem that names the field. |
| 2 | Abram S. Besicovitch | 1891–1970 | originator | Constructed measure-zero sets with segments in all directions (1928); founder of geometric measure theory. |
| 3 | Roy O. Davies | 1927–2019 | originator | Proved planar Kakeya sets have Hausdorff dimension $2$ (1971); fixed the dimensional formulation. |
| 4 | Charles Fefferman | b. 1949 | breakthrough | Disproved the ball multiplier conjecture (1971) via a Besicovitch set, embedding Kakeya in harmonic analysis. |
| 5 | Jean Bourgain | 1954–2018 | breakthrough | Bush argument and $\tfrac{n+1}{2}$ bound (1991); arithmetic method for Kakeya (1999); restriction links. |
| 6 | Thomas Wolff | 1954–2000 | breakthrough | Hairbrush bound $\tfrac{n+2}{2}$ (1995); finite-field model problem; foundational surveys. |
| 7 | Nets Hawk Katz | b. 1973 | breakthrough | Katz–Łaba–Tao $\mathbb{R}^3$ bound; Katz–Tao arithmetic method; Guth–Katz polynomial method; Katz–Zahl $\mathbb{R}^3$ progress. |
| 8 | Terence Tao | b. 1975 | breakthrough | Additive-combinatorial Kakeya bounds; multilinear Kakeya/restriction (with Bennett–Carbery); expositor of the circle of ideas. |
| 9 | Zeev Dvir | b. ~1980 | breakthrough | Proved the finite-field Kakeya conjecture (2009) via the polynomial method. |
| 10 | Larry Guth | b. 1977 | breakthrough | Endpoint multilinear Kakeya (2010); polynomial partitioning (with Katz); $k$-broad restriction estimates. |

Additional shaping figures (beyond the table of ten) include **Izabella Łaba** (Katz–Łaba–Tao; surveys), **Joshua Zahl** (Katz–Zahl and the 2025 $\mathbb{R}^3$ resolution), **Hong Wang** (2025 $\mathbb{R}^3$ resolution, projection theory), **Jonathan Bennett** and **Anthony Carbery** (multilinear Kakeya), and **Antonio Córdoba** (early Kakeya maximal-function geometry).

## Notes

The active community is small, elite, and centered on harmonic analysis and incidence geometry. Its current frontier is defined by the **Wang–Zahl** program, which combined sticky-Kakeya reduction, Bourgain-style projection theorems, Furstenberg-set estimates, and induction on scales to settle $n=3$ in 2025. Key contemporary contributors and adjacent workers include Hong Wang, Joshua Zahl, Nets Katz, Larry Guth, Ciprian Demeter, Pablo Shmerkin and Tuomas Orponen (projection/Furstenberg theory), and Xiumin Du, Ruixiang Zhang, and Shukun Wu (restriction/decoupling). The torch passed from the Bourgain–Wolff generation to a younger cohort fluent in the polynomial method, decoupling (Bourgain–Demeter), and quantitative projection theory; their immediate target is extending the three-dimensional resolution to $n\ge 4$.
