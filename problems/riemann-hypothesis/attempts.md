# Attempts — The Riemann Hypothesis

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

RH attracts more claimed proofs than perhaps any other open problem; the great majority come from outside the research community and are never published in refereed venues. The items below are the historically significant partial results, genuine near-misses, and a small number of prominent claims that were retracted or remain disputed. The dispute summaries are stated neutrally with the standing objection.

## Genuine partial results and near-misses

- **Hardy (1914)** — infinitely many zeros on the critical line. A real theorem, not a proof of RH, but the first hard evidence that the line is special.
- **Selberg (1942)** — a *positive proportion* of zeros on the line. Selberg later remarked on the difficulty of converting positive proportion into a full result.
- **Levinson (1974)** — more than $\tfrac13$ of zeros on the line, via the zeros of $\zeta'$; **Conrey (1989)** raised this to over $40\%$. These remain the strongest results of their kind; the $50\%$ barrier has never been crossed.
- **Beurling–Nyman / Báez-Duarte** — reduced RH to an $L^2$ approximation problem, a clean equivalent that has driven much numerical work without yielding a proof.
- **Rodgers–Tao (2020)** — proved the de Bruijn–Newman constant $\Lambda\ge0$, showing that if RH holds it does so with no margin to spare; this is a theorem about the *shape* of the problem, not a resolution.

## Disputed or unverified claims

- **Louis de Branges (multiple announcements, 1990s–2010s)** — de Branges, who legitimately solved the Bieberbach conjecture in 1984, has repeatedly circulated lengthy manuscripts claiming to prove RH (and GRH) via his theory of Hilbert spaces of entire functions. **Objection:** Conrey and Li (2000) constructed explicit counterexamples to positivity conditions that de Branges's approach appeared to require, and the broader community regards the proofs as not establishing RH; the work has not been accepted in peer review. The dispute is technical, not resolved in de Branges's favor.
- **Michael Atiyah (2018)** — at the Heidelberg Laureate Forum, Atiyah announced a short proof of RH by contradiction, using a function he called the "Todd function" arising from work on the fine-structure constant. **Objection:** mathematicians who examined the five-page preprint found the central construction undefined or inconsistent and the argument non-rigorous; the claim was not accepted, and Atiyah died in early 2019 without the proof being validated.
- **Various "elementary" or physics-inspired claims** — numerous announcements (e.g., reductions to operator positivity, to specific inequalities, or to physical Hamiltonians) appear yearly. As a rule none has survived scrutiny; the absence of a refereed, community-accepted proof is the operative fact.

## Why attempts fail

Recurring failure modes: (i) proving a *positive proportion* or *density* statement and tacitly assuming it upgrades to "all zeros"; (ii) reducing RH to an equivalent positivity/density criterion (Weil, Li, Nyman–Beurling) and then asserting the equivalent without proof; (iii) importing the finite-field proof template without supplying the missing arithmetic geometry over $\mathbb{Q}$; and (iv) constructing a candidate Hilbert–Pólya operator without proving self-adjointness or that its spectrum is the zeta zeros. Each route reformulates the hard core rather than removing it.
