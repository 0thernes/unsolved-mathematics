# Top Mathematicians — The Total Coloring Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Mehdi Behzad | 1960s– | originator | Introduced the total graph $T(G)$ and total chromatic number; conjectured $\chi''(G)\le\Delta+2$ in his 1965 thesis |
| 2 | Vadim G. Vizing | 1960s–2017 | originator | Independently posed the conjecture; his edge-coloring theorem ($\chi'\le\Delta+1$) is its direct ancestor |
| 3 | Gary Chartrand | 1960s– | originator | Co-developer of the total-graph framework; co-author of the first exact total-chromatic computations (1967) |
| 4 | Moshe Rosenfeld | 1970s– | breakthrough | Proved the cubic case $\Delta=3$ ($\chi''\le 5$), 1971 |
| 5 | Alexandr V. Kostochka | 1970s– | breakthrough | Settled $\Delta=4$ and $\Delta=5$; co-author of the List Total Coloring Conjecture |
| 6 | Michael Molloy | 1990s– | breakthrough | With Reed, proved $\chi''(G)\le\Delta+C$ for an absolute constant via the probabilistic method (1998) |
| 7 | Bruce Reed | 1990s– | breakthrough | Co-author of the constant-slack theorem; advanced the local-lemma/$\omega,\Delta,\chi$ program |
| 8 | Oleg V. Borodin | 1980s– | frontier | Drove the planar program (TCC for planar $\Delta\ge 9$, then $\ge 7$); co-author of list total coloring |
| 9 | Douglas R. Woodall | 1990s– | frontier | Co-author of the List Total Coloring Conjecture and list edge/total coloring results |
| 10 | Hian Poh Yap | 1980s–1990s | expositor | Authored the standard monograph *Total Colourings of Graphs* (LNM 1623); proved high-degree cases |

## Notes

The total-coloring community overlaps heavily with the broader graph-coloring and probabilistic-combinatorics communities. The classical "exact small-degree" lineage runs through Behzad, Rosenfeld, and especially **Kostochka**, who remains active and influential. The asymptotic/probabilistic strand is anchored by **Molloy and Reed**, whose 1998 theorem and accompanying monograph *Graph Colouring and the Probabilistic Method* are the field's central modern references; their school (and successors working on entropy compression and local-lemma sharpenings) continues to chip at the additive constant. The **planar and sparse-graph** front is dominated by Borodin, Kostochka, and Woodall and by an active group of Chinese combinatorialists (e.g. discharging-method specialists) who have closed most planar cases except $\Delta=6$. The **List Total Coloring Conjecture** (Borodin–Kostochka–Woodall, 1997) keeps the choosability variant a live research area. Problem compendia by **Jensen and Toft** track the conjecture's status. The community is mid-sized and well-connected; no single result since 1998 has fundamentally altered the landscape, which is why the conjecture remains a prominent open problem in combinatorics.
