**Note on identifiers and verification.** Total coloring is an older subfield whose foundational works (Behzad's 1965 thesis, Vizing's 1964/1968 papers, the 1971 cubic-case papers, Kostochka's $\Delta=4,5$ papers) predate or sit outside reliable DOI assignment, so identifiers are deliberately left as "—" rather than guessed. DOIs are supplied only for the three modern works I can attribute with confidence (Borodin–Kostochka–Woodall 1997 in JCTB; Molloy–Reed 1998 in Combinatorica; Kostochka's 1996 $\Delta=5$ paper in Discrete Mathematics) and even these should be checked against the publisher record. Rows whose author attribution or exact title I am less certain of are flagged **needs-verification**; several "line of work" rows (19, 21, 23) summarize a cluster of papers rather than a single canonical reference and a precise citation should be located before use. No identifiers have been invented. The dominant, certainly-real anchors of the literature are rows 1–4 (founding), 12–14 (the breakthroughs), and 15–16, 24 (the surveys/monographs).
