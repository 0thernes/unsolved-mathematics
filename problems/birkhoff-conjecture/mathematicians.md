# Top Mathematicians — The Birkhoff Conjecture (Integrable Billiards)

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | George D. Birkhoff | 1884–1944 | originator | Systematized convex billiards in *Dynamical Systems* (1927); posed the integrability-rigidity conjecture; proved the Poincaré–Birkhoff twist theorem underlying the dynamics. |
| 2 | Hillel Poritsky | 1898–1990 | originator | 1950 analysis of convex billiards; sharpened the problem into its local "Birkhoff–Poritsky" form, the version most modern theorems address. |
| 3 | Vladimir F. Lazutkin | 1944–2000 | breakthrough | Proved (1973) a positive-measure KAM family of caustics exists near the boundary of any smooth convex billiard; introduced Lazutkin coordinates. |
| 4 | Misha Bialy | contemporary | breakthrough | Proved (1993) global Hopf-type rigidity: full foliation by invariant curves forces the disk; co-leads the polynomial-integrability program. |
| 5 | Andrey E. Mironov | contemporary | breakthrough | With Bialy, resolved the algebraic/polynomial Birkhoff conjecture and advanced the centrally symmetric (Birkhoff–Poritsky) case. |
| 6 | Vadim Kaloshin | contemporary | frontier | Co-author of the local rigidity breakthroughs (2016, 2018); central organizer of the deformation/spectral-rigidity program. |
| 7 | Alfonso Sorrentino | contemporary | frontier | With Kaloshin, proved local Birkhoff rigidity for generic ellipses (2018); developed Mather-theory ($\beta$-function) tools for the problem. |
| 8 | Jacopo De Simoi | contemporary | frontier | Co-author of the small-eccentricity rigidity theorem (2016) and of dynamical spectral rigidity for symmetric domains. |
| 9 | Artur Avila | contemporary | breakthrough | Fields Medalist; co-author of the 2016 rational-integrability rigidity result for low-eccentricity ellipses. |
| 10 | Serge Tabachnikov | contemporary | expositor | Authoritative surveys and the book *Geometry and Billiards*; framed and popularized the modern Birkhoff–Poritsky formulation and caustic geometry. |

## Notes

The active community is compact and tightly interconnected, centered on a few collaboration clusters: the **Kaloshin–Sorrentino–De Simoi** axis (deformation theory, Mather's $\beta$-function, dynamical and Laplace spectral rigidity), the **Bialy–Mironov** program (algebraic and polynomial integrability, central symmetry), and the expository/structural work of **Tabachnikov** that keeps the precise formulations honest. Younger contributors such as **Comlan Koval** and collaborators of Kaloshin (e.g., Hong-Kun Zhang, Ke Zhang in adjacent billiard dynamics; Hamid Hezari and Steve Zelditch on the inverse-spectral side) continue to push local rigidity toward larger eccentricity and weaker regularity. Activity concentrates at the University of Maryland / ETH–IST lineage around Kaloshin, Tel Aviv University (Bialy), Sobolev Institute / Novosibirsk (Mironov), and Roma Tor Vergata (Sorrentino). The problem features regularly at dynamical-systems conferences and in ICM-level surveys; progress is steady and verifiable rather than sporadic.
