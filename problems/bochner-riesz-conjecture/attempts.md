# Attempts — The Bochner–Riesz Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

The Bochner–Riesz problem has a relatively clean record: there is no famous *retracted* claimed proof of the full conjecture, in contrast to some other problems in this Atlas. Progress has come instead through a long chain of genuine partial results, each pushing the admissible $(\delta,p)$ range without closing it for $n\ge3$. The honest summary is that the conjecture remains open in every dimension above two.

## Genuine resolutions and near-misses

- **The plane is done.** Carleson and Sjölin (1972) settled $n=2$ completely. Independent proofs by Fefferman (1973) and Córdoba (1977) followed; Hörmander placed the result inside a general oscillatory-integral framework (the Carleson–Sjölin / Hörmander class). This is a true, uncontested resolution — but only for $n=2$.

- **Stein–Tomas baseline.** The $L^2$ restriction theorem (Tomas 1975, with the endpoint by Stein) gives unconditional Bochner–Riesz boundedness for $\delta > \delta(p)$ on a restricted range — specifically up to the Stein–Tomas exponent — in all dimensions. It is sharp as an $L^2$-based estimate, and beating it requires genuinely new input, which is the whole difficulty.

- **Bourgain's $n=3$ improvements.** Bourgain (1991) broke the "$\delta>1/4$" barrier in three dimensions for the first time using Kakeya-set estimates of measure-theoretic type. This was a celebrated near-miss in the sense that it showed the trivial barriers were not the truth, but it fell well short of $\delta(3,p)$.

## Partial results that define the frontier

- **Bilinear era (1998–2006).** Tao–Vargas–Vega, Wolff, Tao, and Lee progressively improved the higher-dimensional ranges via bilinear restriction. Lee (2004) obtained essentially the best range then available in $\mathbb{R}^3$.

- **Multilinear and polynomial era (2011–2019).** Bourgain–Guth (2011), building on Bennett–Carbery–Tao multilinear restriction, gave the records that stood for years. Guth–Hickman–Iliopoulou (2019) used polynomial partitioning to refine these, currently among the best unconditional ranges for $n\ge3$. None reaches the conjectured endpoint.

## On disputes and caution

Because each advance is incremental and peer-checked within a small, expert community, the field has avoided high-profile false-proof episodes. Periodic preprints claiming the full conjecture (or full restriction) appear and circulate informally; experts generally identify gaps — most often an unjustified passage from a multilinear or local estimate to the sharp linear/global one, or an implicit assumption equivalent to an open Kakeya bound. This Atlas records no community-accepted full proof and no notable formally retracted publication; readers encountering a claimed resolution should check whether it silently assumes the restriction or Kakeya conjecture, the standard locus of error. The conjectured threshold $\delta(p)$ itself is *not* in dispute: necessary-condition constructions (Herz; Christ; Tao) confirm it is sharp.
