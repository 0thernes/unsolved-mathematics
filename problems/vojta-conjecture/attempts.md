# Attempts — Vojta's Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

The conjecture has never been claimed proved in its general form, and no serious withdrawn proof of the full statement exists — a sign of its perceived depth. The notable history is instead one of **major special cases** proved (often by the conjecture's own architects) and of a **disputed proof of a logical consequence** ($abc$).

## Vojta's reproof of Mordell (1991)

Vojta's own application — a wholly new proof of the Mordell conjecture using Diophantine approximation, Roth's lemma in many variables, and Gillet–Soulé arithmetic intersection theory on $C\times C$ — was the first dramatic confirmation that the program could prove a theorem of Faltings' caliber by approximation methods. It is fully accepted. **Bombieri (1990)** then gave a substantially more elementary version avoiding heavy Arakelov machinery, and **Faltings (1991)** generalized it to subvarieties of abelian varieties (the "Big Theorem"), with **Vojta (1994, 1996)** extending to semiabelian varieties and integral points. These are near-misses only in the sense that they prove the conjecture in geometrically special settings; the general-type case remained untouched.

## Subspace-Theorem cases (Ru–Wong, Corvaja–Zannier, Levin, Autissier)

**Ru–Wong (1991)** proved the conjecture for hyperplanes in general position; **Corvaja–Zannier** (from 2002) and **Levin** (from 2009) proved striking integral-point degeneracy results on surfaces and on $\mathbb{G}_m^n$ that are instances or close relatives of the conjecture, including new cases of Vojta's inequality for divisors with several components in general position. **Autissier (2009)** and the **Ru–Vojta (2020)** "General Theorem" consolidated these. All are accepted; all require positivity/general-position hypotheses the full conjecture does not.

## The disputed $abc$ proof — Mochizuki (2012)

Because Vojta's conjecture implies the **abc conjecture**, any proof of $abc$ bears on it. In 2012 **Shinichi Mochizuki** released a four-paper proof of $abc$ via **Inter-universal Teichmüller theory (IUT)**, published in *PRIMS* in 2021. The proof is **not accepted by the broader mathematical community**. In 2018 **Peter Scholze and Jakob Stix** circulated "Why abc is still a conjecture," arguing that the crucial **Corollary 3.12** contains a gap: a step they contend collapses distinct copies of objects in a way that trivializes the inequality, so that as written it cannot yield the claimed bound. Mochizuki published rebuttals maintaining the argument is correct and that the objection misreads the theory. As of the present, the dispute is unresolved within the community at large, $abc$ is regarded as open, and consequently no implication for Vojta's conjecture can be drawn from it. The Atlas records this neutrally: there is a published proof and a detailed, widely shared objection; consensus has not formed.

## What no attempt has reached

Every accepted result attacks either (i) divisors in general position (Subspace Theorem) or (ii) varieties with group structure (abelian/semiabelian). The **canonical-class term $h_{K_X}$ for a general variety of general type with small or no boundary** — the part that would yield Bombieri–Lang and the deep content of $abc$ unconditionally and effectively — has resisted all methods. There is no known reduction of the analytic Second Main Theorem (proved in many cases) to its arithmetic mirror, and the absence of an arithmetic analogue of negative curvature / the Ahlfors–Schwarz lemma is the standing obstruction. The conjecture's tractability is rated very low for exactly this reason.
