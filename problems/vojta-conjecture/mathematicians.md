# Top Mathematicians — Vojta's Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Paul Vojta | 1987–present | originator | Formulated the conjecture and the Nevanlinna–Diophantine dictionary; reproved Mordell (1991); semiabelian integral-points theorems (1994, 1996). |
| 2 | Charles F. Osgood | 1980s | originator | Observed the numerical coincidence between Roth's exponent and Nevanlinna's defect relation that seeded the analogy. |
| 3 | Rolf Nevanlinna | 1920s | originator | Second Main Theorem of value-distribution theory — the analytic template the conjecture mirrors. |
| 4 | Klaus F. Roth | 1955 | originator | Sharp Diophantine approximation exponent $2$, the arithmetic side of the analogy. |
| 5 | Gerd Faltings | 1983–1994 | breakthrough | Proved Mordell (1983); Diophantine approximation on abelian varieties and the Mordell–Lang "Big Theorem" — central cases in the conjecture's orbit. |
| 6 | Enrico Bombieri | 1990, 2006 | breakthrough | Elementary reproof of Mordell via the Vojta method; co-author of *Heights in Diophantine Geometry*. |
| 7 | Min Ru | 1991–present | frontier | Subspace-Theorem second main theorems; with Wong, the general-position case; with Vojta, the 2020 General Theorem. |
| 8 | Pietro Corvaja & Umberto Zannier | 2000s | frontier | Subspace-Theorem method for integral points on curves and surfaces; many new degeneracy cases. |
| 9 | Aaron Levin | 2009–present | frontier | Generalized Siegel/Picard theorems; new cases of the Vojta inequality for divisors in general position. |
| 10 | Joseph H. Silverman | 2000s | expositor | With Hindry, the standard textbook *Diophantine Geometry: An Introduction* presenting Vojta's program. |

## Notes

The active community sits at the intersection of arithmetic geometry, Diophantine approximation, and Nevanlinna theory. Core figures include **Paul Vojta** (Berkeley) and his collaborators; the **Subspace-Theorem school** of **Corvaja, Zannier** (and students such as **Capuano, Turchet**), **Levin** (Michigan State), **Autissier**, and **Jan-Hendrik Evertse / Roberto Ferretti** on quantitative subspace estimates; the **Nevanlinna-theory side** of **Min Ru** (Houston), **Junjiro Noguchi**, **Jörg Winkelmann**, **Katsutoshi Yamanoi**, and **Carlo Gasbarri**, who pursue the analytic mirror and arithmetic transfers. Related uniform-bounds progress by **Vesselin Dimitrov, Ziyang Gao, and Philipp Habegger** (uniform Mordell, 2021) and **Lars Kühne** keeps the conjecture's quantitative consequences in active development. The $abc$-via-IUT debate involving **Shinichi Mochizuki**, **Peter Scholze**, and **Jakob Stix** remains the most visible — and unresolved — controversy adjacent to the conjecture's consequences.
