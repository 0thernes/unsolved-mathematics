# Top Mathematicians — Köthe's Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present. Real, well-documented mathematicians only._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Gottfried Köthe | 1930s | originator | Posed the conjecture (1930): a ring with no nonzero nil two-sided ideal has no nonzero nil one-sided ideal. |
| 2 | Jacob Levitzki | 1940s–50s | breakthrough | Proved nil one-sided ideals are nilpotent under chain conditions; introduced the Levitzki (locally nilpotent) radical, settling the Noetherian case. |
| 3 | Shimshon A. Amitsur | 1950s–60s | breakthrough | Radicals of polynomial rings; $J(R[x])=N[x]$ for nil $N$; the Amitsur–Levitzki theorem and the polynomial reformulation of the conjecture. |
| 4 | Evgeny Golod | 1960s | breakthrough | With Shafarevich, constructed infinite-dimensional nil non-nilpotent algebras (Golod–Shafarevich), the first "wild" nil rings. |
| 5 | Igor Shafarevich | 1960s | breakthrough | Co-author of the Golod–Shafarevich construction underpinning all counterexample attempts to nil-ring conjectures. |
| 6 | Jan Krempa | 1970s | breakthrough | Proved the key equivalences: Köthe $\iff$ $M_2(R)$ nil for nil $R$ $\iff$ $R[x]$ Jacobson radical for nil $R$. |
| 7 | Edmund R. Puczyłowski | 1980s–present | frontier | Extensive work on radicals of rings and reformulations of the Köthe problem; co-author with Smoktunowicz; influential surveys. |
| 8 | Agata Smoktunowicz | 2000s–present | frontier | Constructed a simple nil ring and a nil ring $R$ with $R[x]$ not nil; authored the standard survey on results related to Köthe's conjecture. |
| 9 | Tsit-Yuen Lam | 1990s–present | expositor | *A First Course in Noncommutative Rings* gives the canonical textbook treatment of nil radicals and Köthe's conjecture. |
| 10 | Louis H. Rowen | 1980s–present | expositor | Ring-theory monographs and PI-theory exposition framing nil-radical questions and the PI resolution of the conjecture. |

## Notes

The active community around Köthe's conjecture is small and concentrated in radical theory and noncommutative ring theory, with the Warsaw/Polish school (Puczyłowski, Smoktunowicz, and collaborators) the most visible contemporary center; Smoktunowicz's Edinburgh-based work on nil rings, growth of algebras, and the Köthe problem has set the modern agenda. Adjacent communities in PI-theory (descendants of the Amitsur–Levitzki and Kaplansky tradition) and in infinite-dimensional algebra (Golod–Shafarevich heirs, including those studying just-infinite and self-similar algebras) supply tools and counterexample constructions. Progress is steady but incremental: the field's honest consensus is that resolving the conjecture awaits a genuinely new method for controlling nil index in rings without chain conditions, and most current papers refine the matrix/polynomial reformulations or map out exactly which strengthenings are false.
