# Top Mathematicians — Hall's Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Marshall Hall Jr. | 1971 | originator | Stated the conjecture $|x^3-y^2| \gg \sqrt{x}$ from computational study of $y^2 = x^3 + k$. |
| 2 | Harold Davenport | 1965 | breakthrough | Proved the function-field analogue $\deg(f^3-g^2)\geq \tfrac12\deg f + 1$ with sharp constant; co-author of the small-gap constructions. |
| 3 | Andrzej Schinzel | 1965 | breakthrough | Co-author (with Davenport, Lewis) of parametric families forcing $|x^3-y^2|\asymp\sqrt{x}$, establishing sharpness of the exponent. |
| 4 | L. V. Danilov | 1982 | breakthrough | Explicit infinite family with small Hall ratio, constraining (and casting doubt on) the strong-form constant. |
| 5 | Alan Baker | 1968 | frontier | Linear forms in logarithms giving effective bounds for $y^2 = x^3 + k$ per fixed $k$. |
| 6 | Joseph Oesterlé | 1985 | frontier | Co-formulated the $abc$ conjecture, from which weak Hall follows. |
| 7 | David Masser | 1985 | frontier | Co-formulated the $abc$ conjecture and the surrounding height framework. |
| 8 | Paul Vojta | 1987 | frontier | Conjectural height inequalities placing weak Hall inside the Bombieri–Lang–Vojta program. |
| 9 | Noam D. Elkies | 1998 | frontier | Lattice-reduction search producing record small-ratio examples (e.g. $r\approx 0.0211$). |
| 10 | Michel Waldschmidt | 2000s | expositor | Surveys of open Diophantine problems situating Hall among $abc$-type questions. |

## Notes

The active community around Hall's conjecture is small and sits at the confluence of three larger fields rather than forming a school of its own. The **$abc$/Vojta side** (Oesterlé, Masser, Vojta, and expositors such as Waldschmidt, Granville, and Nitaj) treats weak Hall as one of many corollaries of the central height conjectures, so progress there is entirely tied to progress on $abc$ — currently stalled around the disputed Mochizuki manuscripts and the Scholze–Stix objection. The **computational/Diophantine-search side** (Elkies, Gebel–Pethő–Zimmer, Jiménez Calvo and collaborators, and the integral-points-on-elliptic-curves community of Stroeker–Tzanakis) maintains and extends tables of record Hall ratios, supplying the empirical data that constrains the strong constant. The **function-field/$abc$-analogue side** (descended from Davenport, Mason, Stothers, and continued in arithmetic-of-function-fields work) holds the one fully proved version and keeps the structural picture honest. There is no large conference circuit or prize specific to Hall; it lives on as a benchmark example in courses and surveys on the $abc$ conjecture, heights, and integral points.
