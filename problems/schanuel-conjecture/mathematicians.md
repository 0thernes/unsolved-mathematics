# Top Mathematicians — Schanuel's Conjecture

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Stephen Schanuel | 20th–21st c. (1933–2014) | originator | Formulated the conjecture (~1960), unifying classical transcendence theory under a single trdeg inequality. |
| 2 | Serge Lang | 20th c. (1927–2005) | originator | Named, published (1966), and propagated the conjecture; framed its consequences for transcendence theory. |
| 3 | Charles Hermite | 19th c. (1822–1901) | breakthrough | Transcendence of $e$ (1873) — the first special case the conjecture generalizes. |
| 4 | Ferdinand von Lindemann | 19th–20th c. (1852–1939) | breakthrough | Transcendence of $\pi$ (1882); with Weierstrass, the Lindemann–Weierstrass theorem (algebraic case of Schanuel). |
| 5 | Alexander Gelfond | 20th c. (1906–1968) | breakthrough | Gelfond–Schneider theorem (1934); founded algebraic-independence methods central to the conjecture's special cases. |
| 6 | James Ax | 20th–21st c. (1937–2006) | breakthrough | Proved the differential/formal analogue (Ax–Schanuel, 1971), the strongest theoretical evidence for the conjecture. |
| 7 | Yuri Nesterenko | 20th–21st c. | breakthrough | Algebraic independence of $\pi, e^\pi, \Gamma(1/4)$ (1996) via modular functions — deepest trdeg result near Schanuel. |
| 8 | Gisbert Wüstholz | 20th–21st c. | breakthrough | Analytic subgroup theorem (1989); strongest unconditional linear transcendence results on algebraic groups. |
| 9 | Boris Zilber | 20th–21st c. | frontier | Pseudo-exponentiation and categoricity (2005), recasting Schanuel as a model-theoretic predimension axiom. |
| 10 | Jonathan Kirby | 21st c. | frontier | Model theory of exponential fields; exponential-algebraic closedness reducing Zilber's conjecture to Schanuel. |

## Notes

The current active community spans two cultures that have been steadily converging. On the **transcendence-theory** side, the analytic tradition of Baker, Waldschmidt, Philippon, Nesterenko, Brownawell, and Masser continues to push algebraic-independence results and effective bounds, and remains the only source of unconditional new cases. On the **model-theory and o-minimality** side, a vigorous group — Boris Zilber, Jonathan Kirby, Martin Bays, Vahagn Aslanyan, Angus Macintyre, Alex Wilkie (1948–2024), and younger researchers such as Vincenzo Mantova — has reframed the conjecture inside the theory of exponential fields and Hrushovski constructions, and has essentially proved the *closedness* half of Zilber's program, isolating Schanuel itself as the lone arithmetic obstruction. The functional-transcendence wing (Jonathan Pila, Jacob Tsimerman, Ngaiming Mok, and collaborators) has proved the Ax–Schanuel statement in ever broader geometric settings, feeding the André–Oort circle of results. The bare arithmetic conjecture, however, has no current attacker with a credible route to a full proof; the field's consensus is that a fundamentally new idea is required to cross the functional-to-arithmetic divide.
