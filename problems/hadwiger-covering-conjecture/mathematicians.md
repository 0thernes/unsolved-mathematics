# Top Mathematicians — The Hadwiger Covering Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Hugo Hadwiger | 1908–1981 | originator | Posed the covering conjecture ($c(K)\le 2^n$, parallelepipeds extremal) within his combinatorial geometry program; foundational figure of integral/convex geometry. |
| 2 | Friedrich (Frigyes) Levi | 1888–1966 | originator | Settled the planar case ($c(K)\le 4$, equality only for parallelograms) via boundary-illumination, seeding the illumination viewpoint. |
| 3 | Israel Gohberg | 1928–2009 | originator | With Markus, independently stated the covering-by-homothets form (1960), giving the "Gohberg–Markus" name. |
| 4 | Alexander Markus | 20th c. | originator | Co-author of the 1960 Gohberg–Markus formulation of the covering conjecture. |
| 5 | Vladimir Boltyanski | 1925–2019 | breakthrough | Defined the illumination number and proved $I(K)=c(K)$ (1960); developed belt-body theory; co-authored the standard monograph. |
| 6 | Martin Lassak | 20th–21st c. | breakthrough | Proved the conjecture for centrally symmetric bodies in $\mathbb{R}^3$ ($c\le 8$) and sharp planar covering-ratio results. |
| 7 | Horst Martini | 20th–21st c. | frontier | Co-developed belt-body and combinatorial-geometry illumination theory; co-author of the canonical survey monograph. |
| 8 | Károly Bezdek | 21st c. | frontier | Verified large classes; co-authored the definitive modern survey "The geometry of homothetic covering and illumination." |
| 9 | Márton Naszódi | 21st c. | frontier | Pioneered fractional illumination and concentration-of-measure covering bounds; active surveyor of the problem. |
| 10 | Beatrice-Helen Vritsiou | 21st c. | frontier | Co-author of the 2021 thin-shell improvement to the general covering bound, the best recent asymptotic progress. |

## Notes

The active community sits at the meeting point of **discrete geometry**, **convex geometry**, and the **asymptotic/local theory of normed spaces**. The classical lineage runs Hadwiger–Levi–Boltyanski through the Eastern European school (Gohberg, Markus, Soltan) and the belt-body program of Boltyanski and Martini. The modern frontier is led by two overlapping groups: a *combinatorial/low-dimensional* wing (Bezdek and collaborators, who push the $\mathbb{R}^3$ and $\mathbb{R}^4$ cases and special classes) and an *asymptotic-geometric-analysis* wing (Naszódi, Artstein-Avidan, Slomka, Tkocz, Vritsiou, Huang) that imports thin-shell, concentration, and fractional-relaxation tools to attack the general upper bound. Károly Böröczky, Apostolos Giannopoulos, and the broader convex-geometry community supply adjacent machinery (covering numbers, volume estimates). Conferences in discrete and convex geometry keep the problem prominent, and the Bezdek–Khan and Naszódi surveys serve as the field's shared map. Despite this activity, the headline targets — the general $2^n$ bound, the full $n=3$ case, and the equality characterization — remain open.
