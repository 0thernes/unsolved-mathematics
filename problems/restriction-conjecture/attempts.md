# Attempts — The Restriction Conjecture

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

The restriction conjecture has, unusually, attracted little in the way of erroneous "claimed proofs" — its difficulty is so widely understood, and progress so incremental and refereed, that the literature is a record of honest partial advances rather than disputed resolutions. The story is one of successive sharp partial results, each setting a new exponent record in $\mathbb{R}^n$.

## The two-dimensional case (resolved)

In $n=2$ the conjecture is a **theorem**. It follows from the Carleson–Sjölin theorem (1972) on convergence of Fourier series and from Zygmund's and Fefferman–Stein's $L^4$ arguments for the circle. The planar extension operator maps $L^\infty(S^1)\to L^q(\mathbb{R}^2)$ for all $q>4$, with $q=4$ a clean $L^2$ computation. This is the only complete case and serves as the proof of concept; all difficulty lies in $n\ge 3$.

## Exponent records in $\mathbb{R}^3$ (near-misses to the endpoint)

The conjectural threshold in $\mathbb{R}^3$ is $p>3$ (i.e. $q>3$ for the extension operator). The sequence of records illustrates how close — and how stubbornly far — the frontier is:

- **Stein–Tomas (1975):** $p\ge\tfrac{2(n+1)}{n-1}=4$.
- **Bourgain (1991):** first improvement below $4$, opening the Kakeya route.
- **Tao–Vargas–Vega / Tao (1998–2003):** bilinear methods give $p>\tfrac{10}{3}\approx 3.33$.
- **Bourgain–Guth (2011):** broad–narrow analysis, modest further gains.
- **Guth (2016):** polynomial partitioning gives $p>3.25$.
- **Subsequent refinements** (Guth 2018; Wang; Hickman–Rogers) hold the record near $p>3.25$ but have **not** reached the conjectural $p>3$.

Each is a genuine theorem; none is disputed. The gap from $3.25$ down to $3$ is the open near-miss.

## Higher dimensions

In $n\ge 4$ the analogous records (Stein–Tomas baseline, Bourgain–Guth, Hickman–Rogers polynomial-partitioning estimates) remain strictly inside the conjectural range. No dimension $n\ge 3$ is closed.

## Adjacent claims and their reception

- **Kakeya in $\mathbb{R}^3$:** the long-pursued three-dimensional Kakeya *set* conjecture was announced by **Hong Wang and Joshua Zahl** and posted in 2025. This is widely regarded as a landmark, currently under careful community scrutiny; it is an adjacent (necessary) result, **not** a proof of restriction. One should be careful: resolving Kakeya does not automatically resolve restriction, since the implication runs restriction $\Rightarrow$ Kakeya, not the reverse.
- **Decoupling (Bourgain–Demeter, 2015–2017):** correct, celebrated, and sharp — but, as noted, its sharp range stops at the Stein–Tomas exponent and does not by itself yield the restriction endpoint. It is sometimes informally over-credited; the precise statement is uncontested.

## Summary

There are no notable retracted or formally disputed proofs of the full conjecture in $n\ge 3$. The honest state of affairs: the problem is solved in the plane, approached to within a definite exponent gap in $\mathbb{R}^3$, and open in every higher dimension, with the community treating any claim of full resolution with appropriate caution.
