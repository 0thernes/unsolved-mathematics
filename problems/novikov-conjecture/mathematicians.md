# Top Mathematicians — The Novikov Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|-----------------|
| 1 | Sergei P. Novikov | 1960s–present | originator | Posed the conjecture (1965); proved topological invariance of rational Pontryagin classes, the result from which it grew. Fields Medal 1970. |
| 2 | Alexander S. Mishchenko | 1970s–present | breakthrough | Introduced the $C^*$-algebraic symmetric signature and Fredholm representations; proved early cases and built the index-theoretic reformulation. |
| 3 | Gennadi G. Kasparov | 1970s–present | breakthrough | Created $KK$-theory and the Dirac–dual-Dirac method; proved Novikov for discrete subgroups of Lie groups and bolic/hyperbolic-type groups. |
| 4 | George Lusztig | 1970s | breakthrough | First substantial case: proved the conjecture for $\mathbb{Z}^n$ via families of elliptic operators (1972). |
| 5 | Alain Connes | 1980s–present | breakthrough | With Gromov and Moscovici, used cyclic cohomology and Lipschitz/bounded cohomology to prove Novikov for hyperbolic groups. |
| 6 | Henri Moscovici | 1980s–present | breakthrough | Co-developed the higher index theorem and cyclic-cohomology pairing that establishes homotopy invariance of higher signatures. |
| 7 | Guoliang Yu | 1990s–present | frontier | Proved Novikov for groups of finite asymptotic dimension and for groups coarsely embeddable into Hilbert space — the broadest unconditional results. |
| 8 | Andrew Ranicki | 1980s–2018 | expositor | Algebraic $L$-theory, the assembly-map formulation, and co-editor of the definitive *Novikov Conjectures* survey volumes. |
| 9 | Jonathan Rosenberg | 1980s–present | expositor | Co-editor and surveyor of the field; clarified the $C^*$-algebraic and $K$-theoretic landscape linking Novikov to Baum–Connes. |
| 10 | Arthur Bartels & Wolfgang Lück | 2000s–present | frontier | Proved the Farrell–Jones Conjecture (which implies Novikov) for hyperbolic, CAT(0), and many further classes via controlled topology and flow dynamics. |

## Notes

The active community spans three overlapping camps. The **operator-algebra / coarse-geometry** wing (Yu, Roe's school, Higson, Skandalis, Lafforgue, Willett, Guentner) works on the Baum–Connes side, refining coarse embeddings, property (T)/expander obstructions, and controlled $K$-theory. The **controlled-topology / Farrell–Jones** wing (Bartels, Lück, Reich, Kasprowski, Rüping, Wegner) attacks the stronger isomorphism conjecture group-class by group-class, each result yielding Novikov as a corollary. The **surgery / $L$-theory** wing (in the tradition of Ranicki, Weinberger, Ferry, Carlsson) maintains the manifold-theoretic and assembly-map perspective. Cross-pollination is intense: index theory, large-scale geometry of groups, and high-dimensional manifold topology now form a single ecosystem, and the conjecture functions as a shared organizing target rather than the province of any one subfield.
