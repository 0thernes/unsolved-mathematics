# Top Mathematicians — The KPZ Universality Conjecture

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Mehran Kardar | 1986– | originator | Co-author of the KPZ equation; the lateral-growth nonlinearity and the $1/3$–$2/3$ exponent prediction. |
| 2 | Giorgio Parisi | 1986– | originator | Co-author; brought disordered-systems and RG intuition; Nobel laureate (2021). |
| 3 | Herbert Spohn | 1990s– | breakthrough | Krug–Spohn scaling theory, the Airy process (with Prähofer), and nonlinear fluctuating hydrodynamics extending the class. |
| 4 | Kurt Johansson | 1999– | breakthrough | Shape fluctuations of last-passage percolation; Tracy–Widom limits for corner growth; the Airy process geometry. |
| 5 | Craig Tracy | 1994– | breakthrough | The Tracy–Widom distributions $F_1,F_2$; exact ASEP fluctuation formulas (with Widom). |
| 6 | Harold Widom | 1994– | breakthrough | Co-discoverer of the Tracy–Widom laws and the ASEP asymptotics. |
| 7 | Jinho Baik | 1999– | breakthrough | Baik–Deift–Johansson theorem; deep Riemann–Hilbert asymptotics for the longest increasing subsequence. |
| 8 | Ivan Corwin | 2010– | frontier | KPZ equation one-point law (with Amir, Quastel); Macdonald processes (with Borodin); the field's leading synthesizer and expositor. |
| 9 | Jeremy Quastel | 2010– | frontier | KPZ one-point law; construction of the KPZ fixed point (with Matetski, Remenik); convergence theorems. |
| 10 | Martin Hairer | 2013– | breakthrough | Theory of regularity structures giving a rigorous KPZ solution theory; Fields Medal 2014. |

## Notes

The KPZ community is one of the most active and tightly knit in contemporary probability, straddling integrable systems, random matrix theory, and stochastic analysis. Beyond the ten above, central present-day contributors include **Alexei Borodin** and **Leonid Petrov** (integrable probability, Macdonald and stochastic vertex models), **Bálint Virág** and **Duncan Dauvergne** (the directed landscape and its geometry), **Konstantin Matetski** and **Daniel Remenik** (KPZ fixed point), **Sourav Chatterjee** and **Timo Seppäläinen** (probabilistic / geodesic methods and exponent bounds for less-solvable models), **Patrik Ferrari** (Airy processes, time correlations), **Tomohiro Sasamoto** and **Takashi Imamura** (physics-side exact solutions), **Massimiliano Gubinelli** and **Nicolas Perkowski** (paracontrolled / energy solutions), and **Promit Ghosal**, **Shirshendu Ganguly**, **Riddhipratim Basu**, **Alan Hammond**, and **Jacopo Borga** among a vigorous younger generation. The yearly flow of results — convergence theorems, time-correlation laws, geodesic structure, and crossover phenomena — has made KPZ a flagship for the "integrable probability" program, while the non-integrable universality question keeps drawing in tools from RG, dynamical systems, and stochastic PDEs. Activity is global, with strong groups in the US (Columbia, MIT, Berkeley, Stanford), Europe (Bonn, TU Munich, ETH/Imperial via Hairer's school), Japan, and Canada (Toronto).
