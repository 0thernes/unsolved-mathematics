# Attempts — The Palis Conjecture (Finitude / Density of Attractors)

_Notable attempts, near-misses, retracted proofs._

The Palis Conjecture has not attracted "claimed full proofs" in the manner of headline problems like the Riemann Hypothesis; its status as a *program* rather than a single equation means progress is measured in secured sub-cases and in obstructions that reshape the target. The notable record is one of partial victories and of negative results that recalibrate expectations.

## Secured sub-case: one-dimensional density of hyperbolicity

The cleanest near-resolution is in dimension one. Kozlovski, Shen, and van Strien (2007) proved that hyperbolic maps are **dense in the space of $C^k$ interval and circle maps** ($1 \le k \le \infty, \omega$), confirming Smale's one-dimensional conjecture and, with it, the one-dimensional shadow of the Palis program. Combined with Lyubich's "regular-or-stochastic" dichotomy and the Avila–Lyubich–de Melo–Martens metric-universality results for unimodal families, the *quadratic family* is now known to satisfy the full Palis picture for almost every parameter: almost every quadratic map is either regular (a hyperbolic attracting cycle) or stochastic (an SRB/acip), with no infinitely-many-attractor pathology. This is a genuine, complete verification — but only in a regime where renormalization rigidity is available.

## Near-misses in higher dimension

In dimension $\ge 2$ the strongest results stop short of the conjecture along a predictable seam:

- **Pujals–Sambarino** and **Crovisier–Pujals** obtained essential hyperbolicity and the hyperbolicity-vs-bifurcation dichotomy, but only in the **$C^1$ topology**, where physical (SRB) measures need not exist — so the "metric covering" and "statistical stability" clauses are not delivered.
- **Bonatti–Díaz–Viana** built SRB measures and proved finitude for many **partially hyperbolic** classes, but under structural hypotheses (mostly-contracting or mostly-expanding center) that exclude the general neutral-center case.
- **Benedicks–Carleson / Mora–Viana** secured positive-measure parameter sets with SRB attractors for Hénon-like families, a measure-theoretic confirmation in a single family that does not globalize.

None of these is a retracted or disputed proof; each is an honest partial theorem whose hypotheses mark exactly where the program is stuck.

## Obstructions that reshaped the problem

Two strands of negative results function as the conjecture's hard constraints, and have prevented several plausible-looking strategies:

- **Newhouse phenomenon (1974, and Palis–Viana / Romero in higher dimension):** open sets with infinitely many coexisting sinks. This permanently rules out any *topological*-density formulation of finitude and forces the measure-zero qualifier into the statement.
- **Berger's robust universality (2016, *Inventiones*):** Pierre Berger proved that in regularity $C^r$, $r \ge 2$ (and analytic), there are **open sets of dynamics exhibiting persistent Newhouse phenomena and "universal" behavior**, with attractors of unbounded complexity that survive perturbation. These results are widely read as a *caution* that even the measure-theoretic finitude clause may be in genuine jeopardy in high regularity — a serious, unresolved tension rather than a counterexample to the conjecture as Palis stated it (which concerns full-measure parameter sets in generic families). Berger's work is not disputed; rather, it sharpens what a correct statement and proof must accommodate.

The overall picture, stated honestly: the conjecture is **confirmed in dimension one**, supported by partial $C^1$ and partially-hyperbolic results in higher dimension, and under live pressure from high-regularity universality phenomena that may force refinement of the precise hypotheses. There is no accepted proof, and no widely circulated erroneous one.
