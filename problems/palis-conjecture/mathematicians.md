# Top Mathematicians — The Palis Conjecture (Finitude / Density of Attractors)

_Ten figures who shaped the problem, past and present._

| # | Name | Era | Role | Key contribution |
|---|------|-----|------|------------------|
| 1 | Jacob Palis | 1960s–present | originator | Posed the global conjecture (finitude, metric covering, statistical stability); built the homoclinic-bifurcation theory and the IMPA school behind the program. |
| 2 | Stephen Smale | 1960s–1970s | originator | Founded hyperbolic dynamics (Axiom A, the horseshoe), and posed the density-of-hyperbolicity question whose failure motivates the conjecture. |
| 3 | Sheldon Newhouse | 1970s–present | frontier | Discovered persistent homoclinic tangencies producing infinitely many sinks — the obstruction forcing the measure-zero formulation. |
| 4 | Ricardo Mañé | 1980s–1990s | breakthrough | Ergodic closing lemma and proof of the $C^1$ stability conjecture; pioneered the $C^1$-generic methods central to the program. |
| 5 | Floris Takens | 1970s–2000s | breakthrough | Co-developed homoclinic-bifurcation and fractal-dimension theory with Palis (Palis–Takens). |
| 6 | Marcelo Viana | 1990s–present | frontier | SRB measures for partially hyperbolic systems, abundance of strange attractors (with Mora), and the modern non-uniform hyperbolicity toolkit. |
| 7 | Christian Bonatti | 1990s–present | frontier | Robust transitivity, dominated splittings, and (with Díaz, Viana) the framework "Dynamics Beyond Uniform Hyperbolicity." |
| 8 | Enrique Pujals | 2000s–present | frontier | Pujals–Sambarino and Crovisier–Pujals dichotomies establishing essential hyperbolicity away from homoclinic bifurcations. |
| 9 | Sebastian van Strien | 1990s–present | breakthrough | With Kozlovski and Shen, proved density of hyperbolicity for one-dimensional maps — the strongest confirmation of the program. |
| 10 | Mikhail Lyubich | 1990s–present | breakthrough | "Regular or stochastic" dichotomy for the quadratic family, realizing the Palis picture in one-dimensional dynamics. |

## Notes

The community is unusually cohesive and largely organized around the **IMPA school** in Rio de Janeiro founded by Palis, together with strong groups in France (Crovisier, Bonatti, Berger and the Orsay/Dijon/Paris dynamics communities), the United States (Newhouse, Lyubich's Stony Brook), the United Kingdom (van Strien, Shen), and Brazil more broadly (Viana, Carlos Gustavo Moreira, Alves in Porto). Active contributors not in the table above include **Lorenzo J. Díaz** (heterodimensional cycles), **Sylvain Crovisier** and **Pierre Berger** (current frontier on dichotomies and robust universality), **José F. Alves** (non-uniform expansion and SRB measures), **Lan Wen**, **Shaobo Gan**, and **Dawei Yang** (Chinese school on $C^1$ dynamics), **Artur Avila** (renormalization and the one-dimensional metric theory), and **Warwick Tucker** (rigorous Lorenz). The field meets regularly through IMPA programs and the dynamics community's conferences, and treats the Palis program as the organizing horizon for work on attractors, SRB measures, and generic dynamics.
