# History — The Palis Conjecture (Finitude / Density of Attractors)

_Origin, formulation, and timeline._

## Origin and intellectual context

The Palis Conjecture crystallizes a programmatic vision that Jacob Palis developed over three decades of work on smooth dynamics. Its roots lie in the collapse of the 1960s dream of "structural stability everywhere." Stephen Smale's early hope — that hyperbolic (Axiom A) systems would be dense among all diffeomorphisms — was killed by Smale's own horseshoe-driven counterexamples and by the Newhouse phenomenon: open sets of surface diffeomorphisms with infinitely many coexisting sinks (homoclinic tangencies generating persistent non-hyperbolicity). By the late 1980s it was clear that the space of all dynamics is irreducibly wild on a topologically generic ($G_\delta$) set. Palis's response was a deliberate retreat from topological genericity to a *probabilistic* notion of "typical": properties that hold for *almost every* parameter, i.e. on a set of full Lebesgue measure in generic finite-parameter families, even where they fail on a residual set.

## Precise formulation

The conjecture asserts that for a dense set of dynamical systems (diffeomorphisms or flows on a compact manifold), and for Lebesgue-almost-every system in any generic parametrized family through them, the system has **finitely many attractors**, the union of whose **basins has full Lebesgue measure** in phase space, and each attractor supports a **physical (SRB) measure that varies continuously / is statistically stable** under typical perturbations. Equivalently: finitude of attractors, metric (full-measure) covering of phase space by their basins, and statistical stability of the asymptotic behavior of almost all orbits. A central structural sub-conjecture is the **density of hyperbolicity / homoclinic bifurcations**: every system can be approximated either by a hyperbolic one or by one exhibiting a homoclinic tangency or a heterodimensional cycle — making these bifurcations the universal mechanism of complexity.

## Timeline

**1965–1970** — Smale's Axiom A theory and the realization (Abraham–Smale) that structural stability is not dense; hyperbolic paradigm bounded.

**1974** — Newhouse constructs open sets of $C^2$ surface diffeomorphisms with infinitely many sinks, exposing the obstruction the conjecture must dodge by going to full measure.

**1987–1993** — Palis and Takens develop the theory of homoclinic bifurcations and fractal dimensions; the book *Hyperbolicity and Sensitive Chaotic Dynamics at Homoclinic Bifurcations* (1993) frames tangencies as the gateway to complexity.

**1995** — Palis articulates the **global conjecture** explicitly in lectures and writings, proposing finitude and metric stability of attractors as the generic norm; refined in his 2000 "A global view of dynamics and a conjecture on the denseness of finitude of attractors" (*Astérisque* 261).

**2000** — Palis publishes the canonical statement; the "Palis program/conjecture" enters the literature as a guiding open problem.

**2000–2008** — Density-of-hyperbolicity in dimension one (the simplest case): Kozlovski, Shen, van Strien prove density of hyperbolicity for $C^k$ real one-dimensional maps (interval/circle), settling Smale's one-dimensional version — strong evidence for the program in low dimension.

**2003–2009** — Bonatti–Díaz–Viana synthesize the field in *Dynamics Beyond Uniform Hyperbolicity* (2005); robust transitivity, dominated splittings, and partial hyperbolicity become the working tools for the conjecture.

**2007–2012** — Crovisier, Pujals, Sambarino and collaborators advance the $C^1$ generic picture: essential hyperbolicity, the dichotomy hyperbolicity-vs-tangencies/cycles in higher dimension via Mañé–Pujals–Sambarino arguments.

**2016–2024** — Crovisier–Pujals ("essential hyperbolicity and homoclinic bifurcations") and Berger's work on robust universality / Newhouse phenomena in higher regularity sharpen both the positive and negative frontiers; the full conjecture remains open in every dimension $\geq 2$, with only fragments (one-dimensional density of hyperbolicity, partial $C^1$ results) secured.
