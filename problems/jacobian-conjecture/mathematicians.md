## Notes

The active research community around the Jacobian Conjecture is small but persistent and international. The Dutch school centered on **Arno van den Essen** (Radboud University) and his collaborators — including **Michiel de Bondt** — has produced much of the modern structural theory, including reductions to symmetric and nilpotent-Jacobian cases. **Wenhua Zhao** (Illinois State University) anchors the differential-operator and Mathieu–Zhao-space program, which has spawned its own subfield connecting the conjecture to noncommutative algebra. The **Belov-Kanel–Kontsevich** bridge to the Dixmier conjecture has drawn in researchers from noncommutative geometry, quantization, and operad theory. Alongside these are contributors working on the polynomial-automorphism side — the tame/wild theory advanced by **Ivan Shestakov** and **Ualbai Umirbaev** — and a steady stream of low-dimension and computational verifications. The field is also marked by vigilance: because erroneous general "proofs" appear regularly, the community places a premium on careful refereeing and on the explicit obstruction-tracking pioneered in the 1982 survey.
