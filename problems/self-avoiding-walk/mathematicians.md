## Notes

The SAW community sits at the intersection of probability theory, statistical mechanics, and combinatorics, and it remains small but intensely active. The contemporary frontier is led by **Hugo Duminil-Copin** and collaborators — including **Stanislav Smirnov**, **Alexander Glazman**, **Alan Hammond**, and **Ioan Manolescu** — who pursue exact lattice results and conformal invariance through discrete holomorphicity and parafermionic observables. **Gordon Slade**, with **Roland Bauerschmidt** and the late **David Brydges**, drives the rigorous renormalization-group program that settled the four-dimensional logarithmic corrections for the weakly self-avoiding model. On the computational and enumerative side, **Nathan Clisby**, **Iwan Jensen**, and **Anthony Guttmann** (Melbourne) produce the high-precision exponent and connective-constant estimates that anchor every conjecture. The SLE perspective continues through the broader conformal-probability school descended from **Schramm**, **Werner**, and **Lawler**. Major venues include *Annals of Probability*, *Communications in Mathematical Physics*, *Journal of Statistical Physics*, and *Probability Theory and Related Fields*; the field's shared text remains Madras–Slade's *The Self-Avoiding Walk* (1993), supplemented by Slade's renormalization-group lectures. The defining open challenge — a rigorous proof of conformal invariance and the exact 2D exponents — keeps the community tightly focused.
